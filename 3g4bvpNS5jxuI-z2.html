The Global Angel Wings Project In 2012, American visual artist Colette Miller launched an artistic creation called "The Global Angel Wings Project," starting from Los Angeles. Across dozens of cities worldwide, Miller used her brush to paint pairs of life-sized colorful wings onto walls that were originally monotonous and cold. Each pair of wings featured similar colors but different poses: some spread out powerfully, as if they might take off at any moment; others hung naturally downward, resembling angels descended to earth. When the first colorful "angel wall" appeared in Los Angeles' downtown art district, it quickly attracted many celebrities and social media influencers who came to check it out and take photos. It rapidly gained widespread attention online. To this day, not only can countless followers of the "angel walls" be found in Hollywood and Beverly Hills in America, but also on streets across the ocean in Australia and Japan. Footprints of the "Angel Wall" Around the Globe Asia: - Japan (Yokohama) - Taiwan (Taipei, Taichung) Europe: - Turkey (Izmir) - Italy (Sicily) - France (Pau, Dunkirk, Calais) - Germany (Hamburg) - United Kingdom (Wakefield) Americas: United States (Los Angeles, Miami, Asheville, Richmond, Washington, Maryland, Philadelphia, New York, Baltimore, Houston, Orange, Santa Monica, Pasadena, etc.), Mexico, Cuba Africa: Kenya Oceania: Australia (Sydney, Melbourne, Perth) The countries and cities listed above are only a partial list. The Inspiration Behind the Creation of "Angel Wings" The enduring appeal of "Angel Wings" lies not only in the visual beauty and impact it brings as a large-scale interactive street art project, but also stems from Miller's heartfelt dedication behind its creation. She has said that this global charity initiative was originally intended to celebrate the goodness within humanity and spread the belief that "all people are angels on Earth - kind angels." As both creator and initiator of the project, Miller further stated, "No one can truly own it, not even me, because it belongs to all of humanity." And choosing "City of Angels" Los Angeles as the first stop for the entire project is because in such an international metropolis that blends different nationalities, languages, and faiths, people are more willing to embrace diversity and inclusivity, thus allowing the love and positive energy of "Angel Wings" to smoothly reach more people. **City of Angels—Los Angeles** Los Angeles is the second largest city in the United States, with a pleasant spring-like climate year-round, favorable weather conditions, endless beaches, and bright, charming sunshine. As early as the 17th century, when Spanish expeditions established churches here, this romantic nation named it "Town of Our Lady Mary the Queen of Angels." In Spanish, Los Angeles precisely means "City of Angels." From then on, Los Angeles has enjoyed the prestigious reputation of being the "City of Angels." Today, as more contemporary artists seeking freedom and cutting-edge trends relocate here, every flower, tree, street, and wall in Los Angeles transforms into their canvas, birthing countless profoundly influential artworks. Among them, the success of the "Angel Wings" project not only demonstrates the international popularity of America's avant-garde street culture but also reflects how audiences engage with artistic content as art becomes more accessible to the masses. The photographer was Gary Leonard, who has photographed hundreds of people throughout Los Angeles. His other street photography works are also highly praised within the industry.
