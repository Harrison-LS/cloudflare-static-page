Disney's ticket types mainly fall into one-day tickets, two-day tickets, multi-day tickets, and annual passes. Children under 3 years old do not need to purchase admission tickets; children aged 3-9 require child tickets; those 10 and above must buy adult tickets. Tickets can be purchased directly on the California Disneyland official website, but payment requires an international bank card. When purchasing online, a dialog box will pop up requesting account registration; you must register using an email address to proceed with booking. After successful reservation, electronic tickets are sent to your email within 24 hours, so be sure to monitor whether they have been issued. If you encounter delayed ticket issuance, remember to follow up via phone or email. Of course, you can also purchase from travel agency partners; the prices are reasonable and there are certain discounts compared to the official website! Once you receive electronic tickets like those shown below, verify the ticket type and validity period, save the digital ticket on your phone, and simply scan the QR code upon park entry - that's all it takes! Tips: Disney tickets are divided into three types based on the season: Value Season tickets, Regular Day tickets, and Peak Season tickets. Each has a one-year validity period. It's important to note that Value Season tickets cannot be used during Regular or Peak seasons; Regular Season tickets cannot be used during Peak Season. If you plan to use tickets across different seasons, you'll need to pay the price difference at the park entrance upon entry, with prices determined by what the park charges. 1) One-Day Tickets If you're only staying at Disney for one day, there are two ticket options available: 1.
