Los Angeles Shopping Guide | Shopping Districts, Malls, and Outlet Stores

Los Angeles

Brief

Content

For Los Angeles, deciding where to shop isn't a question since excellent shopping malls, outlet stores, and street shopping options can be found everywhere. Hit the hottest Robertson Boulevard to find brands beloved by celebrities; explore Melrose Avenue for unique fashion designs; visit The Grove to discover latest trends and enjoy incredible low prices in downtown LA's fashion district. 1. What's Worth Buying in Los Angeles 1) Lakers Team Memorabilia For devoted Lakers fans, the city offers more than its entertainment scene - direct access to team experiences is unmatched. Around the arena, you'll easily discover premium Lakers merchandise including jerseys, hats, towels, and keychains among countless other options. 2) Disney Souvenirs Disneyland provides an abundance of charming keepsakes like keychains, pins, and glassware that capture the magic of the experience. The park's unique charm extends to its adorable plush toys - many featuring distinctive limited edition designs that become treasured mementos visitors can't resist bringing home. 3) Luxury Goods When it comes to overseas shopping sprees, most people immediately think of Hong Kong and Europe. However little is known that shopping in the United States truly offers vast landscapes with few crowds, numerous brands, and extremely low prices. Besides tax refunds, American luxury goods are not only cheaper originally but frequently discounted and on sale, making purchases very worthwhile. 4) Jewelry & Accessories The USA is considered a shopping paradise by people worldwide—a premier destination for shoppers. Various accessories here offer great value with vibrant colors and styles. Most importantly, prices are significantly lower than in China. 5) Celebrity Memorabilia Los Angeles is an international metropolis where many celebrities call home. Walking around LA, you might easily encounter your favorite stars. You even have opportunities to participate in filming for famous TV shows—making this the ultimate destination for fan meetups. In numerous souvenir shops, you can purchase authentic celebrity items such as clothing with signatures, special props used in movies, or autographed memorabilia from stars. 6) Chocolate Los Angeles offers an impressive variety of chocolates. Major shopping centers throughout the city stock affordable yet high-quality chocolate at excellent prices. The most popular choice is Godiva, a Belgian chocolate beloved by many Los Angeles residents. 7) Electronics & Digital Products The United States hosts numerous renowned electronics brands including Apple, GE, Microsoft, Google, BlackBerry, HP, Kodak, and Dell. Here you can purchase the latest trendy digital products you desire. 2、Shopping Stores/Districts in Los Angeles 1) Desert Hills Premium Outlets in Palm Springs The largest outlet mall with over 120 specialty stores offering discounted past-season items from world-class luxury brands at factory prices. Almost all major brands you can think of will appear here, including Burberry, Coach, A/X, Calvin Klein, Diesel, Christian Dior, DKNY, D&G, Polo, Giorgio Armani, Gucci, Hugo Boss, Levis, Tommy Hilfiger. It has earned the reputation as a "shopping paradise in the desert." If planning to shop here, it's recommended to avoid visiting right after major Chinese holidays like National Day or Lunar New Year, because during these long holiday periods many compatriots come to do shopping, leaving fewer good items behind. Address: 48400 Seminole Drive Cabazon, CA 92230, United States 2) Camarillo Premium Outlets Divided into three areas with luxury department store outlet stores including Barneys New York Outlet, Last Call by Neiman Marcus, and Saks Fifth Avenue Off 5th. Many high-end brands' past-season inventory is sold here, making it an excellent place to buy premium clothing and handbags. Within the specialty store, there are fewer of the top European luxury brands such as Dior, Prada, and Gucci; these are not found here. However, Giorgio Armani and Hugo Boss are available. Daily discounts range from 25% to 65%. Services include a consultation center, size charts in multiple languages, wheelchair rental, gift cards, ATMs, and more. Address: 740 E. Ventura Boulevard, Camarillo, CA 93010, United States (3) Citadel Outlets - Castle Outlet This is the only outlet mall located within Los Angeles city proper, just 10 minutes from downtown LA. With its excellent location and numerous attractions nearby, it allows visitors to explore Los Angeles downtown sights while shopping all in one go. Geographic factors are this outlet's greatest advantage. Inside, there are over 130 brand discount stores featuring brands popular among Chinese visitors such as Coach, Michael Kors, TUMI, Armani Exchange, H&M, Hugo Boss, Kate Spade New York, Calvin Klein, Tommy Hilfiger, Swarovski, and Ecco. If you have limited time or driving is inconvenient, this one outlet mall is sufficient to visit. Transportation is relatively convenient, and basically all major brands are available, though there are no European or American luxury brand outlet stores. Address: 100 Citadel Drive, Suite 480, Los Angeles, CA 90040, United States 4) Fashion District Mall A famous women's shopping street with shops lining the entire area, selling mid to low-range products. All the latest items you can see are available here, with prices starting at 30% off. One of the largest and cheapest shopping destinations in Los Angeles. Free guided tours are offered, each lasting about 60 minutes. Address: 110 East 9th Street A1175 Los Angeles, CA 90079 5) Macy's A famous American chain department store, mainly operating clothing, shoes, hats, and home decorations. Strolling around slowly, you can see many brands are much cheaper than in China, especially those American brands. Address: 8500 Beverly Boulevard Los Angeles, CA 90048 6) DFS Duty Free Store (Los Angeles International Airport location) Located at Terminal 7, with service staff reminding visitors when entering the airport. Estée Lauder will also have sales and offer small gifts or bonuses. It would be even better if you can catch the promotional events. 7) Third Street Promenade - Santa Monica Third Street Promenade Both sides of the street feature many cozy, romantic cafes, movie theaters, and numerous small shops beloved by young people. The "Third Street District," stretching from Santa Monica Plaza to Wilshire Boulevard, is all concentrated on this pedestrian-friendly shopping area. There are many American brands here, such as J.Crew, A&F, Guess, etc. Address: 1351 Third Street Promenade, Santa Monica, CA 90401, USA 8) Rodeo Drive Hailed as "one of the world's top ten shopping paradises," international master designers' precious jewelry and fine apparel converge here. The luxurious storefronts are grand like art galleries, earning its reputation as the most expensive shopping avenue in the world. Rodeo Drive boldly adopted elaborate decorations that match its status as the world's premier and priciest shopping destination. Adjacent to Rodeo Drive include Rodeo Drive Second and various European-style boutiques, along with cobblestone streets, fountains, and wine bars. Address: L 4 Rodeo Drive LLC, 268 N Rodeo Dr, Beverly Hills, CA 90210, USA 9) Beverly Center The premier high-end fashion shopping destination in Southern California, located at the foot of Beverly Hills, this is the best place to purchase seasonal luxury goods. The brands include top-tier luxury brands such as LV, Gucci, Prada, Hermès, Fendi, and others. Beverly Center has nearly 100 stores accepting China UnionPay cards. The customer service center provides Chinese service guides for consumers, and the official website also offers Chinese information for Chinese tourists.
