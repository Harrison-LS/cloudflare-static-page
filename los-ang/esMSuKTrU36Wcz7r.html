About GO123 What is GO123? This question was quite confusing for me before booking tickets. I've heard of Greyhound, Starline, but had no concept whatsoever about this Go123. Simply put, GO123 is a ground bus system that operates both within and outside cities. They have stops at different scenic spots where you can get on or off. With your ticket, you can disembark at the scenic spot you like, then board again to return after finishing your visit. Even without self-driving, you can still arrange your itinerary independently—the places I want to stop at, the time I want to spend—it's all up to me to decide. Three days for only $75 with unlimited on-and-off trips—worth it! This time, why not rent a car? First, before even arriving, friends told me this bustling city's poor road conditions can rival those of Beijing-Shanghai-Guangzhou, and parking is both difficult to find and paid. Second, GPS is convenient, but for a completely unfamiliar city with 8-lane expressways running parallel, you can only say it's drivable—it's just very draining. I don't want to waste all my time on the road. Third, normally renting a car for a day including insurance costs at least over $100. For three days that would be more than $300. I only spent $75, and the saved $200+ let me enjoy three big meals. Since this trip is purely for sightseeing and not very long, even if we rented our own car daily, we'd still be traveling between attractions every day. So rather than wasting energy on driving, it's better to go play at Universal Studios with full energy for a whole day. Long Beach Whether on Line 4 or Line 5, Go123 provides everyone with a full 8 hours of stay time during which you can explore all of Long Beach. First Stop: Pacific Aquarium California's largest aquarium covers 20,000 square meters and houses over 550 species totaling 12,500 marine creatures. With aquariums ranging from 5,000 to 350,000 gallons in capacity, there is a spectacular underwater tunnel that takes you into a colorful underwater world where you can view the vibrant, diverse marine life up close. The outdoor exhibits allow you to personally pet sea turtles or small sharks, while weekends and holidays feature exciting sea lion performances. Since opening, this attraction has welcomed 13 million visitors. It ranks as the second-best place in Greater Los Angeles for families, behind only Disney. Second Stop: Whale Watching by Sea When visiting California, there is one very interesting and relaxing activity that tourists often overlook – whale watching! More than 30,000 whales migrate along the Southern California coast each year. Winter is an excellent time to spot gray whales and humpback whales, while summer belongs to blue whales – the world's largest mammals! During this 2.5-hour journey, besides seeing whales, you can also observe other marine animals such as dolphins, sea lions, and various seabirds. Dolphins chasing the cruise boat in groups and huge whales rolling in the distance create an exciting scene that will be unforgettable for a lifetime. Note: The Pacific Aquarium and whale watching tour are packages led by aquarium professionals for learning about marine mammals. This activity takes approximately 4-5 hours. After visitors disembark at Pacific Aquarium to participate in the activity, they need to take the Aqua Bus to Shoreline Village station, then park at LOUISIANA Charlie's BBQ entrance parking lot to board the GO123 shuttle bus. Third stop: Naples Island - Take a Gondola Thanks to some genius idea for bringing Italy Venice's unique "Gondola" to the canals on the Pacific South Bank. With handsome boatmen's melodious songs in ears, ride small boats called "Gondolas" through million-dollar mansions. Drink champagne, chat with boat owners sunbathing at private docks - what a pleasant day! Besides Gondola boat tours, every Christmas magnificent decorations are also attractions for visitors.
