The upper platform provides panoramic views of Los Angeles city, and the dome theater allows 360-degree stargazing to truly feel how small humans are. • Located atop the northern mountain overlooking Los Angeles, offering panoramic views of the entire city. • Features astronomical exhibits covering planets and the universe. Hollywood Walk of Fame Walk of Fame by JYC The Hollywood Walk of Fame (Hollywood Star Avenue), one of Los Angeles' landmarks. This avenue includes over 2500 five-pointed stars made of terrazzo and brass, embedded along the sidewalks of 15 blocks on "Hollywood Boulevard" and 3 blocks on "Vine Street." These "stars" represent an eternal commemoration for those who have achieved outstanding excellence in the entertainment industry, recording names of many actors, musicians, directors, etc. Friends who love kung fu movies can also look for Jackie Chan and Bruce Lee's handprints to take photos as souvenirs~ Hollywood Walk of Fame · The Hollywood Walk of Fame was established in 1958, a sidewalk extending along Hollywood Boulevard and Vine Street in the United States, representing one of Los Angeles' famous cultural and historical landmarks. · The street features over 2000 star-shaped medals with names of Hollywood celebrities, commemorating their contributions to the entertainment industry. · Unless occasionally relocated due to nearby construction or other reasons, the positions of the star-shaped medals on the avenue remain permanently unchanged. Los Angeles County Museum of Art Inside the Los Angeles County Museum of Art are five buildings, three outdoor sculptures, an art library, an arts resource center, a large lecture hall and theater, housing thousands of artworks. It is the largest "encyclopedic" public museum on the US West Coast. The exhibition interior features a pillar-free spacious hall with open space; combined with natural light introduction, it creates an ideal display environment for contemporary art. The Getty Center mainly collects Western artworks from the Middle Ages to modern times, featuring everything from medieval illuminated manuscripts, sketches, manuscripts, sculptures to photographic masterpieces. The crown jewel at the Getty Center is "Irises" created by Van Gogh one year before his death. Stroll slowly with your loved ones here and feel romance and art in a quiet atmosphere. The Getty Center • A cluster of unique buildings standing on the cliffside, representing one of Los Angeles' most important and wealthiest artistic institutions in the world. • Comprising six buildings, extensive gardens, and open fields - it's an excellent spot for panoramic views of Los Angeles. • Houses significant American manuscript autographs and vintage photographs, making it one of the largest collections of such artifacts in America. • The collection includes ancient Roman and Greek marble sculptures, illuminated manuscripts, European paintings, sketches, carvings, and more. Unmissable Local Cuisine in Los Angeles The Farmers Market Los Angeles' Original Farmers Market has a long history and has grown into a tourist destination market with fixed operating hours and iconic architecture. Here you'll find not only the freshest daily produce, seafood, but also numerous American and Mexican restaurants and snack stalls - delicious with generous portions. Farmer's Market A farmer's market in downtown Los Angeles is a tourist-oriented market featuring fixed location, operating hours, and permanent buildings, serving as both a marketplace and dining destination. It offers parking facilities, fresh produce stands, made-to-order candy shops, various ethnic restaurants, souvenir stores, book and magazine shops - everything you could want. Little Tokyo Little Tokyo is a food street in Downtown LA filled with Japanese-style cuisine, hence its name. It's also a uniquely exotic area of Los Angeles. Here you can enjoy authentic, high-quality yet reasonably priced sushi, skewers (yakitori), ramen, curry, and more. After exploring, you can confidently choose your own path - restaurants, eateries, supermarkets, and small shops all radiate strong Japanese atmosphere. Experiencing Japan's ambiance in America is itself a unique experience. Little Tokyo Whether the street scenery, restaurant shops, window displays, entertainment venues, or even the passersby - everywhere exudes Japanese flavor, hence its name "Little Tokyo," also a uniquely exotic area of Los Angeles. Little Tokyo is easily explored on foot. Its history dates back approximately 100 years, becoming prosperous after World War I, though it was again destroyed during World War II. After recent renovation efforts, the area now presents itself as vibrant and cohesive, featuring both 19th-century commercial architecture and modern buildings. The annual highlight of Little Tokyo is Nisei Week, held every August. The event showcases various performances including flower arrangement (ikebana), tea ceremony, calligraphy, Japanese art, parades, and street entertainment. Beyond shopping for Japanese goods, Little Tokyo remains the best place in Los Angeles to experience authentic Japanese cuisine—home to numerous sushi restaurants and izakayas, plus evening karaoke venues and other entertainment options. How do you find your way to these attractions without a car? The itinerary above is very convenient for those who have private drivers or rental cars. But what about tourists without vehicles—how should they reach these destinations to check them out? Here are some practical public transportation options for your reference: 1. **Universal Studios**: Take the subway Red Line to Universal City Station. A free shuttle is available not far from the exit and takes about 5 minutes to arrive. 2. **Griffith Observatory**: Take the downtown loop bus DASH Weekend Observatory Shuttle and get off at Griffith Observatory station. 3. **Hollywood Walk of Fame**: Take the subway Red Line to Hollywood/Highland Station, and you'll be right on the Walk of Fame. 4. **Beverly Hills**: Take bus route 4 to Santa Monica/Crescent Station. 5. **Santa Monica Beach**: You can take buses 20, 33, or 534 to Ocean/Colorado Station. 6. **Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA)**: Take bus route 20 to Wilshire Blvd & Ogden Ave station. 7. **Getty Center**: Take bus route 234 to Sepulveda/Getty Center Station. Visitors can only park in the lot at the base of the hill and must take an elevator up and down between the parking area and the art center on top of the mountain. 8. **Pink Wall**: Located at 8221 Melrose Ave, featuring Paul Smith's exterior design. 9. **Castle Rock Outlet**: Take bus route 62 to Telegraph/Citadel Station, then walk about 3 minutes to arrive. 10. Farmers Market: Bus lines 217 and 780, Fairfax/3rd Station, approximately 2 minutes walking distance. 11.
