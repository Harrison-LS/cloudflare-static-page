When it comes to Los Angeles travel, the major attractions you must visit have become an essential part of mainstream travelogues—such as Hollywood, Santa Monica Beach, Universal Studios, Griffith Observatory, etc. Only these "landmark" destinations can achieve that effect where one photo posted allows you to subtly show off; therefore this is also the prime area where tour groups are rampant! If you just want to rush through the entire itinerary in a superficial manner and check off all the must-visit spots one by one, then congratulations—your "tourist" journey is perfectly concluded! But if you desire a travel experience closer to authentic American life? How do Americans spend their weekends? Then you absolutely cannot forget to do these few things below: ☆ Visit restaurants locals actually frequent for breakfast ☆ Cultivate an appreciation for museums like Americans do ☆ Watch a performance at a small theater on a film set location "Food, clothing, housing, and transportation"—living well ranks second only to eating well! Beverly Hills is one of the finest neighborhoods in Los Angeles. From street furniture details and exquisite boutiques, you can glimpse the luxurious leisurely life of America's wealthy class—how many wouldn't want to live here? The rich artistic and humanistic atmosphere is a unique characteristic of the Miracle Mile community. Los Angeles' vibrant fashion energy has not affected its quiet yet wonderful ambiance. Its location near Beverly Hills makes this area resemble an upscale American neighborhood, with exquisite small shops scattered throughout. More importantly, given such excellent geography, could there be a better place to integrate into local life than here? Do you know these niche museums? Museums are the main event for today, but a wonderful day begins with an authentic gourmet breakfast~ Republique is an emerging American + French restaurant. The waiters who move through the spacious hall are cute and friendly, their smiles filled with the scent of California sunshine. The building housing the restaurant was constructed in 1928; its light and airy pastoral interior decor creates a striking contrast with the slightly classical exterior architecture. Transparent glass windows and high, unique ceilings allow people to instantly relax, making time feel exceptionally long. With beautiful presentation and delicious dishes that have captivated a large fan base, Republique has rightfully become an authentic "internet celebrity" restaurant. If you want to dine here after noon, be prepared to wait in line. Republique's head chef Walter Manzke and his wife Margarita prepare various delicious baked goods every morning, so from the start of the day, this place attracts locals who love food. When you receive the menu, there really is no need to overthink it; just trust your intuition because none of their options will disappoint you. Brunch here leans more toward French cuisine, such as kimchi fried rice made with short ribs and hard-boiled eggs, or Belgian waffles paired with Harry's Berries. Republique, with its rich menu and even the habit of changing it daily, must be your first choice for breakfast every day! If an area doesn't have museums nearby, what qualification does it have to call itself an artsy district? Drive just 5 minutes from apartments to reach this museum cluster. Here you'll find the Los Angeles County Museum of Art, The Museum of Craft and Folk Art, the Peacock Museum, and the Petersen Automotive Museum - each with its own unique character. 1. Los Angeles County Museum of Art The LACMA houses an extensive collection of precious artifacts. For those who are art-savvy, spending a whole day enjoying masterpieces from artists around the world is a wonderful choice. Another way to enjoy LACMA is to visit this Instagram-worthy landmark at dusk when the light pillars illuminate. Official Website: *** Address: 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Los Angeles, CA 90036-4597 2. Petersen Automotive Museum If you are a car enthusiast, you absolutely cannot miss the Petersen Automotive Museum! This museum spans three floors, with the top floor displaying vintage collectible cars aged between 60 to over 100 years. They showcase the meticulous craftsmanship of artisans from those bygone days - truly amazing and admirable work! The second floor features industrial displays ranging from high-speed racing cars to modified race cars, as well as emerging electric vehicles—truly showcasing the era's characteristics. The first floor is dedicated to artistic displays, emphasizing vehicle body design and the artistic ingenuity incorporated into each car structure. The fortunate encounters with Ferrari and Bugatti exhibits were quite delightful—all the cool designs make this trip worthwhile, plus there are cars that have appeared in films (like the DeLorean from Back to the Future). The entire exhibition hall's design is exceptional—without those pesky glass barriers preventing you from viewing your favorite models up close and from all angles. So please travel civilly and respect all exhibits within the showroom! If your English skills are good, I highly recommend the 90-minute vault tour; although it requires an additional fee, you can learn many more interesting stories behind these cars through the guide's knowledgeable commentary. Both LACMA Cafe (address: 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Los Angeles, CA 90036-4504) near the Los Angeles County Museum of Art and the museum restaurant at the Petersen Automotive Museum are excellent dining choices. Return slowly along your original route; if you have time, you might even attend a performance at El Rey Theater. This is a small theater that has been open for over 30 years. Many regular customers have spent countless important moments of their lives at this intimate venue. "La La Land," acclaimed as Los Angeles travel promotion, was also filmed here. In the film, after Sebastian joined Keith's band, one of the performances took place at El Rey Theatre. Tickets purchased through the official website do not come with assigned seating. El Rey Theatre operates on a first-come-first-served basis, and interior seating arrangements are adjusted depending on the type of performance.
