Los Angeles | How to Apply for Free Audience Tickets to the 2 Broke Girls Taping

Los Angeles

Brief

Los Angeles, the entertainment capital, is a paradise for fans of American TV series. While planning my trip earlier, I happened upon a website where you can apply for live-on-set viewing tickets, so I tried it out right away. "The Big Bang Theory" was too popular and all tickets were sold in seconds when they went on sale. Fortunately, there were still some left for "2 Broke Girls," so I got to experience the exciting atmosphere of a sitcom recording session firsthand.

Tips for the Day of Screening (1) Arrive a bit earlier! A bit earlier! A bit earlier! Important things are said three times. You ask how much earlier? Let me give an example. A friend went to watch "The Big Bang Theory", the screening was scheduled to start at 6:30 PM, and they arrived at 11 AM. She said the earliest audience members started waiting from 8 AM. I didn't get a ticket for "The Big Bang Theory", so let me talk about the situation with "2 Broke Girls" instead. "2 Broke Girls" filming started at 5 PM, assembly was at 3:30 PM, and we arrived around 2 PM. When we got there, the queue had grown long like a Snake game that's been played for a very long time. So even though we had formal tickets, we were silently given "Good Person Cards" (a green card that records names of audience members who couldn't get in on that day, allowing them to return at any later time to watch any episode) and then dispersed. If you live permanently in Los Angeles, there's no problem - having the option to return anytime with the card is a good choice. But for us who were leaving Los Angeles the next day, it was quite tragic. Fortunately, after explaining our situation to the staff and waiting for one or two hours, they eventually let us into the venue. Thank you to the VIP who left early to visit! I just want to say there are truly loving people in this world and true love exists hahaha~ (2) Recommend driving yourself. Both "The Big Bang Theory" and "2 Broke Girls" filming locations are at Warner Bros Studios. Public transportation from downtown isn't very convenient. Plus as a car-dependent city, it's really hard to get around Los Angeles without a vehicle. So I recommend everyone drive themselves. Also since phones, cameras, and other electronic devices aren't allowed inside the venue, driving means you can leave valuables in your car which is relatively more convenient. More importantly, after leaving the show you can exit this place with no pressure! (3) Rest early the day before, get well-rested T T Don't think I'm being wordy, this is important! Although one episode is only 20+ minutes, filming can take as little as three-four hours or as long as five-six hours. Sitting for that long really drains your energy. For example on our viewing day, filming went from 5 PM straight until almost midnight the next night. Also since laughter was captured live on-site, some jokes needed to be laughed at several times in a row, laughing so much it made everyone feel a bit unwell. However the atmosphere on-site was fantastic. The audience seating area consists of several rows in a long rectangular shape, there's a dedicated warm-up host who interacts with the audience during breaks between shooting segments. Viewers who interact particularly enthusiastically also get souvenirs like T-shirts. Mr./Ms. Huang and I, as representatives for international friends, were also given two of Max's homemade cupcakes hahaha. One last reminder: don't drink too much water, because you need to gather 8 people before staff take everyone to the restroom together. I heard someone held it for over an hour just waiting for 8 people! Since electronic devices aren't allowed on-site there are no photos taken.
