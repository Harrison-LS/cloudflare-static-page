Monument Valley Due to space limitations, only Yellowstone National Park's attractions will be introduced here. Yellowstone on Magma Do you remember the disaster film "2012"? The male protagonist traveled to Yellowstone National Park and discovered that Lake Yellowstone had completely dried up, thereby predicting the arrival of global catastrophe. Lake Yellowstone was formed from water accumulating in a volcanic crater. The entire park sits on a volcanic belt, so saying Yellowstone is a park built upon magma is no exaggeration. This is a park undergoing constant renewal, ready to transform at any moment due to tectonic activity. For instance, the Mammoth Hot Springs area - before 2002 it was a magnificent terraced formation with vibrant colors. But following a tectonic event in 2002, most of the springs dried up and the colorful microorganisms quickly perished. The once-vibrant terraces transformed into a tragic beauty of gray-white tones. So if you havent visited Yellowstone yet, please do not hesitate. Who knows which beautiful spot might disappear without your noticing? How should you accommodate inside Yellowstone National Park? However, in today's guide, I won't write about these well-known popular spots because I want to recommend a lesser-known but must-see attraction around Yellowstone - Bear Tooth Highway. Bear Tooth Highway Bear Tooth Highway experiences its first snowfall in mid-to-late September each year, not fully melting until early July of the following year. With steep slopes and sharp curves, it is only open to visitors from late May to early September each year. The Dangerous and Sharp Curves on Bear Tooth Highway Following the recommended route, drive from Billings to Bear Tooth Highway, then travel along Bear Tooth Highway from the Northeast Gate to Yellowstone's Roosevelt Area, before heading to your next destination. Bear Tooth Highway consists of two main sections. The first segment runs toward Yellowstone starting near Red Lodge, where you'll encounter numerous sharp turns. Many drivers leave sweating and often boast about their adventurous drive afterward when the trip ends. After navigating several tight curves, you enter the High Country Lake District, where the road gradually eases from steep to gentle, allowing your mind to relax as well. Crystal-clear lakes of various sizes dot the landscape, with wildflowers blooming everywhere, making everything feel like a fairy tale that transcends ordinary reality. Driving slowly along the highway, pristine white clouds seem within reach, creating a wonderful sensation of traveling through mist and fog. Bear Tooth Highway is considered niche not only because few domestic tour groups develop it, but more importantly due to its steep roads where large trucks cannot travel—only small self-driving vehicles can enter. Since Bear Tooth Highway remains unknown to most people, why not take the opportunity to experience it? You'll definitely have plenty to brag about in front of other car enthusiasts for years!
