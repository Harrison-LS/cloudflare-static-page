Los Angeles | All the best brunch spots might be in this one article!

Los Angeles

Brief

After exploring, I'm convinced that L.A. is truly the brunch capital! Sleeping in until late morning, waking up refreshed by natural sunlight, and bathing in California's brilliant sunshine - pedestrians and shops on the street all feel very chill~ As for which restaurants are the best? Come along with Lulu to find out~

Content

Rainbow Latte at BBCM First up is BBCM - my favorite brunch spot in L.A., so I headed there right away on the first day. The location is fantastic, right in Beverly Hills, and you can stroll around nearby after eating. The restaurant has both indoor and outdoor areas; I especially love their small garden. With olive trees, floral tiles, and colorful sofas, it has a bit of exotic charm~ If you want to take beautiful photos, the outdoor seating would be more suitable, but the indoor space is also very comfortable. Bright white tones dominate, with handwritten blackboards, light fixtures, and tiles creating elegant accents throughout. Led by Michelin-starred chef Craig Hopson, this place is definitely worth your visit. You must order a Rainbow Latte here - as the name suggests, it's a latte with colorful latte art~ It's also their signature drink and scores 100 points on aesthetics. All Day Brunch format means even if you arrive late in the evening, you can still get these dishes. They use seasonal ingredients from nearby farms, and honestly everything tastes great! The Eggs Benedict is also highly recommended! Address: 8653 Sunset Blvd, West Hollywood, CA 90069 Phone: +1-310-360-6900 Great White: Venice Beach's Trending Hotspot Every time I visit L.A., I always make sure to stroll around Venice Beach; the leisurely and comfortable seaside atmosphere is truly wonderful. This trip, I specifically came to try Great White, a popular brunch spot in Venice Beach that has been gaining traction recently. I was genuinely shocked by the massive crowds lining up—heard it's even worse on weekends. After waiting quite some time, we finally got seated. There were more locals dining than tourists. Besides the few small tables outside, the indoor space is also not large. This compact yet charming room features natural wood tones and white walls as its main elements, creating a relaxed and warm vibe. The clean and minimalist decor maintains proper spacing between tables. Although it's busy with people and slightly noisy, it remains quite cozy. The food looks appealing and tastes good—healthy light meals that combine daily fresh ingredients with seafood caught the same day; the coffee is also excellent. The most popular item on Instagram is definitely this Blue Smoothie Bowl in the bottom right corner. It's a must-order for almost every table. Besides its stunning appearance, the flavor truly delivers~ Made from various fruits and blue spirulina blended into a smoothie, then topped with the restaurant's in-house baked granola and fresh blueberries and strawberries. It tastes tangy and sweet, quite refreshing. Address: 1604 Pacific Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90291 Phone: +1-424-744-8403 Catch: Flower Room Seafood Restaurant Catch is arguably one of the hottest restaurants in L.A. right now - you basically can't even get a table without making a reservation ahead of time. Located on the top floor, as soon as you open the elevator door, you'll be wowed by the dreamy decor. The entire corridor is hung with vines and fresh flowers, looking great from almost every angle for photos. At night, string lights turn on, which is equally beautiful~ Catch focuses on seafood cuisine in a mix of Japanese and Western styles, with many dishes being both creative and delicious. They only serve brunch on weekends, and are open for dinner during the week. There's also a great selection of cocktails to choose from. The portions aren't large, so you can order more to share with friends~ Address: 8715 Melrose Ave, West Hollywood, CA 90069 Phone: +1-323-347-6060 Commissary: Fusion Korean Food in a Glass House Please come dine here on a sunny afternoon! Inside the large glass greenhouse filled with greenery, it is very bright and beautiful—a relaxed and comfortable environment where your mood lifts along with California sunshine~ Commissary is located on the second floor of Line Hotel in K Town's Koreatown and has recently become a popular brunch destination. Dining here feels like entering a botanical garden—refreshing and calming. And Commissary goes beyond just being visually appealing; many small details are done thoughtfully and interestingly, such as cute patterned napkins and utensils, colorful menus, and envelope-style bills at the counter. Even blankets are provided for guests dining outdoors. The dishes differ from typical brunch offerings, featuring Korean-American fusion cuisine. Chef and owner Roy Choi is a well-known representative figure in local Korean fusion dining. He was once selected as one of Time magazine's 100 most influential people in 2016 for pioneering the food truck concept. Menu options are mainly rice bowls with various combinations of fresh ingredients—truly delicious! And the value for money is quite good~ Address: 3515 Wilshire Blvd, Fl 2 Greenhouse, The Line Hotel, Los Angeles, CA 90010 Phone: +1-213-368-3030 Hart & Hunter: Heard to have L.A.'s best avocado toast Coming here on reputation after seeing Amiee Song once rate this place as having the best avocado toast in all of Los Angeles. Located on the first floor of Pali Hotel, now this place is also a hot spot that requires queuing. Best to make a reservation beforehand—you can get a very chill terrace seat. The restaurant matches the boutique hotel's style with an American retro feel, large fabric sofas, mood-setting paintings, and old books scattered around on carpets, looking particularly laid-back and artistic. You naturally find yourself slumped on the sofa, chatting with friends until you lose track of time. Amiee Song's recommended avocado toast is a must-order signature dish here—crispy toast topped generously with avocado and slightly spicy, the taste is indeed good, avocado lovers should all like it~ There are also quite a few meal choices; egg benedict and pancakes are both quite delicious. Address: 7950 Melrose Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90048 Phone: +1-323-424-3055 Zinque: A brunch hotspot endorsed by Gigi Hadid Zinque is basically a French restaurant that was made famous by Kim Kardashian. She's not only a regular customer here, but many street style photos use this place as the background. When you go there, keep an eye out for the chance to spot celebrities! The restaurant has both outdoor and indoor dining areas. In the small courtyard, tall palm trees are set against blue sky and white clouds, creating a very relaxed California atmosphere. The interior decoration is quite cute - like flower tile fireplaces, brass pendant lights that show attention to decorative details. It's perfect for chatting with friends and enjoying a leisurely atmosphere; it feels like most customers are locals too~ The restaurant operates from morning to night. During the day it's a brunch spot, but at night it transforms into a bar with many craft beer options. I've heard the atmosphere is also quite good~ The brunch dishes focus on healthy ingredients - salads, sandwiches, etc., all incorporating plenty of fresh vegetables and fruits. The taste is refreshing, feeling very fresh. This might be one of the reasons why Kim likes this restaurant so much~ The coffee is also quite good~ Address: 600 Venice Blvd, Venice, CA 90291 Phone: +1-310-437-0970 Republique: French brunch in an old building Now one of the most popular influencer restaurants in L.A., Republique is guaranteed to have endless lines during peak dining hours. You'll find it impossible to miss its distinctive blue door and historic brick walls from the sidewalk. The restaurant uses a self-ordering system where you order first, then claim your seat, with food delivered directly to your table. Located inside a building constructed in 1929, Republique preserves its historical courtyard architecture, featuring weathered brick walls and vintage street lamps that create an atmosphere like dining within a medieval castle. The spacious ceilings are lined with large glass windows that flood the space with bright natural light, making it especially atmospheric during daytime visits. The semi-open bakery kitchen is equally captivating. Wooden shelves neatly arranged before white tiles display various utensils in perfect order, while constantly wafting aromas of freshly baked bread and pastries stimulate your appetite. Nearly every table orders a kimchi fried rice that feels anachronistic yet perfectly suited to this setting, with genuinely delicious flavor~ Address: 624 S La Brea Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90036 Phone: +1-310-362-6115
