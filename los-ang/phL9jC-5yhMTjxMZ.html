What are the major questions you need to know before reserving weekday tickets? 1. When does the NBA season begin each year? How long does it last? The regular season starts at the end of October/beginning of November and continues until around April 20 of the following year. With 30 teams playing a total of 1189 games, there are multiple matches every day. The playoffs begin in late April and conclude in mid-June. It is an elimination tournament where the 16 teams that advance compete to determine the two finalists. Preseason games are unofficial warm-up contests before the regular season, lasting about one month, typically not played at team home courts. All-Star Weekend is a non-official showcase featuring NBA stars, including the Rookie Game, Skills Challenge, and All-Star Game, usually held on a weekend in February. So from October to June of the following year, you can watch NBA games in America. 2. How early can tickets be reserved? Regular season tickets can be purchased before the season begins until the day before each game. Playoff tickets only start selling after the regular season ends. Finals tickets begin selling near the final because it is uncertain which teams will advance and where they will play. All-Star Weekend packages start selling six months in advance. 3. How are NBA tickets priced? Can we have fixed prices year-round? No. While regular season dates are confirmed early, ticket prices for each game vary significantly. Playoffs and finals make it even more unpredictable since teams and venues cannot be confirmed in advance, eliminating any possibility of fixed pricing. What is the approximate price range? Regular season tickets generally range from $50 to $500 USD, with popular team games potentially starting at $150 or higher. Playoff prices are significantly higher, with hundreds to thousands of dollars being common. Finals tickets can even exceed $10,000. Which teams are most popular? Los Angeles Lakers, Los Angeles Clippers, Golden State Warriors (San Francisco), Boston Celtics, Cleveland Cavaliers, Houston Rockets, San Antonio Spurs, Dallas Mavericks, New York Knicks, Miami Heat, Toronto Raptors, and Chicago Bulls. 4. What areas correspond to ticket prices? Stadium seating is generally arranged in an oval around the court, divided into three tiers: inner circle (sections numbered 1), middle circle (sections numbered 2), and outer circle, commonly called "up top" or "the mountain" (sections numbered 3). The inner circle is most expensive, while the outer circle is cheapest. Sections along the sides cost more than those at the ends (behind the baskets). However, even though they're at the ends of the inner circle, these seats are still priced higher than outer circle side sections. The price hierarchy can be expressed as: Inner circle side > Inner corner > Inner basket end > Middle circle side > Middle corner > Middle basket end > Outer circle side > Outer corner > Outer basket end Stadium Map 5. Tickets are generally electronic and typically do not display the price on them, but will include the buyer's name, which does not affect usage. For resale tickets, there might be the previous owner's name, but this also does not impact usability. When using them, you need to print out the electronic ticket, scan the code for entry - no additional ticket exchange is required. 7. If a game is scheduled in your itinerary, how much time should be reserved for the match? The official game duration is 12 minutes × 4 quarters = 48 minutes, with overtime being 5 minutes per period. There is a 130-second break between the first and second quarters, as well as between the third and fourth quarters. Section 2 and 3 have a 15-minute break. Between the 4th quarter and overtime, as well as between any overtimes, there is a 100-second break. There are also fouls, free throws, timeouts, substitutions, etc., so a game takes about two hours or more. Including entrance and exit time, it is recommended to allow at least 3.5-4 hours when planning your itinerary. 8. Other things to note when watching games? Can boxes be reserved? Besides regular seating, there are also private boxes. Boxes are generally located between the middle section and outer circle. The view is not as good as from the middle or inner circles, but they offer independent space, comfortable seats, TVs to watch live broadcasts, plus drinks and snacks. Boxes come in different sizes, typically accommodating 6-30 people. Average cost per person is around $200-$500. Playoff and final game prices available upon request. Boxes cannot be booked directly online; you need to contact the stadium sales office, so it is recommended to place your order at least one month in advance. Do you need tickets to enter the stadium on regular days? When watching a game, besides the ticket fee, are there other fees? The stadium is generally not open to tourists unless under special circumstances, where authorization can be requested from relevant organizations. When attending a game, you only need to purchase game tickets with no additional fees. Food inside the stadium incurs separate charges. Are there any tips for watching games? How early should I arrive? Can I bring bags and food? You can enter 90 minutes before the game starts; it is recommended to go early so you can see stars practicing on-site at that time. Stadium management is not as strict then, and with good luck, you might even find seats in the inner field area to take commemorative photos with players in the background. (Low Probability) You may bring bags into the arena, but all must undergo security screening. Backpacks and professional photography equipment may be required to check at the security deposit area. No food is permitted inside (if you have special dietary habits or requirements, proof or medical advice must be provided). There are many food vendors inside the stadium, mainly fast food and snacks, with prices slightly higher than usual. Before the game begins, everyone will stand up for the American national anthem. Even though we're not Americans, when visiting someone's culture it is respectful to follow their customs—so you should probably also stand up. Dress code recommendations: casual attire works best, matching the relaxed and entertaining atmosphere on site. When watching games live in person, be absolutely certain about home versus away teams! If you support a home team, join the enthusiasm of the majority of people; home "super fans" can even wear their favorite player's jersey to the game. But if you support an away team, try hard to find your fellow supporters. When the away team scores, it is best not to show off too much—after all, you're on someone else's turf, safety first. While watching, maintain basic etiquette and watch civilly; avoid casually throwing things when excited, or you might be "asked" out of the arena by security personnel. When the game ends, please take away your own trash. Of the 30 teams, only the Toronto Raptors are not based in the United States; their home is in Canada's Toronto. 2. What's the difference between watching live versus on TV? Watching live allows you to experience the team's performance firsthand, with your emotions rising and falling along with their score, while also feeling an atmosphere unlike what you get from television. American people are warm and open-minded. Even strangers you barely know have a good chance of coming over to high-five or even hug you when someone scores for the same team they support. The home team's mascots frequently run out to interact with the audience - very affectionate. The opening performances and dance squads are also quite entertaining (not just visually appealing, there's more to appreciate). During halftime there will be segments where the big screen interacts with the audience. Sometimes they play trending music, and randomly selected spectators must improvise dancing; sometimes it's "kissing time" or "hugging time," where people caught on camera are encouraged to embrace or kiss each other - even if not actual couples, most open-minded Americans will happily cooperate. This feeling of being fully immersed in the game definitely feels much more exciting than quietly watching the broadcast at home. Even basketball fans who only know a little about the sport can easily get into this atmosphere. 5. What are the differences between NBA rules and FIBA standards? Compared to other basketball competitions, the NBA places greater emphasis on entertainment value and commercial appeal, so its rules differ somewhat from international norms. Here are some of these differences: National Basketball Association (NBA) Fédération Internationale de Basketball (FIBA) Court dimensions: - NBA courts are shorter and wider than FIBA courts - The NBA restricted area is rectangular, while FIBA's is trapezoidal NBA's three-point line is 6.70 meters from the basket, while FIBA's is 6.25 meters. Rules: - NBA has 7 timeouts per game; FIBA has 4. - NBA timeout duration is 1 minute 40 seconds; FIBA is 1 minute. - NBA personal foul limit is 6 fouls; FIBA is 5. - NBA on-court referees are 3 people; FIBA usually has 2. There are many more differences that I won't list one by one. Remember one point: Many NBA rules serve its commercial nature (advertisers). 6. How are the Eastern and Western conferences divided? Is it possible for both teams in the finals to come from the same conference (East or West)? The US is divided into East and West conferences, each with 15 teams. The Western Conference is divided into Northwest, Southwest, and Pacific divisions; the Eastern Conference is divided into Southeast, Central, and Atlantic divisions. Western three divisions: Northwest: Jazz, Nuggets, Trail Blazers, Timberwolves, Thunder; Southwest: Mavericks, Spurs, Rockets, Pelicans, Grizzlies; Pacific: Suns, Lakers, Warriors, Clippers, Kings. Eastern three divisions: Southeast: Heat, Wizards, Magic, Hornets, Hawks; Central: Pistons, Cavaliers, Bulls, Pacers, Bucks; Atlantic: Raptors, Nets, 76ers, Knicks, Celtics. The finals is played between the division champions of the Eastern and Western conferences, so it cannot be two teams from the same conference. What is the difference between the regular season and the playoffs? The regular season involves every team in the league. Teams within the same conference and division play each other home-and-home (two home games, two away games), totaling four matches. Teams from different conferences play one home game and one away game against each other, for a total of two matches. Teams from the same conference but different divisions play between 3 to 4 games depending on the team schedule, ensuring each team plays exactly 82 regular season games in total. After the regular season concludes, the top eight teams from each conference advance to the playoffs. The first-place team from each division automatically qualifies for the top three playoff seeds. The playoffs use a best-of-seven format with a 2-2-1-1-1 home court allocation system. The team with four home games hosts Games 1, 2, and (if necessary) Games 5 and 7, while traveling to opponents' arenas for Games 3, 4, and (if necessary) Game 6. Home court advantage is not evenly distributed between the two teams. The playoffs include the Finals in four rounds. The second round is called the Conference Semifinals, the third round is the Conference Finals. Winners of these rounds become Conference Champions. The Eastern and Western Conference champions advance to the NBA Finals, where the first team to win four games becomes that season's champion. The most outstanding player on the championship team receives the Finals MVP award. In summary, all teams participate in the regular season, but only the top eight teams from each conference can advance to the playoffs. One team from each conference will compete in the NBA Finals.
