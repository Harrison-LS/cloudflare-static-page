Influencer Walls When thinking of Los Angeles, any image in your mind will definitely include Paul Smith's pink wall. Even though it's been posted countless times already, it remains the original influencer wall and has become one of LA's most famous landmarks. Whether you've visited or not, please accept this list of Los Angeles influencer walls first. The Angel Wings Address: Melrose Ave & N Fairfax Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90046 Image credit to @***, copyright belongs to the original author LA's second most famous Instagrammable wall after the Pink Wall, this is the world's first famous angel wings wall. This was created by Colette Miller starting in 2012 as a "Global Angel Wings Charity Project", aiming to tell everyone that each person is an angel with broken wings from their past life, hoping people will cherish the present moment and those around them. Now 38 cities worldwide have obtained angel wing patterns. Urban Light Address: 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Los Angeles, CA 90036 Images from Instagram, copyright belongs to the original author The Urban Light installation is located in front of LACMA (Los Angeles County Museum of Art), where you can see various photographers among rows of light gray Roman-style lamp posts. The intricate background offers plenty of photo inspiration. You can visit either when morning sunlight hits or when evening lights illuminate the posts for different vibes. Images from @kimlephotography, copyright belongs to the original author the Broad Museum Address: 221 S Grand Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90012 Images from wacowla, copyright belongs to the original author The Broad Museum is now one of Los Angeles' most famous museums. Established by philanthropists and art collectors Eli Broad and Edythe Broad, this museum cost 140 million USD to build, featuring a collection and exhibition of top-tier modern artworks. I believe everyone will be familiar with these artistic elements, but when you personally visit the museum to see the originals, you'll even be shocked by these extraordinary creations. The most famous artwork is undoubtedly Yayoi Kusama's Infinity Mirror Room. After entering the museum, you need to queue separately for this exhibit. Each visitor only has a short 45 seconds to appreciate this work~ Image from @anniewearsit, copyright belongs to the original author Image from twitter, copyright belongs to the original author Since the museum's popularity is extremely high now, there are always long lines at the entrance. Friends who don't want to queue should remember to make a reservation on the official website at least one month in advance, saving you quite some time~ the Bradburry Building Address: 304 S Broadway, Los Angeles, CA 90013 Image from @la.curbed, copyright belongs to the original author Image from ***, copyright belongs to the original author When wandering around DTLA and passing by this building named Bradburry Building, why not stop and step inside to explore? You'll discover what it means to be extraordinary. Built in 1893, it is not only Los Angeles' downtown oldest commercial building but also listed on the National Historic Landmarks Register. Victorian-style vintage atrium design, wrought iron railings, cage elevators - scenes you would only see in retro movies come into view, feeling like traveling through time. The first floor public area is open for tourists to visit; upper floors remain as office spaces. Visitors who come can quietly take photos, that's perfectly fine. Image from @CLUTCH Magazine, copyright belongs to the original author Angels Flight Railway Angel's Path Mini Train Address: 350 S Grand Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90071 Image from @aglobewelltravelled, copyright belongs to the original author The Angels Flight tram that became famous because of the movie "La La Land" - the railway is 99 meters long and it's the world's shortest mountain railway. Built in 1901, originally intended to allow wealthy people to avoid the trouble of climbing up. The train once went into a stop state, until the movie became popular, it was then refurbished and put back into use. Now it's an internet-famous tourist attraction in downtown Los Angeles. Riding the tram train once requires paying $1 USD, remember to come with the person you love to experience this movie-like romance together. Nearby Recommendations If you have ample time, consider exploring Los Angeles' surrounding areas. I'd like to recommend some attractions that can be visited and returned from within a single day. MALIBU WINE SAFARIS Address: 32111 Mulholland Hwy, Malibu, CA 90265 Image credit: @Pinterest, copyright belongs to the original author This winery in Malibu operates as a small zoo where you can enjoy wine tasting while feeding animals on the farm - alpacas, zebras, and more. You might even encounter Hollywood celebrity Stanley the giraffe, who became famous after starring in "The Hangover Part III." Visiting the winery tour requires advance reservation. The visit duration depends on the tour you choose, ranging from one to two hours, with prices between $70 and over $100. Potato Chip Rock (薯片岩) Address: Ramona, CA 92065 Image courtesy of @kelleylouise. All rights reserved by the original creator. Famous for its resemblance to a potato chip, combined with unique photographic angles that make you feel as if you're standing on a cliff edge. To reach Potato Chip Rock requires hiking 3-4 hours, so those planning this trip should adjust their condition and equipment accordingly. Please do not attempt in high heels! Newport Beach Image courtesy of @timeout. All rights reserved by the original creator. Those who enjoy going to sea may consider joining a whale-watching tour at Newport Beach. Of course, whether you see whales depends on the season and your luck. Newport Beach itself is also a prosperous small harbor, featuring various yacht excursions and large shopping centers. Mission Inn Hotel & SPA Address: 3649 Mission Inn Avenue, Riverside, CA 92501 Image courtesy of @amenagementdesign, all rights reserved to the original author The Mission Inn with its distinct Spanish ambiance is about an hour's drive from Los Angeles. Entering this hotel feels like being in a secluded paradise; you can leisurely stroll around for hours. I strongly recommend staying here overnight so you'll have ample time to take beautiful photos!
