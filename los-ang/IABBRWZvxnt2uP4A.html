I. How much do you know about the NBA? In 1896, the first basketball organization in America - the "National Basketball League" (abbreviated as NBL) was officially founded. At that time, basketball was a newly emerging sport with imperfect game rules, underdeveloped organizational structure, and lacking systematic and effective management and operations. Within just a few years, the NBL existed only in name. In 1946, eleven ice hockey arenas and stadiums jointly initiated and established the "Basketball Association of America" (abbreviated as BAA), proposed by Walter Brown, owner of Boston Garden. In 1949, under Brown's efforts, the two major American basketball organizations - BAA and NBL - merged to form the National Basketball Association (NBA). Developing and growing over subsequent decades, in 1976 with the addition of Dallas Mavericks, Charlotte Hornets (New Orleans Pelicans), Miami Heat, Toronto Raptors from Canada, and Charlotte Bobcats (Charlotte Hornets) teams, the number of franchises expanded to thirty. As mentioned in NBA's slogan - where amazing happens. In just a few decades, countless incredible records were born: Wilt Chamberlain's 100 points in one game; Bill Russell's 11 championship titles; Kareem Abdul-Jabbar's career total scoring record with the NBA regular season at 38,387 points; Tracy McGrady's 13 points in 35 seconds; Stephen Curry making 402 three-pointers in a single season. Amazing performances continue to unfold and records keep being broken - and you very well might become a witness to the next miracle. How is the schedule divided? The entire league has a total of 30 teams, with 15 in each conference. Every team plays 82 games during the regular season. The regular season runs from late October each year until late April of the following year, resulting in over 1,200 games played by all 30 teams throughout a season. Unless players go on strike, game dates are not easily changed. The playoffs and Finals run from late April to mid-to-late June. The championship is contested between the Eastern Conference champion and the Western Conference champion. Games are decided in a best-of-seven series with a format of 2-2-1-1-1. Teams with better winning percentages during the regular season secure home court advantage, while the first team to win four games claims the Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy. Members of the championship team each receive an NBA Championship ring. II. The competition spans three days. 2.1 Three-Point Contest The contest is usually held on the day before the All-Star weekend's main event. Almost all participating players are among the league's top "elite shooters." The game consists of two rounds using an elimination format. Each player shoots 30 balls, including 5 money balls worth 2 points each; regular balls are worth 1 point each. The player with the highest score wins. Three teams with the highest scores after Round 1 advance to the finals. The format of Round 2 is identical to that of Round 1. In case of tied scores in the finals, additional games will be played until rankings are determined. 2.2 Skills Challenge The Skills Challenge primarily showcases the fundamental skills and techniques of the best point guards in the league. Players must complete dribbling, passing, obstacle navigation, shooting, and other segments within a specified time limit; victory is decided by who finishes fastest. Legendary ball-handling guards such as Nash, Kidd, Williams, Parker, and Rose have all won championships in this event. 2.3 Slam Dunk Contest The annual Slam Dunk Contest has been a "feast" during All-Star Weekend each year. Started in 1984, it remains the highlight of NBA All-Star Weekend throughout its history. The competition consists of two rounds. In the first round, no one dunks more than three times; the top three players by total score advance to the finals. Judges are comprised of current or retired legends and past dunking champions. During the final stage, each player performs twice, with the highest scorer crowned champion. In 2015, Timberwolves' Zach LaVine decisively won the championship relying on four spectacular dunks. In 2016, "legendary dunker" Zach LaVine faced Aaron Gordon of Orlando Magic in the finals and successfully defended his title after two overtime periods. Who do you think will win the 2017 Slam Dunk Championship title? 2.4 Rookie Challenge The NBA Rookie Challenge is one of the important games at the NBA All-Star Weekend, with players from both teams mainly consisting of first and second-year rookies. The NBA coaching staff selects the top nine rookie and sophomore players for this season each January, forming a Rookies team and Sophomores team. The assistant coaches of teams with the best records from the East and West divisions serve as temporary head coaches respectively. Sometimes NBA star players are also invited to serve as honorary managers to add more media attention. After 2012, game participants changed—two legendary former players now select their preferred young rookies and sophomores for each team. 2.5 All-Star Game The NBA All-Star Game typically takes place around mid-February each year. The top players from both the Eastern and Western conferences form two competing teams annually. Each team fields 12 players: five starters are selected through global fan voting on the NBA's official website, while seven reserves are chosen by their respective head coaches. The game prioritizes entertainment value, with referees applying lenient rules. This creates a more relaxed competitive environment where winning isn't the sole focus. Despite this casual atmosphere, NBA officials enforce specific expectations—during critical moments, players and coaches must genuinely compete with real intensity and effort. 2016, the Western Conference All-Star team defeated the Eastern Conference 196-173, with a combined total of 369 points, breaking the all-time scoring record for regular All-Star games.
