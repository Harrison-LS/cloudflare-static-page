Los Angeles | Hollywood Stories When discussing American cinema, what immediately comes to mind is undoubtedly Hollywood, famous worldwide. Since the early 20th century, this area in Los Angeles' suburbs has produced countless unforgettable classic films and launched numerous talented actors into stardom. La La Land Griffith Observatory Located on a hilltop in northwest Los Angeles, the Griffith Observatory (Griffith Observatory) offers the best vantage point for capturing both the Hollywood sign and Los Angeles city skyline. It was also a classic filming location for "La La Land," which received an Oscar Best Picture nomination. The observatory features numerous celestial models, and the dome theater on the top floor uses 3D naked-eye technology to let visitors appreciate the beauty of the universe in 360°, learning about the entire process of celestial evolution. In addition, the Griffith Observatory is equipped with four giant telescopes that are open free to the public every night at 19:00. Whether you climb to the dome with your partner to admire the starry sky above or dance like the movie's lead couple, it will surely be an unforgettable night during your trip. More Movies Beyond the most famous La La Land, numerous sci-fi action blockbusters including GoldenEye, Charlie's Angels: Full Circle, Transformers, and Gangs of New York were also filmed at Griffith Observatory. Same City Same Movie Enter downtown Los Angeles, spend $1 to visit the century-old Angels Flight Railway, watch the sunset over the Pacific at Hermosa Beach at dusk, then enjoy a delicious dinner at Lighthouse Cafe nearby where the movie couple separated... everything looks just like in the film. Mulholland Drive Mulholland Avenue If you're a fan of suspense films, then you must have heard of David Lynch's classic horror-suspense film "Mulholland Drive." The story of the two female protagonists revolves around this Mulholland Drive leading to Hollywood. Mulholland Drive is built on the ridge of the Santa Monica Mountains near West Hollywood. Named after famous engineer William Mulholland, it is considered one of Los Angeles's most famous roads alongside Sunset Boulevard. The drive stretches 50 miles in total, running west from Hollywood to California's renowned Malibu Beach. The mountain scenery along the way is exceptionally beautiful, and it traverses affluent neighborhoods of Los Angeles, making it a top choice for road trips. **Same City, Same Movie** Besides the well-known Mulholland Drive, other locations beloved by European and American celebrities also appeared in classic scenes from "Mulholland Drive." These include Runyon Canyon Loop (a hiking paradise), Paramount Pictures, Inc. (one of Hollywood's eight major studios), and Sunset Boulevard, which frequently appears in movies and musicals. **Discover Surroundings** If you have already driven to Mulholland Drive, another quick push on the accelerator takes you to the Getty Center, America's art palace. The original Hollywood movie studio is now Universal Studios Hollywood... There are many tourist attractions along both sides of Mulholland Drive that visitors cannot miss. Boston | Where America Reveals Its Details Boston is one of the oldest cities in America, housing over 100 universities including prestigious institutions like Harvard and MIT within an area less than one-third the size of Shanghai's urban core. This has made Boston a popular filming location for numerous films depicting American life. Good Will Hunting Massachusetts Institute of Technology The film that launched famous actor Matt Damon into stardom, Good Will Hunting, tells the story of Boston. Filmed in multiple locations throughout the city, MIT stands out as the most recognizable site and also serves as where the narrative begins. MIT is a comprehensive private university with an outstanding reputation in natural sciences and engineering. Consistently ranked among the top five universities worldwide, it is a world-renowned institution that should not be underestimated. In contrast to Harvard's historic charm, MIT possesses a unique energy characteristic of science and technology fields. The campus features iconic landmarks such as the dome auditorium and Frank Gehry-designed "quirky architecture"... More than just an educational institution, MIT is a modern sanctuary brimming with imagination. Same City, Same Story Beyond MIT, Boston's Public Garden also served as a primary filming location for Good Will Hunting. Whether witnessing the deep friendship between Will and Sean in the film, or paying tribute to the late Hollywood star Robin Williams who passed away years ago, Boston Public Garden is a destination worth visiting for film enthusiasts. More Movies Don't know how many people remember the duck sculpture that quickly appeared in "Ted", and the scene where John and Teddy Bear sit on a bench chatting - these scenes were all filmed at Boston Public Garden. Atlanta | The Superhero City When you think of Atlanta, you might associate it with the Olympics or Coca-Cola. But you would never guess that Atlanta is actually a superhero city beloved by sci-fi films. Avengers: Infinity War Piedmont Park Many Marvel fans are undoubtedly familiar with the New York street battle scene from the opening of "Avengers: Age of Ultron." However, that sequence was actually filmed not in New York, but at Piedmont Park in Atlanta. Located in downtown, Piedmont Park serves as an urban oasis for Atlanta. With its expansive 189-acre footprint, it has become the city's center for sports and entertainment. Beyond jogging paths and lawns suitable for running, the park features small lakes designed specifically for fishing enthusiasts. The beautiful natural scenery complements perfectly with the skyscrapers of downtown, making it a popular recreational spot for locals. The City of Super Heroes Atlanta is one of Marvel Studios' favorite filming locations. Pinewood Atlanta Studios, which has produced many Marvel films, and Fairlie-Poplar Historic District have frequently "masqueraded" as other cities in movies like "Ant-Man" and "Spider-Man." The district are both essential stops on any superhero city tour. Discover Surroundings Beyond paying tribute to superheroes, there are many must-see attractions when you come to Atlanta. Visit the World of Coca-Cola to learn about the drink you've been consuming since childhood, or visit CNN Studios to try your hand at being an "American TV personality" and experience the diverse Atlanta that begins here. Practical Information Piedmont Park How to Get There: Take bus 99 and get off at the 10th St Charles Allen Dr. stop to reach Piedmont Park Opening Hours: 6:00-23:00 Piedmont Park Located at one end of the completed Eastside trail, this park boasts nearly 200 acres of green space. This prime central location has become an ideal spot for both Atlanta residents and tourists to picnic, play games, walk their dogs, and relax. The park hosts various major city events, including April's Dogwood Festival and September's Music Midtown festival. Additionally, it serves as the finish line for the 10km Peachtree Road Race, held annually to commemorate Independence Day. The Hunger Games Atlanta History Center The Atlanta History Center is located in the Buckhead District in northern Atlanta, an area featuring numerous historic buildings and museums. Among them, the Swan House is most famous as the filming location for the presidential residence in The Hunger Games movie. This European-style villa with nearly a century of history is one of Philadelphia Trammell Shutze's most renowned works. Once a private residence many years ago, it now belongs to the History Center and operates as an architecture museum. The interior still preserves the luxurious American Southern decorating style from the 1920s-30s. In addition, the Smith Family Farm and Atlanta History Museum within the center are also highly worth visiting. Tourists can learn about different historical periods in the southern United States through numerous historic sites, ranging from slavery through the Civil War to the Atlanta Olympics era. "The Hunger Games" thrilling scenes were not only shot at the History Center but also filmed at the four-star Marriott Marquis Hotel located in downtown Atlanta (Atlanta Marriott Marquis) and at Road Atlanta, a prestigious venue in the racing world. Seattle | Where to Meet Love On a film tour in America, Seattle is an absolutely must-visit destination. Walking through this city where fortune and romance coexist, love may be hidden in coffee shops around street corners. "Sleepless in Seattle" Lake Union Park I have to say that many people first knew this city through the classic film "Sleepless in Seattle." One of the most frequently featured filming locations was boat house number 2460 on Lake Union where main character Tom Hanks lived. Lake Union spans across Seattle, dividing the city into northern and southern parts. Besides the azure waters, Lake Union Park features expansive lawns and walking paths, making it one of the preferred destinations for locals to enjoy outdoor picnics and recreation. The male protagonist's home from the movie is situated among the villas on the west shore of the lake. Although this villa area currently consists of private residences not open for public tours, dining at a lakeside restaurant while savoring seafood, admiring the sunset on the horizon, and gazing at distant houseboats makes for an excellent experience! Same City, Same Story Besides the boat house frequently visited by the male protagonist, The Arctic Club where the main characters attended their New Year's party and made promises to meet in New York, as well as Alki Beach Park where the couple enjoyed playing with their child, are all iconic filming locations from the film. Discover Surroundings Lake Union Park is located in a bustling downtown area. The south shore is adjacent to Seattle Center (home of Seattle's Space Needle) and Bruce Lee's Grave Site—a must-visit spot for movie enthusiasts—while the north shore is home to the renowned University of Washington. All are attractions worth exploring for visitors. Practical Information Lake Union Park 📍Address: 860 Terry Ave N, Seattle ⏱Open Hours: 4:00-23:30 Lake Union Park The boathouse featured in "Midnight in Seattle" is located within Lake Union Park, north of downtown Seattle. This area is famous for yacht activities, and restaurants around the lake primarily feature seafood. Lake Union spans across Seattle City, dividing it into upper and lower sections. On the south side of the lake are familiar tourist attractions such as the Space Needle, downtown, and waterfront areas. Directly north of Lake Union are Fremont District (FREMONT) and the University District. The park has walking paths suitable for outdoor activities. "Twilight" Olympic National Park Love is not only hidden in Seattle's city corners; the Olympic National Park on the western outskirts is also a sacred place where you might meet your other half, and it was here that the romance between humans and vampires unfolded in the film "Twilight." The Olympic National Park is America's largest pristine area outside Alaska and features the most diverse landscapes among many national parks in the United States. Here is a 100-kilometer stretch of coastline, towering snow-capped mountains rising among lush green trees, hot springs to relax in while hiking, and the temperate rainforest where Bella and Edward found their forever love. More Movies The temperate rainforest within the park isn't just home to vampires in Twilight; vast mosses and coniferous forests, delicate waterfalls and streams also served as forest prototypes for fantasy films like Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings. Discover Surroundings On your drive from downtown Seattle to Olympic National Park, you'll pass through Olympia, the capital city of Washington state (Thurston). Visiting the State Capitol building, nicknamed "The White House," and viewing rare animals at Nisqually National Wildlife Refuge are experiences not to be missed.
