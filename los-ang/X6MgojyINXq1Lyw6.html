Los Angeles | Mountain-Top RV Homestay, Next Door to Hollywood Stars

Los Angeles

Brief

On a hilltop in Los Angeles covering 160,000 square meters lies a homestay converted from a vintage RV. Guests can camp at the summit, enjoy sunsets, gaze at the starry sky, and even overlook Nicolas Cage's home! The owners are a Hollywood celebrity couple who have hosted more than 7,000 guests over the past five years, including many celebrities.

This RV B&B, where neighbors are all big stars The owners of this Airbnb (AIRSTREAM VINTAGE TRAILER ADVENTURE) are a magical couple. Near a mountain in Los Angeles area, close to Nicolas Cage's private residence, lives a truly remarkable couple. His wife is an independent Hollywood producer, whose reality show "Dog Whisperer" ran for 9 seasons over 8 years, broadcast across 200 countries worldwide. In the US alone, every week 10 million viewers tuned in to watch the drama, and it even received an Emmy Award nomination. Her husband has worked as a mechanic, stage designer, and 6 other professions, building houses, modifying RVs, raising horses, keeping bees... In 2012, to celebrate their 27th wedding anniversary, the couple moved the retro RV up to their mountain home, drinking red wine, enjoying lavish meals, watching sunrises... Even more romantic than young people! After that still felt unsatisfying, they left the RV permanently on the mountain top, transforming it into an RV B&B. Unexpectedly, it became incredibly popular. Because this place is filled with love memories, many male guests choose to propose here, with an extraordinarily high success rate! They opened just 2 of their vehicles as rental rooms, only 2 rooms total, accommodating a maximum of 4 people, guests have to cook for themselves and conserve water and electricity. Yet in 5 years they hosted 7000 guests, almost always fully booked every day. To secure a room, you need to reserve at least 2 months in advance! After the Los Angeles Times editor stayed there secretly, he wrote an article featuring Murray's RV B&B, listing it as one of the top 7 places worth visiting in Los Angeles. After producer Ricky from "The Simpsons" stayed and left a thank you note in the guest book... Before they married, Murray and Kay were close friends for 10 years. Their friendship only turned into love after she divorced. In 1994, the couple purchased a 160,000 square meter mountain plot located just 45 minutes' drive from Los Angeles. Murray built his own house and brought along the vintage RV he loved. Five years ago in October marked their 27th wedding anniversary. Murray wanted to surprise his wife but couldn't find an appropriate place online after searching extensively. He then realized that a wonderful spot existed right on their property. Their home features a mountaintop platform offering 360-degree views of the beautiful Santa Monica mountains, the Pacific Ocean in the distance, and even Nicolas Cage's private residence, faintly visible across the bay. Keeping his plan secret from his wife, Murray quietly moved the RV to the summit, set up an umbrella for shade in front of it, arranged lounge chairs with red wine nearby. They spent their 27th wedding anniversary together there. One of Los Angeles' Top 7 Most Recommended Destinations After this romantic anniversary celebration, Murray didn't rush to move the RV down from the mountaintop. He thought: why not put this cool 1957 vintage RV on Airbnb and let more people experience this unique landscape that's one-of-a-kind worldwide? That was during the cold winter in California, USA. Murray originally thought people wouldn't be interested in an outdoor homestay, but just a few days later, guests started booking... The RV was fully equipped; guests could cook their own meals inside. Careful Murray even prepared board games for children, so a family of three would never get bored staying here. However, maintaining the RV was quite hard work. Murray and Kay didn't hire employees—they did everything themselves. They personally carried water up from below the mountain, and after guests left, they had to clean the bathrooms too. The RV ran on solar power, and guests generally were very conscientious about saving water and electricity. Over 5 years, the Murrays hosted a total of 7,000 guests. The RV was almost full every day—the couple was so busy they were jumping around! But despite their workload, they never complained; they genuinely enjoyed meeting guests from all over the world. After an editor from the Los Angeles Times stayed there secretly and wrote an article, they ranked Murrays' RV homestay as one of the top 7 places to visit in Los Angeles. After Ricky, a main producer on The Simpsons, stayed there, he wrote a thank-you note in their guestbook and even drew a small portrait of Simpson. Murray loved it very much and would take it out whenever he had a moment to show it off. There are countless male guests who want to propose to their girlfriends in front of Murray's RV. Once, a couple asked Murray to take a commemorative photo of them. As Murray was halfway through the shot, people suddenly "disappeared" from the frame. It turned out that gentleman actually got down on one knee and started proposing! One day, Kay encountered a fan: A girl came alone to stay at the RV rental. She looked upset, so Kay chatted with her. She found out that her mother had just passed away. She said that she used to sit on the sofa with her mom watching "The Dog Whisperer." Kay replied, "I'm actually the producer of this show!" The girl was both happy and moved, feeling it must have been her mother's mysterious arrangement in fate! Beyond managing the RV rental, Murray cares for over a dozen small animals. They have 2 dogs, 2 cats, 1 parrot, 2 horses, plus various chicks—all rescued by Murray. Occasionally, Murray also tends to beehives. The local climate produces some of the world's finest honey. Bored, he self-taught beekeeping and achieved moderate success. Last winter alone, he harvested 56 kilograms of honey, which he gave as Christmas gifts to friends and family—very popular! We asked Murray: running a rental property, feeding horses, maintaining beehives... is life this tiring? Have you ever thought about retirement? He says he's not tired at all, and hasn't even considered retiring. Americans generally retire at 65, while Murray is already 71 this year, and Kay is a few years older than her husband. Murray and Kay As long as his health permits, Murray wants to keep running the RV accommodation forever, allowing more people who love RVs to have the opportunity to stay there. But when it comes to not being able to often accompany his wife on trips due to being busy, Murray still feels a bit guilty. However recently, he's been busy repairing his own RV, and plans to take Kay to Mexico for a trip around Christmas this year, making up for the regrets!
