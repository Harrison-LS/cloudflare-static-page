"Superman" movie filming location - City Hall, image from internet Los Angeles City Hall This is the office location of the Los Angeles city government. Completed in 1928, City Hall was the tallest building on the West Coast at that time and held this record for over two decades. The main construction materials of the building were all sourced from local California soil and water sources. The tower design at the top emulates the Mausoleum, one of the ancient world's Seven Wonders, making this civic building unique. It has become a filming location for numerous Hollywood movies, including the household-name film "Superman." Unlike government buildings in other places that are heavily guarded and difficult to approach, Los Angeles City Hall is much more accessible. The spherical rotunda on the ground floor along with some office areas and meeting rooms are free for public access. You can even visit the state government press conference venue, of course all under staff supervision. If you're interested in the American political system, want to understand how a state government operates, or simply wish to tour inside a government building, Los Angeles City Hall is not to be missed. Bradbury: One of Los Angeles' landmark buildings. The entire building radiates Italian Renaissance style from inside out. Transparent lighting allows indoor light and shadows to blend seamlessly with the steel architecture, allowing visitors to appreciate the building's exquisite craftsmanship within limited space. Within this limited space, you can appreciate the exquisite details of the entire building: beautiful courtyards, elegant dark green wrought-iron stairs, stained glass, marble floors, and narrow antique elevators.
