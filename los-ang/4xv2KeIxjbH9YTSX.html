Which Los Angeles Michelin restaurants do you know?

As a food lover, I enjoy everything from humble street snacks to fine dining delicacies. Today, let me share some Michelin restaurants in Los Angeles - are you ready? Los Angeles Michelin Restaurant Recommendations Urasawa | Masa on the East Coast, Urasawa on the West Coast Urasawa - 218 N Rodeo Dr Beverly Hills CA 90210 USA Phone: (310) 247-8939 Masa on the East Coast, Urasawa on the West Coast. Everyone knows how highly Japanese cuisine is regarded in America. The transcendent dining experience, exquisite presentation and knife skills leave you feeling that spending any amount on this meal is absolutely worth it. If you are seeking an unforgettable lifetime dining experience, bring your special someone here. Reservations recommended - use your taste buds to savor every dish, as photography is not permitted. Mélisse | Savoring Seasonal Creative Cuisine Melisse Restaurant - 1104 Wilshire Blvd Santa Monica CA 90401 USA Phone: (310) 395-0881 Josiah Citrin's French restaurant has been famous in Santa Monica for over a decade. How many romantic stories have unfolded within this purple restaurant. Though French in style, this restaurant incorporates many Californian elements. Its greatest highlight is its seasonally curated menu that delivers high-end dishes. You can savor Santa Barbara sea urchin and shrimp, Italian pasta with lobster sauce, perfectly cured aged beef, and various memorable simple appetizers while enjoying quality service and exquisite cuisine by the seaside in Santa Monica. Reservations are highly recommended. Providence | SoCal #1 Seafood Restaurant Providence - 5955 Melrose Ave Los Angeles CA 90038 USA Phone: (323) 460-4170 Even though the Michelin rating system no longer exists, Providence remains firmly established as Southern California's premier seafood restaurant. Under chef and owner Michael Cimarusti's leadership, it has become one of the most significant restaurants in America. Every kind of Southern California wild fish can be skillfully prepared into dishes with authentic Los Angeles flavor. If you visit Los Angeles, you should not miss this place. Reservations are absolutely necessary. Spago | The Divine Realm of a Culinary Master Spago - 176 N Canon Dr Beverly Hills CA 90210 USA Phone: (310) 385-0880 Wolfgang Puck, who has been responsible for the Oscars dinner parties for decades and whose fame in Los Angeles is unquestionable, opened Spago in Beverly Hills years ago. It has long been a beloved favorite among locals. Every evening here features lively conviviality with abundant drinks. Though he no longer personally handles the dishes, he still frequently walks around the restaurant to observe guests' reactions. This is an excellent place to experience traditional modern American cuisine and pan-Asian cuisine. The upscale decor instantly imparts a sense of elegant luxury. I recommend making reservations; Walk In is only suggested if you're simply looking to step inside for a cocktail. Asanebo - Must-Try Sea Urchin Asanebo - 11941 Ventura Blvd Studio City CA 91604 USA Phone: (818) 760-3348 Even though ownership has changed, the new owner was an apprentice from those years, ensuring perfect inheritance of skills. The sea urchin remains in its best condition; handmade tofu is unforgettable. Steaming hot egg custard conceals sea urchin inside; conch soaks in miso soup; they select the freshest seafood catch. The menu changes according to season, with perfect combination of sauces and spices that deliver the original taste of ingredients intact. Cut - Refined and Delicate Meat Flavor Cut - 9500 Wilshire Blvd Beverly Hills CA 90212 USA Phone: (310) 276-8500 Are American steaks only bold and rugged? Actually they can also be refined and delicate; CUT in Beverly Hills exemplifies this. Wolfgang Pucks ingredients are truly top-tier. Here you can find Angus steaks from the United States and Australia, Japanese Wagyu beef, and most authentically, genuine Hyogo A5 Kobe steak grilled over hardwood and charcoal. No meat lover can resist its charm. Dont worry about nutritional imbalance - there are plenty of fresh vegetables and creative sides on the menu. And dont worry about the decor or wine cellar; nothing but breathtaking can describe it. Mori Sushi｜Hall of Hand-Pressed Sushi Mori Sushi - 11500 W Pico Blvd Los Angeles CA 90064 USA Phone: (310) 479-3939 Hand-pressed sushi represents the ultimate challenge for Japanese restaurants, whether it is the rice vinegar balance, fish quality, house-made soy sauce, or presentation. This may be paired with custom ceramics and elegant decor, or specially contacted rice suppliers, making you understand deeply that details determine success. Although there have been changes in ownership, the successor has seemingly maintained the proper standards. Patina｜Unparalleled Customer Experience Patina Restaurant - Walt Disney Concert Hall 141 South Grand Ave Los Angeles CA 90012 USA Phone: (213) 972-3331 The impeccable service alone is worth earning a star rating. This culinary group gives you complete confidence in its heritage. A master chef hired at great expense also instills confidence in the dishes. Being located near Walt Disney Concert Hall ensures it remains at the pinnacle of dining excellence. Valentino | The Origin of Italian Cuisine Valentino - 3115 Pico Blvd, Santa Monica, CA 90405, USA Phone: (310) 829-4313 An Italian restaurant that has been popular in Los Angeles for decades, truly a veteran establishment. It even sparked the trend of eating Italian cuisine in Los Angeles. Although the menu may seem somewhat old-fashioned now by today's standards, it remains highly recommended for those who appreciate traditional flavors. Don't forget to try their extensive selection of aged wines. Water Grill | Fresh Seafood Cold Dishes Water Grill - 544 S. Grand Avenue, Los Angeles, CA 90071, USA Enjoy delicious seafood cold dishes, perfectly prepared fish steaks, and the absolutely must-try bread pudding. This is the ideal place for friends' gatherings and family dinners. As a restaurant in the heart of downtown with iconic status, it definitely deserves this honor. Osteria Mozza | Perfect Fusion of Italian Cuisine and Baked Goods Osteria Mozza - 6602 Melrose Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90038, USA Phone: (323) 297-0100 Italian cuisine is truly loved by all, but finding a restaurant that excels at both pasta and pizza is not easy. Famous Italian chef Mario Batali, who frequently appears on TV, traveled around Bologna and Italy to explore local food culture and brought each region's essence into his cooking. Combined with Nancy Silverton, a renowned baker from Los Angeles, their strong partnership made Osteria Mozza an acclaimed Italian restaurant that excels in both areas. Sushi Zo | Hand Sushi Sushi Zo - 9824 National Blvd Los Angeles CA 90064 USA Phone: (310) 842-3977 If your budget is under $500 but you still want to enjoy hand sushi, at Sushi Zo you can get high-quality omakase for about $100-200. Small portions, fresh seasonal seafood, house-made soy sauce, and carefully selected ingredients - from yuzu juice, sea salt, perilla leaves, wasabi - each perfectly paired with the fish.
