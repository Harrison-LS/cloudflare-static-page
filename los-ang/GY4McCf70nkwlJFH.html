You must know these 8 Q&A about US visas! The application process for travel/business visas to the United States can be roughly divided into three stages: materials preparation, online application, and consular interview. Regardless of which stage, successfully obtaining a visa ultimately follows only one principle: whether the applicant will return normally to their home country. What's the difference between 5-year and 10-year US visas? Currently, Chinese citizens applying for business or tourism (B1/B2) to the United States can apply for a 10-year multiple-entry visa, while students can apply for a 5-year multiple-entry visa. Is a B1/B2 visa always valid for 10 years if applied? Not everyone is eligible for a ten-year validity visa. Visa officers will make judgments based on each person's individual circumstances, but generally they do grant 10 years. Does a 10-year validity mean you can stay in the United States for ten years? No! A ten-year validity visa means you can enter the United States repeatedly over the course of ten years. How long you may stay during each visit is determined by immigration officers at the port of entry. I've heard single people are more likely to be rejected? Hmm… Especially for single women, it's true that you need to prepare more thoroughly than others, but it absolutely doesn't mean rejection is guaranteed! As long as you can convince the embassy interviewer that you have no immigrant intent and will return home, you'll pass with confidence~ Will poor English really lead to rejection? No! Interviewers are fluent in both English and Chinese, and they actively greet applicants and ask questions in Chinese, so there's no need to worry too much. However, mastering some basic phrases would be more beneficial for your success~ How can you improve visa application approval rates? The key to a successful visa lies in: convincing the embassy that you will definitely not overstay illegally and will surely return home. Actually, all preparation materials are intended to prove this point, so you must clearly understand your objective and prepare all visa documentation around that goal. How is a US visa processed? You can apply yourself or entrust a travel agency to handle it. Materials needed for appointment and interview? It is recommended to prepare all materials in advance. Preparation of materials falls into two parts: essential materials at application time, and supporting documents at the interview. How to determine your own approval rate? Before officially starting the application process, what people care about most is definitely how high their success rate for obtaining a US visa is, whether it's worth trying. Possible Outcomes of a U.S. Visa Interview 1) Immediate Approval: Receive a receipt on the spot and leave your passport behind, indicating successful visa approval. After approval, you may collect your passport at the designated CITIC Bank branch. 2) On-site Check: During the interview, if the consular officer has questions about your submitted materials, they may conduct phone verification immediately. If verification proceeds smoothly, you can receive approval on the spot. Therefore, on the day of your interview, ensure all phone numbers provided in your parents' employer information or any other contact details are verified and will be answered correctly. The person answering should provide information consistent with what is shown in your materials to guarantee a flawless investigation on your interview day. 3) Additional Materials: In very rare cases where the consular officer finds some of your submitted materials insufficient but does not immediately deny your application, they may request you to submit additional required documents within a specified timeframe for reconsideration regarding visa issuance. You must strive to complete and submit these supplementary materials to the embassy according to their requirements within this deadline, which is typically by the end of that business day. 4) Denial: After denial, do not argue persistently. Ask politely, express gratitude, and depart gracefully. Never lose your composure or speak in anger, as you must make a good transition for future visa applications. You may even politely inform the consular officer: "I will be back next time." What Exactly Is the EVUS System? Starting November 1, 2016, Chinese citizens holding US ten-year validity B1 or B2 visas must register with and pay fees to the "Electronic Visa Update System" (EVUS) before being permitted entry into the United States. Each registration remains valid for two years. If either your visa or passport expires, the EVUS registration information becomes invalid, requiring visa holders to log in again through EVUS prior to traveling to update their personal information. How to use EVUS? Each EVUS registration must include the visa holder's name, date of birth, passport details, other personal information, and employment information. The website is available in both English and Chinese versions, but all ten-year visa holders must complete the EVUS forms in English. Friends, relatives, travel industry professionals, or other third parties may submit the required information on behalf of travelers to EVUS. To date, the system remains in development. After November 1, 2016, applicants will need to pay a nominal fee for system maintenance. Specific fee details will be provided before the system officially launches. This means that even with a valid U.S. ten-year multiple-entry visa, travel is no longer as straightforward. To depart without delay, travelers must register their information in advance to avoid disrupting their plans. Note: For future queries of your EVUS application record, remember your application number and provide passport number, visa number, and date of birth. More EVUS Common Questions Q&A! 1. How long does it take to apply for EVUS registration? If you have all the required information, it will take about 20 minutes. 2. What is the difference between EVUS and a visa? EVUS registration is not a visa. Travelers planning to visit the United States must hold a valid B1, B2, or B1/B2 visitor visa when traveling. Chinese tourists who only possess a valid 10-year B1, B2, or B1/B2 visa must apply for registration on EVUS and hold the visa before travel. If you have already registered on EVUS and arrive at the airport, that passenger will be able to enter the United States. Any other non-B1, B2, or B1/B2 visa types with 10-year validity do not require EVUS registration application. 3. Do I need to apply for EVUS registration if I am just transiting through the United States to another country? Chinese citizens transiting through the United States must hold a valid visa. If passengers plan only to transit through the U.S. and continue to another country, when registering on EVUS, for the question "Will you be transiting through the United States to another country?" they or she must answer "Yes". 4. If my visa is still valid but in an expired passport, can I use my visa? If your visa is still valid but in an expired passport, you should use the expired passport containing the visa and another new valid passport to apply for EVUS registration. When completing the EVUS registration, you will use the new passport as the primary passport. When traveling to the United States, you will be required to present both a valid passport and the expired passport containing the valid visa simultaneously. 5. If I have already planned my trip, when should I apply for EVUS registration? You can complete EVUS registration at any time before traveling to the United States. The U.S. Department of Homeland Security recommends that passengers apply for EVUS registration at least 72 hours before departure. The validity period of an EVUS registration, except in cases where it is revoked, is generally two years from the authorization date, or until 6 months before your passport expires, or until your U.S. visa expires. 6. What are the requirements for passports and visas when applying for EVUS registration? Your passport must be valid beyond your planned visit to the United States by at least 6 months, and you must have a valid 10-year B1, B2, or B1/B2 U.S. visitor visa. If your valid visa is in an expired passport, you must obtain a new passport to apply for EVUS registration. When traveling to the United States, you must present both your valid passport and a valid 10-year B1, B2, or B1/B2 U.S. visitor visa. 7. Is there a fee for applying for EVUS registration? U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) states that no registration fee will currently be charged. While an EVUS registration fee is expected to be implemented eventually, the specific implementation date has not been confirmed yet. So hurry up and complete your registration! 8. Can others submit information for travelers in EVUS updates? Yes. Friends, relatives, travel industry professionals, or other third parties can submit required information on behalf of travelers to EVUS. Third parties may also pay related fees on behalf of travelers. However, the traveler themselves is responsible for ensuring the truthfulness and accuracy of all information submitted on their behalf. 9. If I have already registered, does my travel authorization guarantee entry into the United States? No. A successful EVUS registration demonstrates your eligibility to use your 10-year B1, B2, or B1/B2 U.S. visitor visa to enter the United States, but it does not guarantee admission into the country. When you arrive in the United States, at ports of entry you will be inspected by Customs and Border Protection officers. Such officers have the authority under any grounds provided by U.S. law to determine that you are inadmissible to enter the United States. We recommend those holding ten-year U.S. B-category visas: if you have upcoming travel plans to the United States, quickly complete your registration with the EVUS system. After registering in the EVUS system, that's not all—you must pass review to qualify for entry into U.S. Customs. The review period can be as long as 72 hours, so remember to monitor your registration review status. The EVUS system has just launched and does not yet have a complete fee collection function, so currently registration is free. We recommend taking this opportunity to register quickly!
