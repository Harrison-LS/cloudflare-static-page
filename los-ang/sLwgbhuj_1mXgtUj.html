Los Angeles Hotel Recommendations Previously, I recommended hotel options in San Francisco. Now let's recommend hotels in Los Angeles. Because Los Angeles is simply too vast, choosing a hotel becomes extremely difficult for someone like me who struggles with indecision. So, let's also look at the range of hotel choices in Los Angeles: 1. Airport Area - Inglewood Many travelers choose Los Angeles as their entry/exit city for the US. Indeed, round-trip international flight tickets through LA tend to be more economical in price. However, from a trip planning perspective, entering/exiting via LA may not be the optimal choice. We'll discuss this later~ If your arrival flight in LA is late or you need to transfer directly to an inland city like Salt Lake City on the second day, you could choose to stay at an airport hotel for one night. Advantages: - Free shuttle service to and from the airport. - Close proximity to rental car companies; you can return your vehicle, then walk/take a taxi back to your hotel, and catch the complimentary shuttle to the airport early morning the next day. Disadvantages: - Los Angeles suffers from serious traffic congestion, making this location unsuitable if you plan to stay near the airport and daily explore downtown attractions. 2. Santa Monica Beach Area Advantages: Located very close to Santa Monica Beach, with a shopping district situated right alongside the shoreline. The area is very safe, and there are many great restaurants. Disadvantage: Hotel budgets are relatively high. 3. Beverly Hills and West Hollywood Area: Advantages: Due to proximity to wealthy Beverly Hills, safety levels are higher. Very close to various Hollywood attractions; Universal Studios is just a 10-minute drive north. Disadvantage: Hotel prices are definitely the highest, with paid parking that's quite expensive. 4. Chinese Communities Monterey Park/Rosemead/Montebello/West Covina, etc. Advantages: A gathering place for Chinese people, Los Angeles' new-generation true Chinatown. Hotels offer the best price-to-value ratio—rooms are spacious and relatively modern, with mostly free parking. The area boasts numerous food courts, Chinese restaurants, and convenient shopping at local supermarkets. Disadvantage: Traveling to regular attractions takes about 30-40 minutes by car without traffic delays, though conditions worsen significantly during peak congestion times. Disney Area If you're traveling with children for a full day at Disney, consider staying at a hotel near the Disneyland Resort the night before. Disadvantages: Located somewhat far from downtown and major attractions.
