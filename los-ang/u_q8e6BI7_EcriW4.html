Highlights! Must-see small towns and beautiful scenery along America's Highway 1!

Los Angeles

Brief

Highway No. 1 has been named by National Geographic as one of the top 50 destinations you should not miss in your lifetime. This highway is worth driving over 10 times. Its very existence teaches people a simple truth: sometimes the destination isn't that important; the scenery along the way will make you want to stay forever!

Content

Why choose self-driving along Highway 1? Traveling California's Highway 1 is North America's most popular self-driving route, number one without a doubt! Almost every person who visits the US will want to drive it at least once. People say that driving along California's Highway 1 is a pleasure, a lifestyle attitude, a spiritual cleansing, and a fusion of people, sea, and sunshine. Highway 1 starts north of San Francisco and winds its way south all the way to southern Los Angeles, crossing California's entire western coastline. Unlike the desolate "American highways" in our imagination, Highway 1 is lined with continuous sunshine and endless ocean views, featuring charming small towns and national parks along the way that make it far more than just a simple sightseeing trip by the sea. Californians are probably the Americans who know how to live best; they have their own philosophy of enjoyment. For them, Highway 1 is not just a road but truly their lifestyle. On one side lies the vast Pacific Ocean, on the other rise steep and towering cliff mountains. Rocks splash with sheets of white foam waves—the most iconic scenery along Highway 1. In the early morning, light mist drifts gently, as if traveling through clouds. At dusk, watching the sunset's afterglow paint across the Pacific is simply relaxing! Adding special character to Highway 1 are the charming small towns and national parks scattered along the route: Carmel—art town Solvang—Danish style Bixby Bridge—the world's tallest concrete arch bridge The only purple beach on Earth Big Sur—one of America's top 50 must-visit destinations ... Every stop you make becomes a chapter in your life story. How to explore it most conveniently? In the United States, well-developed highway facilities, low gas prices, and most importantly, scenic roads that feel like driving through a park make travelers reluctant to leave. Highway 1 possesses all these qualities, making self-driving and private charter tours the top choices. The designer of Highway 1 thought very carefully. At any beautiful scenic spot, Highway 1 always provides an extra area for tourists to stop, view, and take photos. This prevents the two-lane Highway 1 from becoming congested, and ensures travelers won't regret missing out on beautiful scenery. In fact, such parking spots are so densely distributed along Highway 1 that often within your line of sight there are three or four of them. If you stopped at every spot, you'd become overwhelmed, which also indicates that the number of beautifully scenic locations along this winding road is truly vast. American highways follow a pattern: all odd-numbered ones run north-south, while even numbers run east-west. From the west coast to the east coast, highway numbers increase in an organized manner from low to high. Highway 1 represents the westernmost north-south highway. On one side lies California's beautiful coastline—the Pacific Ocean; on the other side rises the majestic Rocky Mountains. On Highway 1, it is recommended to drive from San Francisco toward Los Angeles, allowing you to travel along the Pacific side and fully enjoy the scenic views throughout the journey. Recommended driving route: San Francisco → Monterey → 17-Mile Drive Scenic Route → Carmel → Big Sur → Solvang → Santa Barbara → Malibu → Los Angeles Must-know tips for self-driving Highway 1! Self-Driving Tips: 1. This highway segment must be driven southbound only—starting from San Francisco heading toward Los Angeles. When approaching from this direction, you're on the side closer to the coastline. Driving northbound means most scenic overlooks won't have accessible parking (note that this route's highlights feature cliffs right alongside the road and mountainous highway sections. Drivers need both courage and skill here, making it impossible to easily view scenery while driving—thank you to our fellow drivers!) 2. From San Francisco to Los Angeles, never simply use start-to-end automatic navigation. You must set waypoints along the route. Automatic navigation chooses US Route 101 instead of California State Route 1, and you'll miss everything worthwhile. Many charming towns and natural landscapes worth remembering require manual location settings. Ideally, navigate from one stop to another. Even if time is tight and you can't visit every attraction, still follow this positioning to ensure you're actually on CA-1 and don't miss some coastal scenery. 3. California sunshine is notoriously blinding (especially at sunrise and sunset). Safety first—always bring quality sunglasses. Polarized lenses are the driving choice, but they make navigation screens hard to read, so passengers must stay alert and properly monitor the navigation and road conditions. 4. Depending on your travel schedule, choose to overnight in Carmel or the Danish town of Solvang along the way. There are also many small lodges scattered throughout. Stop and rest as evening approaches—don't rush through night driving! Beyond obvious safety concerns, night traveling ruins the entire scenic experience. Scenic views and charming small towns worth your time! *Half Moon Bay State Beach Along the California coast are scattered surf towns, and Half Moon Bay is world-famous for its superior location near San Francisco and its peaceful, leisurely atmosphere. You can play golf on the cliff edge, surf at the famous Mavericks, or casually visit local small breweries! *Pigeon Point Lighthouse Shortly after starting Highway 1, you'll arrive at an ancient lighthouse—the tallest on America's Pacific coastline and still in use today. Admission is free. Every November, a ceremony is held to light the lamp using kerosene lamps and lenses, the same method people used over 100 years ago. It truly makes for excellent photographs. *Monterey A small town rich with California's Spanish and Mexican cultural atmosphere, Monterey features beautiful scenery and friendly locals, making it a quintessential California town. Attractions include the famous Monterey Aquarium, the 17-mile drive, and more. Monterey Town Monterey boasts a well-developed arts community, with both local and internationally renowned artist museums and entertainment venues everywhere. There's Cannery Row, famous for commemorating writer John Steinbeck; Simultaneously, this area boasts rich and diverse marine life—the Monterey Aquarium is one of the largest in North America. **17 Mile Drive** Continuing south from Monterey, you will see the sign marking Pebble Beach's famous "17-Mile Drive." This is a coastal road on private land stretching 17 miles long, requiring a $10 fee per vehicle—the only toll along your entire Highway One journey. This winding road follows an arched peninsula and offers one of the West Coast's most stunning shorelines. During your drive, you'll spot luxurious mansions partially hidden among the hills—each backed by mountains with unobstructed ocean views, all uniquely different, some quite magnificent and breathtaking. This is America's ultra-wealthy vacation destination, where opulent homes and exotic cars are everywhere. World-famous golf courses also dot this landscape, hosting high-quality tournaments regularly. The route features 21 scenic stops where you can pull over sequentially to admire the views—several offering spectacular scenery. If time permits, consider spending another night in Carmel, a beautiful town brimming with artistic charm. **Carmel-by-the-Sea** This charming town exudes Bohemian flair. Despite its history spanning less than a century, it is renowned far and wide for the refined artistic atmosphere cultivated over many decades plus exceptional natural beauty. Writers and artists have long been drawn to this locale. Over 100 years, Carmel has maintained its distinctive character with its population of only around 4,000 residents—this enduring charm owes itself entirely to the careful stewardship and active preservation efforts of the townspeople. Town residents resist modernization: they reject all advertising, prohibit neon signs and parking meters, ban fast-food restaurants... Carmel-by-the-Sea Carmel-by-the-Sea has become a highlight along California's Highway 1 and the 17-Mile Drive thanks to its beautiful natural environment and refined artistic atmosphere. Here, whether fashion boutiques, antique shops, candy stores, galleries, toy stores, or craft shops, everything from storefronts to merchandise delights visitors' eyes and captivates them. As early as a century ago, "Carmel of artists, poets, and writers" was already famous worldwide. In 1969, renowned Chinese master painter Zhang Daqian once lived here and called his residence "a place worth living in." In this paradise-like Carmel, many unique-style artists and writers live in this small bohemian city facing the sea along the mountain slopes. The unusual buildings and scenery are as beautiful as fairy tales. Local residents strive to resist modernization. To date, posting advertisements, installing neon lights, or building fast-food restaurants remain prohibited within town limits to preserve its original appearance. Traveling west along the main street Ocean Avenue to its end leads you to Carmel Beach on the 17-Mile Drive's distinctive large beach. Bixby Creek Bridge The bridge with the highest screen presence in Western America. When completed in 1932, this bridge was the world's largest single-arch concrete bridge. The bridge is 714 feet long. To this day, it remains one of the favorite attractions for tourists. Because it's located on California's scenic coastline, car advertisements are often filmed here. Big Sur (大苏尔) Anyone who loves travel has definitely heard of Big Sur. It is the most beautiful scenery along California's Highway 1; its tranquility, naturalness, freshness, ethereal quality, and seclusion are all unique in this world—it is a true idyllic paradise. Big Sur represents the most beautiful and essential section of Highway 1, one of the world's most unforgettable coastlines; it could be said to be the climax of Highway 1 arriving early. The Big Sur coastline stretches from Carmel Town to Hearst Castle, spanning about 90 miles. Elephant seals, forests of redwoods, enveloping sea fog create an undefined edge without a central urban core. It possesses a mysterious charm that attracts visitors (including Henry Miller and Beat Generation writer Jack Kerouac). Cliffs, ocean, blue sky—in short, this is exactly where you want to go. Big Sur California's highways are built within paintings themselves; traveling south from San Francisco along the famous Highway 1, hundreds of kilometers of winding road unfold continuously—breathtaking beauty that captures your breath at every turn. On the left is a steep rock wall rising abruptly from flat ground and slanting upward toward the blue sky. California pines on the cliff ridges stand in neat rows, densely packed and impenetrable, holding their distinctive posture: upper bodies tilted inland with green branches at the top intertwined to support each other. Brownish tree trunks form countless parallel lines intersecting with the ground. On the right stretches a breathtakingly vast expanse of azure that leaves viewers speechless. The water displays shades ranging from sapphire and indigo to deep navy and pale blue, with thick pure white foam churning across its surface. Golden, pale yellow, grass-yellow, and dark amber sands curve gently into the depths of the waves. Stretching out from coastal cliffs toward the open ocean are rocky projections that block incoming surges. When the sea crashes against them, water splashes in crystalline clusters before scattering helplessly in all directions. This is one of the 50 must-visit destinations—typical Big Sur scenery featuring untouched primitive natural beauty. *Piedras Blancas Elephant Seal Rookery* After approximately passing through the state park, you'll come to Elephant Seal Beach, a site well worth seeing. From viewing platforms on low cliffs, you can observe elephant seals sunbathing in clusters across the sandy beach—a truly spectacular sight that is rare to encounter. These elephant seals appear completely unaware of their surroundings, sleeping soundly as if they were alone on the beach, seemingly untroubled by anything around them. One important thing to remember: when viewing them, never approach from behind them because elephant seals are most afraid of having their escape route to the ocean cut off. Otherwise, they will be extremely angry and emit a series of rough roars after another. *Hearst Castle* The Hearst Castle was once the private estate of William Randolph Hearst, an American newspaper tycoon. As one of California's most prestigious residences and among the finest homes in Western America, it boasts having hosted numerous famous guests, including comedy film star Charlie Chaplin, US President Franklin Roosevelt, and British Prime Minister Winston Churchill. Hearst Castle is California's most luxurious castle. Its owner, William Randolph Hearst, was a media mogul during the 1920s and 1930s. Built atop a mountain, the construction took 28 years to complete, embodying extreme opulence. The crystal-clear swimming pools both indoors and outdoors serve as the castle's crowning feature. The indoor pool features mosaic designs depicting underwater marine life at the bottom, with gold trim along the edges. The outdoor pool is surrounded by pristine white marble sculptures, creating a stunning contrast with the azure water. Hearst was passionate about collecting art throughout his life, making every item in the castle—including furniture, tapestries, paintings, sculptures, fireplaces, ceilings, staircases, entire rooms—true artistic treasures. *Solvang, Denmark Village* The Danish town of Solvang is the most beautiful small town along Highway 1 that I visited. Every home has lush greenery, each household has blooming flowers, porches are covered with vibrant vines, windmills spin leisurely beside cottages, and misty mountains rise in the distance—all making one understand why Andersen wrote fairy tales. Famous for its bakeries, restaurants, and specialty shops, here you'll find architectural styles that closely resemble those in Denmark itself. For instance, there is a statue—a replica of Copenhagen's famous Little Mermaid. There is also a bust of Hans Christian Andersen, the renowned Danish fairy tale writer. In the center of Solvang town, you can see a copy of the Round Tower from Copenhagen, one-third the size of the original. Danish Village Solvang comes from Danish, meaning "sunshine countryside." It was created by Danish immigrants from the early 20th century who fully replicated their homeland's Danish architectural style and community planning to create a Scandinavian-style town within the United States. Statistics show that two-thirds of the permanent population are descendants of Danes. So if you're in North America, why travel thousands of miles to Northern Europe's Denmark to experience Danish culture? Just come here instead. Besides, everyone speaks English here. *Santa Barbara This Spanish-style town was rated as one of the best places in America for retirement. Many buildings preserve Spanish colonial style, and tall palm trees are the hallmark of Santa Barbara. The Santa Barbara coastline features flat, expansive beaches, earning it the nickname "America's Blue Coast," located just 90 kilometers north of Los Angeles. Palm tree shadows line the roadsides; besides the palms, the seagulls on Santa Barbara Beach are also exceptionally abundant. Santa Barbara is a serene enclave where wealthy individuals and celebrities purchase real estate, naturally making it one of the most expensive areas in the country. Classic Route Recommendation - 1-Day Itinerary **Classic 1-Day Trip Approach** If you only have one day and time is tight! Then San Francisco-Monterey must be your top choice. In the early morning, depart from a hotel in downtown San Francisco. Highway One's northernmost starting point is located at the Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco, passing through the city sequentially until 19th Avenue. After merging onto Interstate 280 (I-280), traveling several kilometers will bring you to Daly City. From San Francisco to Santa Cruz along the coast stretches 70 miles. Along this route, you'll encounter numerous natural beaches, vendor stalls set up by organic farms, and many seaside salt villages. The *Half Moon Bay State Beach* extends for 4 miles and is surrounded by scenic campsites that wrap around Highway One. Located 20 miles northwest of Santa Cruz, the *Año Nuevo State Park* is the largest land breeding ground for sea lions globally. Winter is the breeding and mating season for sea lions; advance reservations are required to book guided tours. **Santa Cruz (Santa Cruz)** Heading toward Santa Cruz, the Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk is ideal for family outings. This nostalgic amusement park features classic roller coasters, arcade games, carousels, and serves corn dogs. Santa Cruz is California's renowned surfing destination, with Steamer Lane serving as paradise for elite surf enthusiasts. The expansive beaches and gentle waves remain endlessly captivating. *Monterey* Heading south, the coastal highway gradually fades away before curving back inland. Marina State Beach lies just ahead, crossing into Monterey city. In Monterey, everything revolves around the sea. A premier aquarium now draws countless travelers. As a national marine protected area, diving, whale watching, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding are popular activities. Meanwhile, downtown's historic district preserves California's Spanish and Mexican cultural origins. For over half a century, the old fishing pier has remained one of Monterey's top tourist destinations. The azure seas, flower-adorned reefs, squirrels, seabirds, seals everywhere, dramatic cliffs, ancient pines and cypress trees—create the enchanting landscape of 17-Mile Drive.
