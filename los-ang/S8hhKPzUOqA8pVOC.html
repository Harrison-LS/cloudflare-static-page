Bixby Creek Bridge 📍 20 minutes by car from Carmel town | 15 miles ⌚ Recommended visit time: 1 hour West Coast's most frequently photographed bridge - a weathered arch spanning sheer cliffs above the Pacific Ocean's turbulent waves, creating a magnificent "mountain-sea-bridge" landscape. Bixby Creek Bridge Bixby Bridge is an open single-arch bridge located on California's Highway 1 in Big Sur. With a span of approximately 100 meters, it remains the longest concrete arch bridge in California's highway system. Opened in 1932, it was once the world's highest single-arch concrete bridge and continues to be one of the most popular tourist attractions today. Big Sur 📍 15-minute drive from Bixby Bridge | 10 kilometers ⌚ Recommended visit time: 1 hour Big Sur is known as one of the world's most beautiful vantage points where land meets ocean. Its winding highway with dramatic curves has been named by National Geographic as "one of life's must-see scenic destinations." Big Sur California's highways are built like paintings along the famous Highway 1. Traveling south from San Francisco, hundreds of kilometers of winding road offer breathtaking views that leave visitors breathless. On the left rise steep rock walls that shoot upward at an angle toward the blue sky. California pines atop the cliff ridges stand in rows and lines, densely packed in their characteristic posture: upper bodies tilted inland, green foliage at the tops intertwined for support, while brown tree trunks form countless parallel angles with the ground. On the right stretches a breathtaking expanse of azure. The water, ranging from sapphire to indigo to deep blue to light blue, churns up thick pure white foam. Golden, pale yellow, grass yellow, and dark yellow beaches curve gently into the depths of waves. Rock formations extend like tentacles from coastal cliffs toward the ocean, blocking surging waves as the sea splashes out crystal-clear water that helplessly scatters in all directions. This is one of 50 destinations you must visit — typical Big Sur scenery, radiating a primordial natural beauty never trodden upon. Solvang (Danish Village) 📍 2 hours drive from Hearst Castle | 174 km ⌚ Recommended visit time: 1 day; recommended place to stay A fairy tale town filled with windmills offering authentic Danish scenery. Stroll through the village admiring Danish architecture and seek out windmills and the Little Mermaid statue. Solvang derives its name from the Danish phrase for "sunny countryside," established by early 20th-century immigrants who meticulously recreated their homeland's architectural charm and community layout in California. With approximately two-thirds of residents having Danish ancestry, this American town offers an authentic Nordic experience without requiring a transatlantic journey to Scandinavia—and everyone speaks English here.
