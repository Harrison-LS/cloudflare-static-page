Beyond movies and romance, Los Angeles is also a city of sports. It boasts world-class stadiums and world-class teams, and the interactions between them have created countless legends on the court. Only by being there in person—to see players' determination during games, feel the cheers of fans in the stands, and participate in the natural interactions happening at the venue—can you understand that people's passion for sports here has gone deep into their bones. Crypto.com Arena: Watch NBA Games at Lakers Home If you're a Lakers fan, the one thing you absolutely cannot miss when visiting Los Angeles is watching an NBA game at the Lakers' home arena. Crypto.com Arena is the Staples Center as Chinese people know it. It was renamed in 2021 to its current name. This legendary venue that has been open since 1999 serves as the home for the NBA's Los Angeles Lakers and Clippers, the NHL's Kings, and the WNBA's Sparks. It is also a pilgrimage site for basketball fans around the world. [Image source: Official website] For Lakers fans who are devoted to their team, Crypto.com Arena represents faith—it carries so many moving moments and glories! "Big Shaq" Shaquille O'Neal, "Black Mamba" Kobe Bryant, "King James" LeBron James, Anthony Davis, and Chinese-American star Jeremy Lin have all left their sweat here. How many players have grown from rookies just starting their careers into independent stars, with all ups and downs, joys and sorrows, honors and setbacks performed in game after game. On non-game days, you can also walk around the arena and browse the fan shop. There are Lakers merchandise and gear from other teams for sale. Buying a Kobe Bryant jersey is a wish that many fans hold dear. Near downtown Los Angeles and Crypto.com Arena, there are several Kobe murals worth seeing. Although Kobe unfortunately passed away, his never-give-up "Mamba mentality" will forever inspire future generations. SoFi Stadium: Visit the 2028 Olympic Main Venue in Advance Curious how luxurious the "most expensive stadium in the world" can be? Want to see thrilling American football? Don't miss Los Angeles' new landmark - SoFi Stadium. The innovatively designed SoFi Stadium officially opened in September 2020. It will host not only the opening and closing ceremonies of the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics, but also serve as a venue for the 2026 World Cup. As home to NFL teams the Rams and Chargers, Super Bowl LVI - America's version of Spring Festival Gala - was held here in 2022. SoFi Stadium was built with an investment of $5.5 billion, can accommodate 70,000 spectators, and is referred to as "the most luxurious stadium in history." Simulated on-site cheers will make you get into character instantly. If you happen to experience it during "Rally Day" before an NFL game, it will surely leave fans with unforgettable impressions. Official Website: https://www.sofistadium.com/ Dodger Stadium: Experience the Charm of America's National Pastime Baseball If you are a devoted baseball fan, you must be familiar with the Los Angeles Dodgers. They are a legendary team that has won the MLB World Series championship four times and like the Lakers are a source of pride for Los Angeles! Image courtesy of MLB.com Dodger Stadium, completed in 1962, is home to the Dodgers team. The stadium can accommodate 56,000 spectators and is currently the largest baseball venue in Major League Baseball. Experiencing an American pastime game live here, surrounded by throngs of passionate fans, creates a truly unforgettable experience! Image courtesy of MLB.com This legendary stadium itself is spectacular and has become extremely popular on Instagram as one of the must-visit venues. When there are no games, the facility hosts numerous large-scale events including concerts, musicals, and auto racing activities. Image courtesy of MLB.com If you only want to tour the stadium, themed tours are available. The 90-minute standard tour includes a guide who will show you around the ballpark and everything related to the Dodgers team while sharing legendary stories from this historic venue. The 45-minute "Pre-Game Tour" is designed specifically for fans, offering early access to watch batting practice and warm-up sessions before games.
