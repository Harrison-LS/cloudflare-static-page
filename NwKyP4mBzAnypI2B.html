Los Angeles

Brief

The must-visit pink wall in Los Angeles is the favorite street photography spot for trendsetters from around the world.

Content

In recent years, the number of global street-style photo hotspots that have gained popularity on Instagram is considerable. Most were first discovered by fashion influencers or photographers with large followings, before becoming sought-after shooting locations both domestically and internationally. Los Angeles, America's city that has never lacked for news value and exposure, saw Paul Smith paint his store's exterior wall in bold pink, adding another asset to this "City of Angels" for showing off to the world. Under California's bright sun, as more people compete to take photos here, the "Pink Wall" has also been listed among local popular attractions. Paul Smith: A Color Temptation The British fashion brand with a love for "pink" traces its roots back to England. Since opening its first store in 1970, nearly half a century later remains unchanged are its exquisite British taste and appropriately rebellious spirit. Founder Paul Smith is praised by the fashion world as a genius designer, especially skilled at playfully adding some bright colors into steady gentleman style. Everyone Loves the Pink Wall The "Pink Wall" is located on Melrose Avenue in Los Angeles, adjacent to Hollywood Walk of Fame and Beverly Hills, with surrounding areas being authentic fashion trend blocks. In Los Angeles, among several popular stores renowned for their beautiful facades, Paul Smith attracts a steady stream of customers daily and becomes the site of countless creative street photos thanks to its famous "Pink Wall." by Kidd_Hsu by Kidd_Hsu Just one pink wall seems magical—it can inspire endless creativity from men who usually hide behind cameras when in front of lenses, and make shy girls strike moving poses even around strangers. This seemingly simple yet vibrant wall brings us multiple possibilities: it can serve as a photo background, be the highlight of a photo, or amplify its subject's presence manyfold amidst Los Angeles' charming atmosphere. Interestingly, in June 2017, Paul Smith's Los Angeles store collaborated with Instagram to repaint one of their exterior walls with rainbow stripes to support the local LGBT "Pride Month" event (LGBT Pride Month), and this rainbow-colored "Pink Wall" became a limited-time shooting backdrop at that time. Currently, this "Rainbow Wall" has been restored to its original pink color. How to Pose | N Possibilities in Front of the "Pink Wall" Taking photos in front of the "Pink Wall"—any outfit and pose works well. But if you want your images to be more aesthetic and unique, consider these ideas: Simple Outfit + Side Profile Shots You don't need overly designed or fashionable clothing—just wear everyday clothes that contrast noticeably with the pink wall. Imagine yourself as a hurried passerby; capture your side silhouette walking past the wall. Natural and highlights street photography style. Props + Dynamic Photos If you feel like putting too much effort into posing makes it too girly, a casual jump or leap can significantly boost your photo's coolness and movement. With props like balloons, ice cream, or sunglasses adding flair, you can even create interesting interactions with onlookers outside the frame. Formations + Group Photos Don't waste this large creative space. Gather your partner or several best friends—there's strength in numbers. Come up with a goofy pose or interesting formation together. You'll not only have fun but also attract plenty of attention. # Changing Perspective + Still Life Photography Want to be different from those repetitive headshots and full-body shots? Try changing your perspective, focusing on your beloved dog, the coffee you're holding, or succulent plants. Even shadows of people on the wall can become the protagonist of the image. Of course, there are countless possibilities. It's suggested to stand a bit further when shooting; this way you can leave more wall area visible. The ratio of exposed sky and ground also appears less unnatural.
