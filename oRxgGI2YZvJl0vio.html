You can also see the Queen Mary!) ☞ Queen Mary Cruise Ticket ($32 USD) Queen Mary / 玛丽皇后号 Queen Mary is a famous attraction in Long Beach, Los Angeles. This luxury cruise ship began sailing in 1936 and operated until 1967. Its black-and-white color scheme with three massive orange smokestacks reminds people of the Titanic (yes, that world-famous sinking ship) produced by the same company. However, Queen Mary was about 200 feet longer than Titanic. After retiring in 1967, the City of Long Beach purchased Queen Mary and converted its cabins into hotel rooms. The restaurants on board are open to the public as well, while the ship's chapel has become a popular wedding venue. All facilities aboard remain accessible to visitors, essentially functioning like a small museum. A guide on a haunted tour narrates paranormal events that occurred onboard... Although we may never have seen Titanic's interior ourselves, Queen Mary—built by the same company—allows us to imagine what it might be like inside that legendary vessel. What truly makes Queen Mary famous is its reputation for ghosts, particularly legends about spirits haunting the beds. This ship is genuinely a haunted vessel in every sense. So when you visit, make sure to take a Haunted Tour and hear these chilling ghost stories! It especially saw another ship collide and sink during World War II, plus many wounded soldiers died on board during wartime, so ghost stories are often spread around. Every Halloween there's also a Dark Harbor Ghost Ship event; people with big guts can try it (I'm very timid, so I dare not). For more detailed booking information or tour prices onboard, please refer to their official website. (By the way, the nearby Aquarium of the Pacific at the port is also a great place to kill time with kids!) The La Brea Tar Pits, located next to LACMA (Los Angeles County Museum of Art), is a natural asphalt pit. Because there are often covering materials like leaves, dust, or water on top, animals easily accidentally fall in and get stuck, so over centuries a huge amount of animal bones and fossils have accumulated. There is also the George C. Page Museum here, dedicated to studying tar pits and showcasing the animal specimens discovered within them. Even on regular days, there are long lines at the museum, so most people skip it and use that time to visit LACMA instead, which houses Urban Light - an installation artwork beloved by social media influencers. It's a win-win!
