When you think of Los Angeles, Hollywood, the Walk of Fame, Disney, or Universal Studios likely come to mind - these are naturally the top choices for tourists. But Los Angeles is far more than this. If you have additional time, I recommend staying longer here to experience authentic California culture and a more artistic "City of Angels"! You may not realize that these following landmarks truly best represent Los Angeles! The Birthplace of Instagram Photos - Museums Few people know that Los Angeles is actually a "city of museums," being the city worldwide with the highest number of museums and galleries per capita. From natural history to cultural landscape, from classical art to modernism, every museum in Los Angeles has its own unique character. Many museums have also become favorite photo spots for tourists due to their distinctive architectural beauty~ Los Angeles County Museum of Art Collection: Civilizations and arts from major continents around the world Admission fees: Adults $25, students and seniors $21, free for minors Visit duration: Approximately 4 hours Most notable feature: "City Lights" lamp post sculptures Address: 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Los Angeles, CA 90036 The Los Angeles County Museum of Art is also one of Los Angeles' top tourist destinations, especially the "City Lights" column sculpture installation at its entrance. Founded in 1961, it claims to be America's largest collecting institution for contemporary art since World War II. It has always been dedicated to acquiring artworks spanning various fields including history and geography, showcasing Los Angeles' unique multicultural character. As the largest art museum in western United States, it houses over 110,000 artistic treasures from ancient Egyptian times, European masterpieces, excellent Asian art collections, works originating from North and South America, Islamic art, and marginalized modern artworks—all with high aesthetic value. Los Angeles County Museum of Art The Los Angeles County Museum of Art complex includes five buildings, three outdoor sculptures, an art library, art resource center, large lecture hall and theater. Home to over 10,000 works of art, it is the largest "encyclopedic" public museum on America's West Coast. The interior features a column-free exhibition space that is spacious and open; combined with natural sunlight, it creates an ideal display environment for contemporary art. The Broad Museum Collection Focus: Contemporary Art Free Admission: Free (requires advance reservation, or can join the queue on-site) Visiting Duration: About 2-3 hours Top Attraction: Yayoi Kusama's works Address: 221 S Grand Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90012 The Broad Museum located on Grand Avenue in downtown Los Angeles officially opened on September 20, 2015 and became extremely popular. Inside the museum are not only outstanding contemporary artworks from around the world, but also "Infinity Mirrored Room—A Billion Miles Away" (Infinity Mirrored Room) by Yayoi Kusama that made the museum famous on social media. This mirrored room is decorated with LED colored lights shining like a vast starry river, making visitors feel as if they are in the Milky Way, leaving them deeply impressed. The Broad Museum building is based on the concept of "Masks and Domes" with unlimited creativity, featuring two exhibition floors where numerous exquisite artworks are carefully collected, very worth stopping to appreciate. Broad Museum The Getty Center Collection Areas: From Ancient Greece to early 20th century European art Free Admission: Yes Visit Duration: About 4-6 hours Main Highlights: Garden artistry, Van Gogh's masterpiece "Irises" Address: 1200 Getty Center Dr, Los Angeles, CA 90049 The Getty Center is an important landmark cultural site in Los Angeles and also one of the most comprehensive museums in California and even worldwide that collectors highly value, a destination art lovers dream to visit. Here is a large collection of European paintings, sculptures, decorative arts, and photography works, including Vincent van Gogh's masterpiece "Irises" and five Rembrandt paintings. Beyond that, the Getty Center building itself is also a work of art. Famous architect Richard Meier used square and circular structural elements to spread the Getty Center across two ridges. Visitors need to take a small train up the mountain while enjoying beautiful views of the entire valley. Stepping out of the museum leads you to the colorful Getty Central Garden, which enjoys fame not inferior to the artworks inside. Tree-lined paths, gentle streams flow along the pathways and merge into ponds; at the center of each pond lies an azalea maze, with artistic atmosphere everywhere. Getty Center - A cluster of unique buildings standing on a cliff face, representing one of Los Angeles' most important and wealthiest art institutions in the world. - Composed of six buildings, large-scale gardens, and open fields; it is one of the best locations for panoramic views of Los Angeles. - Houses original manuscripts and old photographs from American artists, making it one of the largest collection institutions of similar artworks in America. - Collections include Roman and Greek marble sculpture antiques, illuminated manuscripts, European paintings, drawings, sculptures, and more. Huntington Library, Art Museum, and Botanical Gardens Collection Areas: Western art from the Renaissance to the 20th century Free admission: Adults $25, under 18 $13; first Thursday of each month free (advance reservation required) Recommended visit duration: 6 hours Main attraction: Garden-style museum Address: 1151 Oxford Rd, San Marino, CA 91108 The Huntington encompasses three major components—The Huntington Library, Art Collections, and Botanical Gardens—a combination unique in America and perhaps even worldwide. Established in 1919, this museum opened to the public in 1928 according to Henry E. Huntingtons will after his death as a collector. Combining libraries, art collections, and horticulture into one entity, it is hailed as the world's most memorable garden-style museumperfect for slowing down and savoring each moment. In recent years, this place has made another grand acquisition—a Chinese Suzhou garden that was "collected" here; every stone and tile was transported from Jiangnan in southern China, making visitors feel like they had traveled home in an instant. The Huntington Library, Art Collections, and Botanical Gardens The Huntington Library (in English: The Huntington Library, Art Collections and Botanical Gardens, or simply "The Huntington") is an educational research institution founded by Henry E. Huntington, situated in San Marino, California. Beyond its library functions, the institution also displays art collections, primarily British portrait paintings and 18th-century French furniture. This library with its books, art, and horticulture has earned the title of the world's most memorable garden-style museum—offering viewing experiences that bring great joy to both body and mind. In recent years, this place has undertaken another grand project—"collecting" a Chinese Suzhou garden. The Suzhou-style garden within the library is called "Liu Fang Yuan." Every stone and tile in the garden was transported from Jiangnan, China, and crafted by artisans from Suzhou. Mr. Huntington personally had a great fondness for Eastern gardens during his lifetime, and many of the plants in the garden originate from China. The Last Bookstore Collection: New and old books, vintage vinyl records, comics, etc. Free entry: Yes Visit duration: About 1-2 hours Most distinctive feature: One of the most beautiful independent bookstores globally Address: 453 S Spring St, Los Angeles, CA 90013 This is one of North America's top ten independent bookstores and among the world's most beautiful bookstores. Located in downtown Los Angeles, it is known as "Los Angeles' Best Bookstore." The interior decoration has a vintage charm, with retro-style bookshelves filled with old atmosphere, adorned with starry pendant lights that create a magical academy feel. Beyond this, the store features artistic displays and photo-worthy book caves and book windows, making it a paradise for book lovers and an Instagram hotspot for young culture enthusiasts. Here you can not only buy and sell books but also choose from vintage vinyl records and comics, making it a well-deserved cultural gathering place. Overlooking Los Angeles - Viewing Platforms Griffith Observatory Free admission: Yes Visit duration: About 1-3 hours Main attraction: Panoramic view of Los Angeles Address: 2800 E Observatory Rd, Los Angeles, CA 90027 Astronomy enthusiasts will certainly not miss the Griffith Observatory. It is one of Los Angeles' iconic landmarks, situated on a mountain northwest of the city center, offering views that encompass all of Los Angeles. Beyond this, it is also one of the world's renowned observatories and a filming location for countless Hollywood movies. Here, you can enter the interior to learn astronomical knowledge about planets and the universe, watch celestial motion demonstrations in the dome theater, or walk up to the open-air viewing platform to overlook downtown Los Angeles and view the entire panorama of Hollywood. Griffith Observatory ·Located on a mountain peak northwest of Los Angeles, offering views that encompass all of Los Angeles. ·Exhibits astronomical knowledge about planets and the universe. The dome theater at the top is built to mimic the sky, allowing 360° appreciation of celestial evolution films. Bare-eye 3D technology brings cosmic wonders close at hand. ·The open-air platform offers a panoramic view of Los Angeles, with neatly arranged streets, orderly city buildings, and downtown skyscrapers all clearly visible. · After night falls, the stargazing platform opens free of charge, allowing you to see the stars in the sky and the outline of the moon's surface. California's Highest Observation Deck - OUE Skyspace LA Is it free: Ticket required Visit duration: About 2 hours Main highlight: Sky Glass Slide Address: 633 W 5th St #840, Los Angeles, CA 90071 OUE Skyspace is California's tallest open-air observation deck, located within the Bank of America Tower in downtown Los Angeles. Rising more than 1,000 feet above ground level, it offers a 360-degree unobstructed panoramic view of the entire Angel City. Here, you can find a cozy window-side sofa indoors to quietly enjoy the sunset, or step outside for an immersive encounter with the surrounding skyscrapers. The outdoor observation area is surrounded by floor-to-ceiling transparent glass walls, providing exceptionally wide viewing angles. It features viral attractions including a 70-story suspended transparent sky slide and angel wings photo spots, making it an essential new landmark not to miss when visiting Los Angeles. Fly High, Dive Deep - Local Featured Activities Six Flags Magic Mountain Is it free: Ticket required Visit duration: One day Main feature: World-class roller coasters Address: 26101 Magic Mountain Pkwy, Valencia, CA 91355 Los Angeles offers not only charming beaches and art exhibitions, but also the most thrilling Six Flags Magic Mountain. Located in Valencia within northern Los Angeles, established in 1971, it ranks first globally for having the most roller coaster theme park. To date, nearly 20 of its coasters have won world awards, including Green Lantern—the world's first 4D coaster, Goliath—America's longest coaster at over 5 kilometers, Drop Tower—the second highest drop tower globally—and Big Apple Loop—America's longest 360° loop. It is truly a paradise for adventure enthusiasts! Six Flags Magic Mountain For roller coaster enthusiasts, Six Flags Magic Mountain located in northern Los Angeles is nothing short of a holy site. Here, nearly 20 coasters have claimed world awards as the park with the most roller coasters. Strolling through the park, screams echo everywhere, and the squeaking sounds of wheels rubbing against tracks fill the air. Each attraction challenges your adrenaline and tests human limits. The world's first 4D coaster Green Lantern (GREEN LANTERN: First Flight), America's longest coaster at nearly 5 kilometers Goliath, Drop Tower—the second highest drop tower globally reaching 126 meters—and various screaming machines deliver unprecedented weightlessness experiences to visitors, making you feel as if you're about to escape Earth's gravity. Even after the ride ends, the feeling lingers long without settling. ## Whale Watching and Dolphin Watching Cruise **Is it free:** Boat tickets required **Duration:** Approximately 2-3 hours **Main Feature:** Available year-round Departing from the port at the southern tip of Los Angeles, you can experience a unique whale watching and dolphin watching tour. The climate is comfortable throughout the year, allowing for whale sightings all seasons. Humpback whales are most commonly seen in winter and spring, while blue whales are frequently spotted during summer and autumn. On lucky days, you may even witness pods of whales. Beyond whales, this area is excellent for observing kingfishers, otters, sea lions, and dolphins. The captain and crew possess extensive marine biology knowledge; their professional commentary will ensure an unforgettable experience. ## NBA Los Angeles Lakers/Claips Games **Is it free:** Game tickets required **Duration:** Approximately 2-3 hours **Main Feature:** Experience the game atmosphere firsthand For NBA fans, watching a favorite team's game in person is a dream come true. When visiting Los Angeles, this opportunity should not be missed! The famous Staples Center, home of both the Lakers and Clippers, is located in downtown Los Angeles. This is a multi-purpose sports arena, one of the major sports facilities in the Los Angeles area. It hosts over 250 events annually and attracts nearly 4 million visitors each year. It is the only NBA arena shared by two teams, and also the only venue in North America to serve as home court for two NBA teams, one NHL team, and one WNBA team. Hollywood Warner Bros Studio VIP Tour Free or Paid: Ticket Required Visit Duration: Approximately 2-3 hours Main Highlight: Experience TV show filming locations On this journey, you will have the opportunity to explore Hollywood's big film production factory, and personally visit numerous filming sites of American dramas and movies such as The Big Bang Theory, Friends, Batman, Harry Potter, and 2 Broke Girls. Through explanations and immersive experiences, understand how the American blockbusters we love are created from nothing. Get up close to the original crews and studios at the beginning of Hollywood's magic. If you're lucky, you might even encounter actors who are currently filming! It is unpredictable which face from your TV screens you will meet around the next corner. After visiting iconic attractions along Highway 1, the journey continues to San Francisco.
