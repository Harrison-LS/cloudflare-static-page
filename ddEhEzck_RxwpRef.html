Taxes: Taxes vary across different US states. Places like California and NYC have higher taxes, though there are also tax-free states. Shipping: Most shopping websites offer free shipping when you reach a minimum purchase amount or during promotional events. Otherwise, shipping fees differ based on delivery speed. For example, Saks Fifth Avenue charges starting at $7.99 for shipping. Amazon Prime members can enjoy fast 2-3 day delivery (standard is 5-7 days), but this benefit is free only for one month; afterward it requires payment unless you cancel the membership in advance if you don't want to pay. Returns: Returns are very convenient in the US, as long as they meet the return policy requirements. Many websites even offer free return shipping. Discounts: In my opinion, discounts come and go, and what's appealing depends on personal preference. Besides well-known major sales like Black Friday and Christmas, smaller discounts typically appear at season endings or during holidays. To be honest, shopping malls are N times more crowded during discount seasons than usual, significantly reducing the shopping experience. On Labor Day, I visited Ten Thousand Villages with crowds about 3-5 times larger than normal. Many stores required queuing to enter, and tasks like trying on clothes or checking out were exhausting enough just thinking about them. Beyond queuing, there was still more queuing. In summary: if you have good stamina and endurance, and happen to catch a discount season, go ahead and shop enthusiastically. If you prefer not to compromise your shopping experience, it's better to buy items on regular days. During annual new collection seasons like March and September, you can even experience products exclusive to certain markets early on. For instance, this time TF (Tom Ford), YSL, Dior, Nars, etc.'s new makeup collections launched in Europe and the US earlier than in China.
