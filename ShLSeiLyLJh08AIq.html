Brief

Where would you choose to go on your first trip to America - which cities, what sights, and which national parks to explore? If you love road trips and nature, then the American West is definitely not something you should miss.

I. Exploring Downtown San Francisco The first stop of choosing San Francisco is because you can drive directly along Highway 1 to Los Angeles without backtracking. It is recommended to arrive two days early, so when you reach Los Angeles you can participate in the Chinese Spring Festival celebration activities. San Francisco's Golden Gate Bridge is one of the most photographed American landscapes. Many domestic TV dramas and movies such as "Dad's Army" and "Legend of Bruce Lee" have recreated the charm of San Francisco. A San Francisco city pass and a boat cruise can satisfy all your fantasies about this city. Some people say San Francisco is old, but some just like this feeling - it is a nostalgic familiar taste. Take the old-fashioned cable cars of San Francisco and savor the unique charm of this city. At the intersection of Powell Street and Market Street in downtown, even in technologically advanced America, you can still see the historical scene of electric trains being turned manually by people. Transportation: Take a bus to the Santa Fe Depot station Recommendation B: Old San Diego Suitable for: Those who appreciate humanities and geography Located on the U.S.-Mexico border, this is one of the first settlements where Europeans set foot overseas. This historic city brims with rich Mexican atmosphere - you can experience its cultural landscape without traveling to Mexico, plus enjoy authentic Mexican cuisine. Address: 2383 San Diego Avenue, San Diego CA 92110 Transportation: Take buses 8, 9, 10, 20, or 30 to reach Old Town San Diego Old Town San Diego San Diego's history is filled with turmoil and bloodshed. This area was originally a Native American gathering place, then occupied by Spaniards, later became part of Mexico, until after the American Revolutionary War territory control changed hands again. Precisely because of this complex history, San Diego has a culture that blends Mexican and Spanish influences, and Old Town is its best representation. Old Town is only minutes away from downtown on foot. Fountains adorn the streets here; uniquely distinctive shops are surrounded by flowered courtyards. City hall, post office, restaurants, schools, and churches are all concentrated in this area. The Indian Cultural Village within Old Town preserves 19th-century village architecture, with staff members wearing period costumes. Every Saturday there is a market event where you can purchase handicrafts and authentic local snacks. There are also many historical museums throughout Old Town. Recommended C: Spanish Art Village of Santa Barbara - A true revival of ancient Spanish villages The Spanish Cultural Arts Village is located within America's largest urban cultural park, Balboa Park. This park features 14 top-tier museums, 4 theaters, a complex stadium, rose gardens, and Japanese-style gardens. However, many visitors are unaware that deep in the park lies this Spanish Art Village. Allegedly, this is a true recreation of an ancient Spanish village. However, now these cottages have all been rented out by artists as their own studios. This place has become paradise for artists, with the entire village gathering makers of oil paintings, glass works, watercolors, sculptures, engravings, and various other types of artists. Address: 1549 El Prado, San Diego Transportation: Take bus route 7 to Park Bl & Zoo Pl stop. Tickets: Entry is free; certain museums within the park require separate tickets. High Roller Ferris Wheel This world's largest Ferris wheel had a $550 million investment and is located near Caesars Palace on Las Vegas Boulevard. It stands 30 meters taller than London's "London Eye," 9 meters higher than China's "Nanchang Star," and 2.7 meters above Singapore's "Flyer." A full ride on this Ferris wheel takes about 30 minutes. The entire structure features 28 fully glass-enclosed air-conditioned cabins, each capable of holding up to 40 people. 2. Grand Canyon: One of the Seven Wonders of the World, America's Grand Canyon is renowned worldwide and is a place many people consider essential to visit when they go to the US. You can take a helicopter for an aerial view of the canyon's magnificent landscape, kayak at the canyon floor, or ride mules through the valley to experience authentic American Western cowboy culture. In short, the Grand Canyon offers not only superb natural scenery but also comprehensive ways to explore it. The Grand Canyon is divided into West Rim and South Rim. If you ask me which one is better, I can only say: for pure natural landscapes, choose the South Rim; for a 360-degree complete canyon experience combined with learning about Native American culture, the West Rim is recommended. Ultimately, it depends on which one is more convenient for you. Address: 3545 S Las Vegas Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89109, USA Opening Hours: Daily 11:30–02:00 Transportation: Via Monorail Las Vegas Monorail Line—get off at Harrah's / The Linq Station or Flamingo / Caesars Palace Station Grand Canyon National Park · Grand Canyon National Park is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World, located in northwestern Arizona, divided into two main sections - the South Rim and North Rim, each with its own unique features. · The South Rim is the most visited route by tourists, and its self-drive road is built quite perfectly; the North Rim has higher elevation with thinner air, but the sky is as blue and clear as a wash. · Although not the deepest canyon in the world, its grandeur and stunning scenery rightfully make it number one, being one of America's most worthwhile national parks to visit. Conclusion This route covers the four classic cities in western United States. San Francisco was chosen as the first stop because along Highway 1, you can drive directly to Los Angeles without having to turn back.
