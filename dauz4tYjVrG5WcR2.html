Six Flags Magic Mountain I. Roller coasters you can ride standing, lying down, hanging, crawling, or upside-down Six Flags Magic Mountain, located in Valencia city in northern Los Angeles, is a large theme park. It opened in 1971 and covers an area of 262 acres. In 2011, it broke the world record for having the most inverted roller coasters in one park with 18 rides. Most of the top 10 roller coasters worldwide come from here, leaving other theme parks far behind. For Six Flags Magic Mountain, located in northern Los Angeles, truly obsessed coaster enthusiasts consider this place a great holy land, where it won the award for the world's largest roller coaster amusement park with nearly 20 roller coasters. Walking through the park, screams are heard everywhere, with grinding sounds between tracks and wheels squeaking. Every attraction challenges people's adrenaline, stimulating human limits of endurance. The worlds first 4D roller coaster Green Lantern (Green Lantern: First Flight), the nearly 5km long America's longest roller coaster Goliath, the world's second highest drop tower Drop of Doom at a height of 126 meters, and various scream machines bring visitors an unprecedented weightlessness experience, as if about to leave earths gravity. Even after the game ends, people remain unsettled for a long time. What does it feel like to experience 17 roller coasters in one visit? Six Flags Magic Mountain houses nine themed parks within its grounds, making it an ideal destination for thrill-seeking tourists. With a dazzling array of sky-high rides spanning land, sea, and air, there's something exciting around every corner that never gets old. There are so many roller coasters here that you simply won't have time to ride them all in one day! With 17 different coaster designs, visitors will exclaim how exhilarating it is. The sheer number of roller coasters is impressive, so if you're visiting Los Angeles and enjoy thrilling rides, Six Flags Magic Mountain is an absolute must-visit destination. Theme Park Attractions Introduction + Must-Visit Popular List 1. Most Popular Attractions - Top 3 ① Full Throttle The world's tallest looping coaster is a noiseless launch high-speed roller coaster, also the largest coaster globally. As an upright seating ride like most common coasters, it features very fast speeds with dual-track design - starting on the inner loop and ending on the outer loop by returning to the start point. Midway it pauses in a tunnel, then reverses before suddenly accelerating forward - extremely thrilling, worth experiencing multiple times if time permits. ② Goliath As the tallest giant steel roller coaster in the entire park at 255 feet (approximately 78 meters) high, when reaching the peak you completely cannot see the track ahead - it's nearly vertical to the ground. Even sitting in the front row gives zero visibility. The full ride lasts 48 seconds with two drops; as you approach the bottom, there's a phenomenon resembling a black hole creating intense depth of weightlessness - truly suspenseful. Tips: Height-phobic riders should exercise caution since all passengers must reach their peak before the descent begins. ③ Twisted Colossus A steel-wood hybrid roller coaster with dual-car racing theme - the name itself evokes twisting sensations. Based on the Giant Coaster built in 1978, it closed in 2014 and was rebuilt at its original site combining new steel structure with preserved wooden elements. After reopening in 2015 as Twisted Colossus, it became the world's longest mixed-material coaster (wood and steel combined) with a track length of 5000 feet (approximately 1500 meters). This roller coaster has a run time of 48 seconds. Its biggest highlight is the first "High Five" in the Western Hemisphere, where two trains pass each other at extreme tilt and rotation, giving you an illusion that you're five times taller than everyone else. 2. Top 3 Most Thrilling Attractions ① X2 X2 is a horizontal roller coaster featuring 360-degree rotating seats as the world's only five-dimensional experience. In terms of intensity, it is one of the largest projects in scale. As a high-popularity attraction within the park, queue times are generally quite long. At the start of the ride, riders lie down in their seats with music enhancing the atmosphere around them. Besides rotating along the track, each rider's seat also tilts and turns following the track angle throughout the journey, making it difficult to discern the path ahead. This creates a very different experience from normal roller coasters, with an overall extremely thrilling feeling. Tips: You need to store your bags here; you can rent one for 2 hours for $1 USD. ② Tatsu Located next to New Revolution, this is a flying coaster themed around a "dragon." It is also one of the three "most extreme" roller coasters (tallest, fastest, and longest flying coaster). During the ride, riders are in a prone position; when you extend your arms, it feels as though you're really like Superman soaring through the blue sky. Because it eliminates the sensation of weightlessness, this attraction is deeply loved by everyone. Plus, you can enjoy beautiful California scenery during the ride—worth trying~ ③ LEX LUTHOR: Drop of Doom This is the world's highest drop tower—the type we commonly call a jump tower—with a height of 400 feet (120 meters) above ground. It lets you experience the intense thrill of free-falling from a 10-story building's height. 3. Other Attractions ① The New Revolution North America's first coaster with VR technology. While it is one of the less enjoyable coasters in the park on its own, adding VR equipment clearly elevates the entire experience. You can even shoot using buttons located on the side of the VR display. It runs for 48 seconds—twice as long as typical coasters elsewhere. ② Scream A floorless coaster. As the name suggests, once it departs, the floor is removed. The entire ride hangs in mid-air above the track (maintaining some distance). The complete run lasts 54 seconds. It delivers a relatively traditional thrill without special design elements—basic rolling and dropping—but you may still feel mild dizziness afterward. ③ Green Lantern: First Flight A spinning coaster with unique vertical tracks. The seats rotate 360° based on each passenger's body weight and momentum. ④ Batman This is a Batman-themed suspended coaster, also known as an inverted roller coaster. With numerous loops, high speeds, and the upside-down riding position, it feels quite unique, making you feel like you're in a Batman movie. ⑤ The Riddler's Revenge This is a standing roller coaster with shoulder restraints where you remain standing throughout most of the ride, putting strain on your legs. After boarding, you need to adjust the safety harness height and secure it properly before the ride begins. The entire experience lasts 54 seconds. Due to the standing position, hills, drops, and turns provide moderate excitement, but the format is still very worth experiencing. ⑥ Viper This is a standard seated roller coaster with a moderate overall height. It features seven inversions at speeds that aren't particularly slow. However, user experience is relatively poor because seats are quite hard. With many turns and loops plus significant bumping and twisting throughout the ride, it's easy to bump or twist yourself. ⑦ SUPERMAN: Escape from Krypton The Superman attraction shares the same tower structure as the drop tower ride. Superman represents one of the tallest individual attractions at Disney California Adventure. Reaching speeds of 100 mph (160 km/h), the Superman car is launched upside down to the top of the tower, then slides downward like Superman flying through the air. The overall duration was relatively short. The sense of weightlessness wasn't as intense as the drop tower, but the instant thrill is still worth experiencing. ⑧ Apocalypse A wooden roller coaster described as a "classic woodie roller coaster" (classic traditional coaster). While it sounds old-fashioned, once you ride it you'll discover the speed isn't traditional at all, so be mentally prepared before boarding. ⑨ Ninja The track is low to the ground, and while the speed isn't slow, there are no looping sections. The ride operates by rocking side-to-side, so those prone to motion sickness should choose carefully. ⑩ Jet Stream Essentially a traditional log flume ride designed to soak you through. Includes a boat viewing segment, followed by a 57-foot vertical drop in the boat section. ⑪ Roaring Rapids A family-friendly attraction where children can also participate. It simulates the rush of Colorado rapids rushing downward, providing an exceptionally cool and thrilling experience. The excitement level is moderately intense, making it perfect for summer visits. ⑫ Tidal Wave A water slide featuring a 50-foot vertical drop free-fall waterfall (also in the log flume category). ⑬ CraZanity Added in 2017, this is "the world's highest pendulum ride," taking riders to a height of 172 feet (52.4 meters), with speeds reaching up to 75 miles per hour (120.7 km/h). Tips: Is one day enough to experience all attractions? On average, you can do about 12 attractions in one day. Actually, if you really want to ride more, you might manage around 15, but riding roller coasters continuously all day will make your head spin~ Six. Transportation Options Six Flags Magic Mountain is located in northern Los Angeles. Because it spans across regions, taking public transportation can be relatively complicated. So if conditions allow, we recommend renting a car or driving yourself. 1. Driving Driving from the downtown area covers about 50 kilometers. Without traffic jams, you can arrive in just 40 minutes. Parking: Parking requires payment at $25 per session. The parking lot is some distance from the park entrance; you could also park your car at nearby hotels. Tips: Be sure to remember where you parked - which zone, and your horizontal and vertical position - otherwise finding your car when leaving will waste a lot of time. 2. Bus/Metro 1. Take the red line to the northern terminal station North Hollywood; 2. After getting off, follow the signs indicating SC757 at McBean Regional Transit Station to alight; Tips: The platform is easy to find; there are no vehicle numbers, only bus signs. Departure times are not fixed, so you need to spend some time waiting for the MRTC (McBean Regional Transit Center) bus. 3. Upon arriving at MRTC, transfer to bus 3 or 7 (the two buses operate on alternating schedules - arriving one is route 3, returning is route 7, but both run round-trip between Magic Mountain and MRTC) heading to Magic Mountain (Santa Clarita). 757 Bus Route Map 757 Bus Schedule 757 Bus Route Map Bus 3 Schedule Bus 3 Route Map & Schedule Tips: Remember to calculate your travel time when going to and from Los Angeles.
