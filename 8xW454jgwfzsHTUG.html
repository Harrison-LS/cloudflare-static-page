Do you have any place you've been longing to visit for a very long time? For me, Salvation Mountain is one of those places! I first learned about this place two years ago when I saw it in a clothing store on Taobao. The vibrant colors and cute patterns immediately caught my eye, especially combined with the unique story behind this place... The Story of Salvation Mountain Salvation Mountain is an artificially created art installation (Wikipedia describes it as: art installation). Its creator, Leonard Knight, was born in 1931. At age 36, he became a Christian. Following that, his "mountain-building" movement began. This construction effort lasted for 28 years. The mountain is built from concrete and artificial clay bricks. The surface is painted with various colors of water-based paint. Under the rough heat of the sun, some colors have peeled off, and the paint appears melted and distorted. Yet, such a Salvation Mountain still leaves people in awe. One person One car One cat With unwavering original intention for 28 years He painted his faith in God, love, and life Where is Salvation Mountain? Salvation Mountain (Salvation Mountain) is located in Southern California, about 150 miles east of San Diego. Driving from Los Angeles takes about 3 hours. It is located in a desert area, surrounded by barren landscapes. But it is precisely because of experiencing this endless desert that seeing Salvation Mountain becomes even more moving. First Experience at Salvation Mountain An almost completely blind old man spent 28 years building Salvation Mountain all by himself. After hearing this story, I was deeply moved. Coming here and seeing these lovely paintings again, full of innocence - what a pure soul it takes to create such scenes. To express his love for God, the old man wrote "God is love" throughout the mountain structure. He believed that love exists everywhere, hoping people could spread love and make the world better. Nowadays, this place has become a popular photo destination, with more and more tourists visiting. Although it becomes difficult to capture panoramic shots without people, once you are here, you will still feel the love Salvation Mountain brings. Scattered around Salvation Mountain are many vehicles - these were all tools he used during construction. The truck I chose for photography was his home in the desert; he lived inside that truck. There was no electricity, running water, or air conditioning in the desert, and a stray cat was his only companion. Things to Note There are now volunteers maintaining this place, so please do not damage it when taking photos. The mountain has designated yellow paths where you can walk - elsewhere, kindly avoid touching anything unnecessarily. 1. Don't wear high heels; there are people up there who enforce a rule forbidding them on the mountain. 2. Redemption Mountain sits east-facing west, so morning photos come out with strong backlighting. It's recommended to visit in the afternoon for sunset photos—they're very beautiful. 3. I visited during winter and arrived around 3:30 PM. I didn't feel particularly hot. People say summer visits make the sun feel especially scorching, so everyone should prepare proper sun protection and bring plenty of water. Do you have faith?
