Content

The movie "La La Land" (爱乐之城) is a comedy musical released in mid-December 2016, telling the love story between a jazz pianist (Sebastian) and an aspiring actress (Mia). In 2017, it won six Academy Awards at the 89th Oscars including Best Actress and Best Director, attracting worldwide attention and captivating audiences. The film was shot at multiple locations in Los Angeles, with Griffith Observatory being one of the most famous. Griffith Observatory is one of LA's most renowned cultural attractions, having appeared in numerous TV shows and films. Its most notable appearance was in the 1955 classic "Rebel Without a Cause," starring James Dean and Natalie Wood. To pay homage to this iconic film, Mia and Sebastian from La La Land also visited the observatory and performed an unforgettable romantic scene at Samuel Oschin Planetarium, which has become one of the movie's most memorable moments. Griffith Observatory, located on a hilltop near the Hollywood sign, is one of the best spots for tourists to take photos. The open-air platform at the top offers views of the entire Los Angeles city - with orderly streets, neat buildings, and downtown skyscrapers all visible at once. Witnessing the red sun setting into the sea from the platform during sunset time has a unique charm. Visiting the Observatory The observatory showcases astronomical knowledge about eight major planets and celestial bodies in the universe. Every evening when night falls, the stargazing area equipped with giant astronomical telescopes opens free of charge to the public. Visitors can clearly see stars in the sky and even the surface contours of the moon, fully experiencing the magic of the astronomical world. The dome theater at the top is built to resemble the sky. Tourists recline on their seats to watch films about celestial evolution from a 360° perspective. Visitors feel as if they are immersed in the vast universe. Naked-eye 3D technology makes cosmic wonders appear within reach, right beside them. The Lighthouse Cafe La La Land still. by WendyW The Light House Cafe is located at Hermosa Beach and has been a jazz music venue since May 1949. Legendary jazz musicians Miles Davis, Chet Baker, Art Blakey, and Cannonball Adderley all performed here. In the film, this is the jazz restaurant where the main couple went for their first date. Address: 30 Pier Ave, Hermosa Beach, CA 90254 Hermosa Beach As one of LA's eight most beautiful beaches, Hermosa Pier stretches along the Pacific Ocean, making it a popular spot for fishing and watching sunsets. After leaving the Lighthouse Café, Sebastian walked along the pier, singing "City of Stars." Hermosa Beach served as a filming location for the movie "La La Land," where Mia and Sebastian shared romantic moments near the pier. Close by stands the Hermosa Beach Pier, a favorite date spot for couples. The nearby restaurants offer stunning ocean views, perfect for enjoying a romantic candlelit dinner while watching the sunset glow. Angels Flight Railway In montage scenes, Mia and Sebastian rode on the iconic Angels Flight railway - the Hill Street line in downtown Los Angeles. While this historic funicular has been out of service since 2013, the producers managed to secure permission for a one-day filming location shoot. Built in 1901, Angels Flight was originally part of the hill street railway system in downtown LA's urban landscape. Riding Angels Flight Railway, you can feel the strong retro charm of downtown Los Angeles. Address: 351 S Hill St, Los Angeles, CA 90013 Angels Flight Angels Flight is the filming location for the movie "La La Land." In the film, Mia and Sebastian have a romantic date, sharing sweet kisses in the wooden carriages. Angels Flight began operation as early as 1901, making it the world's shortest railway. Tourists can ride Angels Flight to experience this brief and enchanting journey with its retro orange-yellow and black painted body and wooden carriages.
