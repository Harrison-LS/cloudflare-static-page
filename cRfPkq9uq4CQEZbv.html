Two Pictures to Understand Los Angeles Los Angeles as We Speak of It Los Angeles generally refers to Los Angeles County, a vast area comprising 88 cities, such as Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, Long Beach, and downtown Los Angeles. However, when we mention going to Los Angeles for tourism, we usually refer to the greater Los Angeles region, which includes surrounding counties like Orange County and Riverside County, among five counties in total. Many famous attractions are located here, such as Disney Land in Orange County and the Desert Outlet Mall in Riverside County. Getty Center Introduction: The Getty Center is a significant cultural landmark in Los Angeles and one of the most renowned museums worldwide, particularly famous for its extensive art collections. It houses an impressive array of European paintings, sculptures, decorative arts, and photography that attracts art enthusiasts from around the globe. Not only that, but the Getty Center building itself is also a work of art. Visitors take a miniature train up the hill, enjoying panoramic views of the entire valley along the way. Upon exiting the museum lies the colorful Getty Central Garden, equally famous as the artworks inside. Tree-lined paths flow with gentle streams that converge into ponds along the trails. At the center of each pond sits an azalea maze. Artistic atmosphere pervades every corner. Address: 1200 Getty Center Dr, Los Angeles, CA 90049 Admission Price: Free Opening Hours: Tuesday-Friday and Sunday 10:00-17:30; Saturday 10:00-21:00; Closed on Mondays Transportation: Visitors can drive or take bus route 234 to the Sepulveda / Getty Center station. Parking is available at a lot at the foot of the hill, then ride the tram up and down to the art center atop the hill. Official Website: *** 2. The Last Bookstore Introduction: One of North America's top ten independent bookstores and among the world's most beautiful bookstores, this establishment is located in downtown Los Angeles and carries the reputation as "Los Angeles' Best Bookstore." The interior features antique-style decoration with vintage bookshelves filled with nostalgic atmosphere. Starry hanging lights add decorative charm, creating a magical academy ambiance. Moreover, artistic photo spots including book caves and book windows are thoughtfully arranged inside this book lover's paradise, making it an Instagram-worthy destination for artsy travelers. Address: 453 S Spring St, Los Angeles, CA 90013 Ticket Price: Free Opening Hours: Friday-Saturday 10:00am-11:00pm, Other Days 10:00am-10:00pm Transportation: Take Metro Purple Line or Red Line to Pershing Square Station, then walk about 3 minutes to arrive Website: *** 3. Los Angeles County Museum of Art Introduction: The Los Angeles County Museum of Art is also one of Los Angeles' tourist attractions, especially the "City Lights" lamp post groups at the entrance. Founded in 1961, it claims to be the largest contemporary art collection since World War II in America. It has always been dedicated to collecting artworks encompassing various fields such as history and geography, showcasing LA's unique multicultural character. As the largest art museum on the US West Coast, it houses over 110,000 artistic treasures from ancient Egyptian times onward, masterpieces by European masters, excellent Asian art collections, artifacts from North and South America, Islamic and marginalized modern art, etc., with high aesthetic value. Address: 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Los Angeles, CA 90036 Ticket Price: Adults $15, Seniors/Students $10, Children Free Opening Hours: Mon/Tue/Thu 11:00-17:00, Fri 11:00-20:00, Sat/Sun 10:00-19:00, Wed Closed Transportation: Take bus #20 to Wilshire Blvd. & Ogden Ave. station Website: *** 4. The Broad Museum Overview: Located on Grand Avenue in downtown Los Angeles, The Broad Museum is extremely popular. The collection includes outstanding contemporary artworks from around the world, as well as "Infinity Mirrored Room - Soul Beyond a Million Light Years" (Infinity Mirrored Room) that made this museum famous, created by Yayoi Kusama. This mirrored room is decorated with LED colored lights that shimmer like the vast galaxy, making visitors feel as if they're in the Milky Way, leaving them shocked and amazed. Address: 221 S Grand Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90012 Ticket Price: Free Opening Hours: Tuesday-Wednesday 11:00-17:00, Thursday-Saturday 11:00-20:00, Sunday 10:00-18:00, Closed on Mondays Transportation: Take the Purple Line, White Line, or Gray Line to Grand Ave and 2nd St W station Official Website: *** 5. Here, you can view your favorite celebrities and famous film personalities up close, from golden age movie stars to today's contemporary celebrities and filmmakers - all are available here. Official Website: *** 2. OUE Skyspace LA - California's Highest Observation Deck Introduction: OUE Skyspace is California's highest open-air observation deck, located inside the Bank of America Tower in downtown Los Angeles. At over 1,000 feet above ground level, it offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the entire Angel City with no blind spots. Here, you can not only find a small window-side sofa indoors to quietly admire the sunset, but also go outdoors for an intimate encounter with the surrounding skyscrapers. The outdoor observation deck features floor-to-ceiling transparent glass surrounding all sides, offering excellent panoramic views. It also includes a viral 70-story suspended transparent slide and angel wings photo spots - making it a must-visit new landmark when exploring Los Angeles. Address: 633 W 5th St #840, Los Angeles, CA 90071 Ticket prices: Adult ticket $25, Child ticket $19. Ticket + slide combo: Adult $33, Child $27. Opening hours: 10:00-22:00 Transportation: Take buses 16, 17, 18, 53, 55, 62, 316, or 355 to the 5th & Grand stop, then walk one minute. Whale and Dolphin Watching Tour from Harbor Introduction: Departing from the port at the southern tip of Los Angeles, you can experience a unique whale-watching and dolphin-watching journey. The temperature is comfortable year-round with whales visible throughout all seasons. Gray whales are most commonly seen in winter and spring, while blue whales appear most frequently in summer and fall. If lucky, you might even encounter groups of whales. This area also offers excellent opportunities to spot kingfishers, otters, sea turtles, dolphins, and sea lions. With professional captains and crew members leading the way, you are guaranteed an unforgettable experience. Parking lots in western America are much more numerous and larger than those in the east, so even within the city center you won't worry about finding parking spaces. Tourist attractions also have dedicated parking areas, which is very convenient. Uber Besides self-driving, using an app to hail a ride is the most convenient option with the best value for money. For exploring within the city center, this is a good choice. Metro Metro is the general term for Los Angeles public transportation, consisting of subways, light rail, and buses. There are 6 lines in total for subway and light rail combined, with the Red Line and Light Blue Line being the most commonly used. The one-way fare is $1.75 USD. Accommodation Options Los Angeles is very large, and public transportation cannot reach every corner, so choosing where to stay is especially important. If you want to experience coastal living, it is recommended to stay near Santa Monica. Those seeking trendy experiences and famous hotels should consider the Beverly Hills area. Beverly Hills: This area features numerous attractions concentrated in one location, with abundant options for shopping and dining. The transportation system is well-developed, making it the annual venue for the Academy Awards. Though Beverly Hills is a renowned affluent district, hotel prices remain reasonable. The surrounding accommodation landscape is mature and safe, offering everything from convenient hotels to ultra-luxury stays. Koreatown: Located in downtown Los Angeles, this area has fewer attractions but offers more affordable hotel rates. It's conveniently positioned—Beverly Hills and Santa Monica are not far to the west, downtown is nearby to the east, and Hollywood lies just north. The similar distances make it ideal for travelers driving themselves. Plus, Koreatown boasts an extensive selection of dining options that stay open late into the evening. Santa Monica: This is the most popular beach destination locally, perfect for visitors seeking a vacation atmosphere and coastal lifestyle experience.
