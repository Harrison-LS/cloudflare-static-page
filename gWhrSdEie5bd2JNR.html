What is worth buying at Los Angeles International Airport?

Los Angeles

Brief

Content

LAX International Airport has N terminals, but international routes are concentrated at Tom Bradley International Terminal (TBIT) and T2. My first flight with Aeromexico to Mexico City was from Terminal 2 - it really is tiny! Two duty-free stores, two convenience stores, two small restaurants! Tom Bradley International Terminal (TBIT) is much bigger - all kinds of duty-free shops, various luxury brand stores, all kinds of restaurants... All three major Chinese airlines board here, you can imagine the purchasing power of Chinese travelers. A salesperson told me this terminal was built with investment from LV's boss, originally doing business with Japanese customers, then Koreans, and now China is the absolute main force. Each store must have a Chinese-speaking salesman; some even speak several languages. Inside Airport Estee Lauder, Clinique, Kiehl's, Fresh - many brands are much cheaper than at home, plus gifts for purchases over a certain amount. One Estee Lauder set comes with a cosmetic bag and samples; Kiehl's Calendula Toner 52 USD in large size, while Double 11 at home also requires 500... Also, buying alcohol is very cheap, super variety; chocolates can be stocked up, especially Godiva. As for electronics, not recommended - prices aren't cheap, plus warranty issues. Duty-Free Store Small gifts are perfect for giving to neighbors and local residents. Restaurants were available after security screening, but we chose to dine outside where it was less crowded. T2 is truly tiny... What's Worth Buying. Overall, buying skincare products here remains an excellent choice.
