Address: 8221 Melrose Avenue, West Hollywood This all-pink wall satisfied the photo-taking desires of all girls, gay best friends, tourists, and even influencer aunts, becoming one of the most photographed graffiti walls on Instagram with the most hashtags. Photography Guide It's an intersection with no covering around it; if sunlight is very strong, this tests your photography skills quite a bit. Since one side of the wall is a parking lot, security personnel will stop you from using DSLRs for photos. Los Angeles afternoons have very glaring sun, so it's best to visit in the morning. Both full-body and close-up shots work well; taking good photos still requires skill—out of 100 photos, there should be a few satisfactory ones. Outfit Guide With a single-color pink background wall, it's recommended to wear solid colors. Black and white are the top choices. Bring sunglasses because Los Angeles sunlight is quite strong. Choose outfit combinations that make you look taller; your resulting photos will instantly resemble model shots. How to Pose | N Ways at the "Famous Wall" When taking photos in front of the "famous wall," any outfit and pose works fine, but if you want your pictures to be more aesthetic and unique, here are some ideas: Minimalist Outfit + Side Profile Shots You don't need elaborate or fashionable outfits - just everyday clothing that contrasts noticeably with the pink wall. Imagine yourself as a hurried passerby; capturing your side silhouette walking past the wall creates a natural, authentic street photography feel. Props + Dynamic Photos If you find struggling to pose too girl-next-door, a casual jump or leap in mid-air can greatly enhance your photos' coolness and movement; with props like balloons, ice cream, sunglasses adding that finishing touch, you can also create fascinating interactions with onlookers outside the frame. Queue Up + Group Photos Don't waste such a large creative space - call up your partner or a few girlfriends, best friends. Numbers make strength; together come up with silly poses or interesting formations that entertain yourselves and attract attention from passersby. Change your perspective + shoot still life photos Want to stand out from those generic headshots and full-body shots? Try changing your angle and focus on everyday details - your beloved pet dog, the cup of coffee you're holding, a succulent plant, or even the shadow cast on the wall. These can all become the star of your photo. When shooting, it's recommended to step back a bit further away. This allows more wall space in your frame and creates a more natural ratio between sky and ground that looks less awkward. For composition, try positioning your subject near the center of the image.
