What to Do in Western USA? 1) Natural Scenery [Highway 1] Highway 1 is a route that visitors to the American West will never want to miss - blue skies, ocean views, sunshine with dramatic cliffs, and black rocks scattered artfully among the crashing waves. From San Francisco heading south for about 40 minutes youll arrive at the famous Stanford University. Continue southwest driving one and a half hours more into Highway 1s premier small town - Monterey. This area features its enchanting 17-mile coastline, world-class luxury Cypress Point Golf Course, family-friendly educational aquariums, and the unique Cannery Row. Carmel, another quiet beautiful town nearby, is only about 20 minutes away by car. You can visit Carmel Mission, Carmel Plaza and other attractions along the way. South of Monterey lies the scenic Big Sur Park and the Danish-style town of Solvang before arriving at Santa Barbara. This city brims with Spanish charm; Stern Pier, the Coastline Park, and the Santa Barbara Mission are all enchanting destinations offering stunning views. There's also fresh seafood and Latin-style souvenirs to enjoy - a must-visit stop on your self-driving journey. Highway 1 Driving Tips: 1) Always drive this route from north to south - starting from San Francisco heading toward Los Angeles. The highway runs closer to the coastline when traveling in this direction. If you go the other way, most scenic overlooks will be inaccessible for stops. 2) Never rely on simple point-to-point automatic navigation from San Francisco to Los Angeles. You must set waypoints along your route because standard GPS often routes through Highway 101 instead of California's Route 1 - missing out on all the highlights. Many charming towns and natural attractions require manual location entry. Ideally, navigate waypoint by waypoint. Even if time is tight and you can't visit every attraction, still follow this approach to ensure you stay on California Highway 1 and don't miss coastal scenery. 3) The California sun is notoriously intense (especially during sunrise and sunset). Safety first - definitely bring a good pair of sunglasses. Polarized lenses are ideal for driving, but they can make navigation screens difficult to read, so the passenger should stay alert and focus on following the GPS directions while watching the road ahead. [Yellowstone National Park] Yellowstone's greatest appeal lies in its diverse landscapes: canyons, waterfalls, high mountains (snow-capped year-round), boreal forests, lakes, rivers, grasslands, plus unique geology shaped by volcanic activity. It truly represents a microcosm of America's national parks - containing the essence and spirit of all, with surprises waiting at every corner. Actually, Yellowstone National Park is open year-round, but during October through April of the following year, many roads within the park are closed, and numerous attractions shut down, making it very inconvenient. At that time, the park is not fully accessible (only partially open), so visitors might miss some sights. It is recommended to avoid visiting then. Generally from May to September, the park opens completely, making this the best time to visit Yellowstone. Yellowstone National Park has five main areas with concentrated attractions: 1) The geyser area in the southwest, featuring numerous geysers, hot springs, steam vents, mud pools, and fumaroles; 2) The northwest area primarily contains Norris Geyser Basin and Mammoth Hot Springs; 3) The central region is canyon country, home to Yellowstone Canyon and its waterfalls; 4) The northeast Roosevelt District preserves authentic Old West scenery; 5) The southeast features the Lake District, mainly including the West Thumb geyser basin and Yellowstone Lake. Transportation Tips: 1) Yellowstone has five entrances located on the east, west, south, north, and northeast sides. From Salt Lake City to Idaho Falls is Interstate 15, approximately a 4-hour drive at highway speeds, but with average scenery. From Idaho Falls to West Yellowstone is on regular highways with excellent road conditions and beautiful landscapes along the way that are quite pleasant to view. 2. Self-driving Precautions [Driver's License Notarization] Most US states recognize Chinese driver's licenses. However, since laws vary by state, usage conditions differ as well. For self-driving in California (well-known), Arizona (where Grand Canyon is located), Nevada (home to Las Vegas), and several states around Yellowstone National Park, you can use your new version of the Chinese driver's license directly. [Documents to Carry] Prepare in advance: - Original driver's licenses (obtained over 1 year ago and still valid) for all drivers - Notarized copies/translations of the license or International Driver's License for foreigners - International credit cards with sufficient limits in the main driver's name - Passports [Traffic Rules] 1. STOP SIGN: Many intersections in the US don't have traffic lights; drivers follow red STOP signs at these junctions to stop and proceed. When all four directions have STOP signs, whoever arrives first stops first, then proceeds when clear. If two vehicles arrive simultaneously from different directions, the vehicle on the right has priority. 2. SPEED LIMIT: When you see a SPEED LIMIT sign, it's best to strictly follow it as a guideline. However, in practice, US traffic law judges based on actual road conditions. This means that even if SPEED LIMIT is 60 mph, most cars drive at 80 mph, so driving at 80 would be acceptable in such cases. How do you determine if you're speeding? The best strategy is to "go with the flow" - maintain roughly the same speed as others around you, and you won't encounter problems. Additionally, America has almost no surveillance cameras, though police officers often hide along roadsides. If you realize you're driving ahead of all vehicles, or significantly faster than cars beside you, then a mishap is probably imminent. 3. Right Turns at Red Lights: In America, right turns on red are permitted, but if you think it's the same as in China where you can just turn immediately, you'd be very wrong. The prerequisite for right turning on red is "MAKE COMPLETE STOP" - meaning you must come to a complete stop before turning right. You need to park first, confirm safety, then make the right turn. Some intersections have NO RIGHT TURN ON RED signs, so you cannot turn right on red there. Remember that even if there's not a single person on the road, when encountering a red light sign, you must stop first before turning right. The secret is "stop at red light, silently count to three seconds, then turn right." [Parking] Parking I consider an important part of renting cars. When parking, pay special attention to signs - it's best not to miss any sign because mistakes are likely. By the road, sign indicators, ground markings. In big cities' popular areas, you can choose parking lots to park, which is more convenient, safe, and easy to operate. 1. Roadside Parking: If there are people in the car, it doesn't count as parking. So when going to small shops by the roadside to buy drinks or snacks, you can leave one person in the car; parking where the road edge is painted red means no parking at any time. When the road edge has no color and there's no ground marking or parking sign, you can park temporarily for a while, but be careful - avoid blocking garage entrances and small inclines on the road. 2. Parking Meters: Parking at meters is more economical for shorter durations. Credit cards such as MasterCard and Visa are generally accepted. First select your desired parking time, then pay with your credit card to start the timer. 3. Parking Garages/Lots: Suitable for longer parking needs or when roadside parking isn't available in busy major cities. These require payment and come in two types. The first type uses direct staffed payment. The second is self-service parking, where you must pay using a credit card at the machine before leaving your vehicle. The process involves first inserting the parking ticket into the machine, then inserting your credit card. After removing it, the screen displays the parking fee. Once payment is successfully processed, the parking ticket pops out. Take this ticket to drive away from the garage. V.
