# Recommended Must-Try Attractions at Disneyland California - Beware of the Terrifying Elevator If You're Easily Scared!

Los Angeles

Brief

Content

The United States has two Disney Parks. One is located in Orlando, Florida - the world's largest Disney Park, so big you'd absolutely not be able to finish it in 3 days and nights. Today we're talking about LA, California, built in 1955 as the world's first Disney park. The entire park is divided into two parts, like two neighboring parks. One half is called "Disneyland," while the other is called "Adventure Land." The former has attractions more suitable for children, while the latter is more thrilling and better suited for adults. Must Stay: LA Disney Hotel! Unlike Shanghai's version, this Disney hotel connects directly to the park - wake up naturally, sunbathe, walk a few steps and you're back to having fun! If visiting in winter, as soon as you enter the hotel lobby, a huge dreamy Christmas tree catches your eye. The room offers stunning views of the park's attractions. A temperature-controlled massage pool provides exceptional comfort, with the iconic drop tower visible from the distance. While queues are unavoidable at Disney parks, visitors will appreciate how meticulously clean and well-maintained these facilities remain compared to other theme parks. Staff members deliver genuine warmth throughout - from performers to security personnel, everyone maintains a friendly demeanor that creates an uplifting atmosphere. Their authentic smiles reflect true happiness in their work environment. Must Play #1: Ferris Wheel Why am I introducing this "boring" Ferris wheel? Wrong! Wrong! Wrong! This Ferris wheel combines both thrill and serenity. Take a close look at the hub—around the outer edge are regular seats, while those seats on the inner rim are the 3.0 upgraded version. They slide down continuously along winding and twisting paths at high speed, and you'll occasionally hear screams from neighboring riders! My friend and I were shaking uncontrollably with fear, but the calm six-year-old blonde girl sitting opposite calmly asked her parents if it wasn't exciting enough and if we could try the freefall tower instead? What!? Foreign kids really have big courage! Anyway, this is genuinely fun! Must-ride #2: Super Fast Roller Coaster For roller coaster enthusiasts, this is the complete version since it must do 360-degree turns! The cars launch at incredible speed without any warning—this sudden start is exactly what makes roller coasters so thrilling. The ride lasts nearly two and a half minutes, which is relatively long. After getting off, your legs will be shaking uncontrollably. Taken while riding, please do not imitate! Check it out for yourself. Must-ride #3: Lightning McQueen Race This attraction suits all ages. It's an upgraded version of Shanghai Little Flyer with a touch more excitement. At the beginning, you'll ride small yellow carts into a beautiful racing factory and a cozy indoor family area. In the second half, as the doors open wide, your vehicle will rush outside into a steep arena. This attraction is incredibly addictive! Quick tip: During acceleration and tilting, make sure to hold on tight - watch out for back strain! Ready to go! Cars Land The gas station crew's lip movements and expressions are remarkably lifelike - they're ready for the race! With slopes and inclines, it feels great! Must-try #4: California Soaring Journey This attraction is actually the "grandfather" of Shanghai Disney's popular "Soaring Over the Horizon," with Shanghai simply adapting the content to feature global scenery. The 4D visuals are incredibly realistic - you'll feel like you're on a helicopter experiencing California's endless charming landscapes. Queue time is approximately 60 minutes. Since this project is very similar to Soaring Over the Horizon, I wouldn't recommend riding both - the queue time doesn't offer great value! Optional #5: The Haunted Elevator Drop tower meets haunted house. Operating in complete darkness, you experience rapid weightlessness as you plunge down, accompanied by eerie sounds and ghostly figures. Overall, it creates a feeling of utter despair. Those with small courage should absolutely not try this haha. Must-See Six: Color Show Water Curtain Spectacle California's water curtain show is a major signature attraction you simply cannot see at other theme parks. Combining amazing sound effects, lasers, and the water curtain to present a 30-minute grand performance, it is absolutely must-see! Every time this happens, tourists always crowd in three layers thick inside and out - remember to find a good spot in advance! The American Disney is truly enormous. While Shanghai's is also quite large, there are relatively fewer attractions open at any given time, requiring long treks between each attraction, leaving you very exhausted after a day. America's characteristic lies in having abundant attractions packed closely together - along the way filled with character interactions and parade floats, so even while queuing, there is entertainment!
