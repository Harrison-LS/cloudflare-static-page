Los Angeles

Content

As the home base for celebrities and influencers, Los Angeles perfectly embodies a fun and photogenic way to travel. Hollywood adds a cinematic filter to this city – buildings on every street corner are filled with vibrant colors. A film like LA LA LAND made Los Angeles' dazzling night skyline famous worldwide. Moreover, this place is the birthplace of street photography, featuring countless cool influencer walls (photo spots). This guide will take you to explore 6 popular photo spots in Los Angeles – both fun and great for photography. With practical tips included, it adds unique enjoyment to your LA trip. Melrose Avenue 💡 Highlight: Hub of Influencer Walls Melrose Avenue by Tang Bu Zhi Xiao Jiang Melrose Avenue is the favorite shopping district for trendy locals in Los Angeles. Here you'll find countless upscale shops, designer showrooms, vintage boutiques, healthy light food restaurants, and more. Mixed among these stores are various famous influencer photo walls. It's recommended to first enjoy a leisurely brunch, then spend half a day exploring and taking photos along the way. Smile Wall The Smile Wall by Emily🌻 Anya Hindmarch is a luxury brand store that has become a popular photo spot for tourists due to the eye-catching yellow laughing emoji displayed in its window, jokingly nicknamed "the smile wall". However, the window displays change with themes; currently, it now features red hearts instead of the original yellow smileys. Highly recommended is Alfed Tea, a famous milk tea shop next door, which not only boasts impressive cherry blossom pink decor but also serves excellent-tasting milk teas. Paul Smith Pink Wall Pink Wall (Paul Smith) via Instagram Paul Smith can be considered one of LA's most popular photo walls, with the eye-catching pink facade looking great in any weather. One side of the wall faces a parking lot where the color changes frequently - it was even painted rainbow-colored at the beginning of 2018. The street-facing side maintains classic pink for a long time. MADE IN LA Wall MADE IN LA Wall via Instagram @carol.eats MADE IN LA is an exterior wall of a Cisco Home furniture store on Melrose Avenue, featuring a sleek gray industrial-style facade that stands out uniquely against the vibrant street colors. Unlike other Instagrammable walls, this one is quite tall; photographers are recommended to shoot from across the street - allowing for full-body portraits while capturing the complete graffiti art. Rainbow Wall The rainbow at the diagonally opposite Rainbow Wall is particularly special - rather than being painted on with pigment, it's woven from different colored hemp ropes. This material adds a sense of depth when photographed, resulting in better photo quality. You can try standing under the rainbow archway and changing poses randomly, then taking 5 consecutive shots. Photography Tips: Recommended visiting time: 10:00-12:00. In the morning there are fewer tourists and ample natural lighting, making it ideal for photography. Please note that Paul Smith is extremely popular and will have security stationed there - they may prohibit using DSLRs (single-lens reflex cameras) and only allow phone photography. 📍 Location: Melrose Ave., Los Angeles, CA, 90046 Melrose Avenue Melrose Avenue is a shopping street where young people in Los Angeles gather, featuring many local specialty boutiques selling unique and rare items as well as avant-garde clothing stores. In addition, major brand stores, art galleries, vintage shops, and upscale restaurants are everywhere. When you're tired from walking around, finding a restaurant is never difficult. Overall, this place has everything you want. OUE Skyspace Observation Deck 💡Highlight: Highest observation deck in Los Angeles Glass observation deck overlooking LA by 掰二雷 Skyspace Observation Deck sits on floors 69 to 70 of Federal Bank Building, California's highest open-air viewing platform, and the highest point ordinary people can reach in Los Angeles besides airplanes. Here you can enjoy panoramic views of all of Los Angeles, and the fully transparent glass slide from floor 70 down to 69 is one of the popular and thrilling experiences. Famous blogger Angel Wings check-in via instagram @msdoncellita After taking the elevator directly to the 70th-floor observation deck, it's best to first queue up for a photo with the viral wings here. There are two tall and short wing walls that you can choose from based on your preference. Next, while the sun hasn't set yet, take some photos overlooking Los Angeles city during the day. Finally, of course, experience the highlight of this observation deck—the sky slide. The slide isn't long at only 10 meters, but when you truly sit in the fully transparent glass slide looking down, even timid girls will find it thrilling and exciting. Sky slide Via instagram @jeffrey_xiong After speeding down from the sky slide, you arrive at the 69th floor, which is also a 360-degree open-air observation deck. You can rest on the terrace seats or go to the cafe for a drink. Once night falls, it returns to an Angel City (Los Angeles) under neon lights. Photo tips: 17:00-18:00 is the best photo time, allowing you to capture both daytime and nighttime styles of Los Angeles cityscape simultaneously. When photographing the transparent slide, since the slide distance is relatively short, it's better to have a companion prepare at the exit in advance. 📍Location: 633 West Fifth Street #840, Los Angeles, CA 90071 ⌚Business hours: 10:00-22:00 💰Per person cost: 💲25 (does not include sky slide) Griffith Park 格里菲斯公园 💡 Highlights: LA LA LAND filming location Griffith Observatory by Ruiri Griffith Park is Los Angeles residents' first choice for weekend getaways, and one of many popular photo backdrops beloved by countless influencer bloggers. The park features numerous attractions worth visiting. Start your visit with a breathtaking night view from the famous Griffith Observatory's summit - you can not only admire LA's dazzling city lights but also see in the distance Hollywood's iconic giant sign. If time permits, I recommend walking to the classic LA LA LAND filming locations for photos and memories. Astronomy Museum Exhibits by Ruiri After arriving at Griffith Observatory, resist rushing straight up to the top - the observatory itself is well worth exploring. Inside feature multiple exhibition halls that explain astronomical knowledge through 3D presentations. Additionally, there's a spherical dome cinema where visitors can feel as if immersed in outer space, enjoying celestial evolution from all angles in an unforgettable visual feast Watching Sunset at Observatory by YangYangiiiiii 5:30 PM - 6:00 PM is the best time to reach the top of the observatory. At this time, visitors on the viewing platform will become increasingly dense, collectively holding their breath waiting for the most beautiful moment. As the sun slowly sets, within just a few minutes, lights throughout the entire city will turn on simultaneously, twinkling like stars, brilliant and dazzling. Screenshot from the movie "La La Land" In "La La Land," the beautiful waltz performed by the male and female protagonists left a deep impression on audiences. With the dazzling lights of Los Angeles city as their backdrop, they danced at Cathy's Corner in Griffith Park. This filming location has become a popular spot for tourists to make a pilgrimage. If the timing is right, it is recommended that you follow the dedicated hiking trail (2KM) there and imitate scenes from the movie, taking some fun photos. Photography Tips: 5:00 PM - 7:00 PM is an ideal time for portrait photography. Many beautiful Instagram photos are taken here. This is a recommended spot to take photos with the Hollywood sign and offers excellent views of Los Angeles' magnificent night skyline. 📍 Location: Observatory Rd., Los Angeles, CA ⌚ Opening Hours: Tuesday - Friday 12:00 PM - 10:00 PM; Saturday - Sunday 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM 💰 Average Cost per Person: Free (dome movie not included) Griffith Observatory · Located on the northwest hilltop of Los Angeles, offering a panoramic view of the entire city. · Features astronomical knowledge about planets and the universe. The dome theater at the top is designed like the sky, allowing 360° viewing of films about celestial evolution. Naked-eye 3D technology brings cosmic wonders within reach. · The open-air platform provides an overlook of Los Angeles, with orderly streets, neat city buildings, and downtown skyscrapers all clearly visible. · After nightfall, the observatory opens free of charge, allowing you to see the stars in the sky and the outline of the moon's surface. Santa Monica Beach 💡 Highlight: End of Route 66 Santa Monica is one of Southern California's premier beaches and also serves as the terminus of Route 66. This place has both urban coolness and a relaxed atmosphere. Third Street Promenade is the most vibrant spot in this area. Street performers can be seen everywhere, enthusiastically singing and occasionally interacting warmly with visitors who stop by. Wooden benches by the beach and Route 66 signs serve as perfect props for capturing American-style street photography. Pacific Park by Liang A-lun Santa Monica Pier at the end of the highway resembles scenes from American animated films. Pacific Park, which extends from the beach all the way to the sea, seems always filled with visitors' laughter and joy. The antique carousel and solar-powered Ferris wheel under California's sunshine make for excellent photo backdrops. Additionally, the park itself is quite interesting, making it an ideal choice for family vacations. Photo Tips: The Route 66 sign is a popular photo spot requiring queues; surrounding visitors may unintentionally appear in frames. Photographers are advised to crouch down and shoot upward - this avoids dense crowds while showcasing the sign against the blue sky from a different perspective. 📍Location: 380 Santa Monica Pier, Santa Monica, CA 90401, USA ⌚Operating Hours (Pacific Park): Weekends & holidays 11:00 AM - 11:00 PM; Regular days 11:00 AM - 7:00 PM 💰Average Cost per Person (Pacific Park): Ages 8+ $28.95; Under 7 years old $16.95 Santa Monica Beach · Closest beach to downtown Los Angeles and the terminus of America's Route 66, its ocean views are beloved by Hollywood directors. · A top choice for LA families on vacation, a paradise where couples can enjoy their private world together. · When night lights glow on, Pacific Paradise stretches from the beach all along to the ocean—an endless sea of joy. · Themed parties are frequently held here with California-style band performances, making it a first-choice destination for tourists visiting LA beaches. Angels Flight Angel's Railway 💡 Highlight: Replicating the century-old railway Angels Flight car by Liang A-lun Originally a commuter train that ran up and down the steep slope of Bunker Hill, this world's shortest train closed its doors for nearly four years. After gaining fame through the movie "La La Land," it reopened once again as an iconic landmark in downtown Los Angeles—the City of Angels. Photo Tips: The vintage orange-and-yellow striped carriages, wooden bodies, and tiered seating all preserve their original century-old appearance—perfect for capturing a series of 1930s retro photos. For couples especially, I recommend stepping inside the carriage and sitting on the wooden seats, just like in the film's ending, to take a romantic "Angel's Kiss" photo together. 📍 Location: 351 South Hill Street, Los Angeles, CA, USA ⌚ Opening Hours: 06:45-22:00 💰 Average Cost Per Person: $1 Angel's Railway Angel's Railway is the filming location for the movie "La La Land," where Mia and Sebastian had a romantic date and shared a sweet kiss in the wooden carriages. The Angel's Railway has been operating since 1901, making it one of the world's shortest railways. Tourists can ride the Angel's Railway to experience this brief yet enchanting journey in the vintage black-and-orange painted cars with their wooden interiors. Urban Light 💡 Highlights: Photo-worthy light posts Neatly arranged light posts by Yu Urban Light is a public art installation in front of the Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA). In fact, LACMA itself is one of America's top museums, featuring seven buildings that house an incredibly rich collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts. Notably, there is a permanent Japanese exhibition hall within the museum, which houses many precious hand-drawn posters from classic Japanese films—well worth exploring. City Lights via Instagram American contemporary artist Chris Burden created a psychedelic space in front of the Art Museum using 202 street lamps. Many international fashion bloggers visit Los Angeles and take beautiful photos beside this iconic building called City Lights. Photography tips: At night when all the street lights are on, the warm yellow lighting against the white plaster columns creates photos that look like they're filtered through an ancient Greek lens. 📍 Location: 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Los Angeles ⌚ Opening hours: Monday, Tuesday, Thursday 11:00-17:00, Friday 11:00-20:00, Saturday & Sunday 10:00-19:00, Closed on Wednesday, Thanksgiving, and Christmas 💰 Average cost per person: Adults $15, Seniors/Students $10, Children free Los Angeles County Museum of Art The Los Angeles County Museum of Art includes five buildings, three outdoor sculptures, an art library, an art resource center, a large lecture hall, and a theater. With over 10,000 works of art, it is the largest "encyclopedic" public museum on the US West Coast. The interior of the exhibition hall features a column-free design that is spacious and open. With natural sunlight flowing in, it creates an ideal display space for contemporary artworks.
