Los Angeles is the second largest city in America after New York, the birthplace of Hollywood, and a famous destination beloved by social media influencers. Today I will share with you how to experience all of Los Angeles' must-visit attractions in one day! 8:30 AM | Departing from the hotel Wake up at 8 AM, enjoy a Western breakfast near your hotel or accommodation. Then depart from the hotel to begin your journey through Los Angeles' famous spots. 9:30 AM | Angels Flight Railway — Filming location for La La Land Angels Flight is a scenic attraction located in downtown Los Angeles and has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places. This railway, with a total length of 99 meters (298 feet), is one of the shortest railways in the world. It began operation in 1901, using two cable cars to transport passengers between Hill Street and Olvera Street up the steep slope. Actually, the original purpose for building this railway was to provide convenient transportation for wealthy residents living on hillsides, eliminating the need to climb the original 200+ steps. Passengers at that time only needed to pay one cent to ride the cable car, which took just one minute. This short railway serves like a silent angel, quietly witnessing the creation of over 100 films. Riding it feels like traveling back to Los Angeles more than a century ago along with the tracks—an experience unlike any other. In "La La Land," Mia and Seb shared their sweet kiss here, filling countless film fans with endless longing for this romantic movie landmark. Practical Information Transportation: Take the Metro Red Line or Purple Line to Civic Center Station or Grand Park Station, then walk a short distance. Practical Information: Phone: +1-213-6242378 Opening Hours: Monday-Sunday 8:00-22:00 Tips: 317 S Broadway Basement Los Angeles, CA 90013 Downtown Grand Central Market 13:30 | Hollywood Walk of Fame - Every photo you take looks like a magazine cover The Hollywood Walk of Fame was built by Southern California artist Oliver Weismuller, who was hired by the city government with the goal of elevating Hollywood's "image." At the beginning of its construction, many honorees received several star medals because they had contributed in various fields. However, in recent decades (from the late 20th to early 21st century), this practice has no longer occurred, so only a very small number of early recipients have received more than one star. The first star was placed in 1960, and its honoree is Joanne Woodward. The current Walk of Fame is jointly maintained by the Hollywood Chamber of Commerce and the self-funded Hollywood Historical Trust. For Chinese visitors, this can be considered a highly famous attraction. It includes over 2,500 "stars" made of five-pointed terrazzo and brass, embedded in sidewalks along 15 blocks of Hollywood Boulevard and 3 blocks of Vine Street. The "stars" represent an eternal memorial to those who have made outstanding achievements in the entertainment industry, recording the names of actors, musicians, directors, producers, music groups/bands, theater troupes, fictional characters, and others. If you do not recognize the names on the stars, you can understand them approximately through pictorial symbols; there are five categories: 1) A movie camera, representing film and television actors and directors; 2) A television set, for excellent talk show or television program hosts; 3) A phonograph record, naturally honoring soul singers; 4) A broadcast microphone, thanking famous broadcasters for their outstanding performance in the radio media field; 5) Comedy and tragedy masks, applauding stage artists for their contributions to theater. Tips: Location: 633 W 5th St, Los Angeles, CA 90071, United States. 16:00 | Griffith Observatory - Hollywood Sign Viewing Spot Griffith Observatory faces across from the Hollywood Sign mountain, offering a panoramic view of the entire city of Los Angeles. Long famous as one of LA's premier viewing destinations, the film "La La Land" beautifully captured its charm here. The observatory consists of three dome buildings, housing exquisite astronomical instruments and exhibits. A huge rotating globe and planetary diagram allow visitors to learn about astronomy-related knowledge. On the west side building, there is a three-beam solar telescope, while on the east side exhibition hall sits a 12-inch Carl Zeiss refractor telescope. Both provide free opportunities for the public to explore cosmic mysteries. Los Angeles locals love visiting Griffith Observatory to view night scenes - looking up and seeing a sea of brilliant stars, then turning around inside the dome rooms to trace their life paths. For many years in the past, people believed that starlight came from heaven's glow. View the city's panoramic view from above at night, and you will surely deeply feel what is meant by "City of Stars."
