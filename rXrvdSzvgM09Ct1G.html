Warm and inviting sunshine, seabirds soaring with the wind, soft and fine golden sand—these elements weave together a 69-kilometer stretch of azure coastline along Los Angeles. Here youll find surfing teenagers, street performers, couples holding hands, middle-aged men fishing... Their leisurely lifestyle forms Los Angelss most beautiful scenery. When twilight approaches, rosy-purple evening clouds spread across the sky and connect with the sea; at this moment the landscape is full of tenderness and boundless romance. Romantic and Retro: Check In at Santa Monica Beach If you can only visit one beach in Los Angeles, 99% of people will choose Santa Monica Beach. This isnt just because it offers beautiful Pacific coastal scenery—it is also the endpoint of Americas Mother Road Highway 66 and a filming location for classic films and TV shows like Forrest Gump. Picture from santamonica.com The Santa Monica Pier, built in 1909, bustles with crowds throughout the day. You can take photos with the Route 66 sign, browse vintage souvenirs at roadside shops, leisurely watch the sea from the end of the pier, and ride on the famous Pacific Ferris Wheel at the old-fashioned Pacific Park amusement park to rediscover childhood fun. Dynamic Balance: Exploring Venice Beach Venice Beach, located south of Santa Monica, is Los Angeles' most diverse beach. Known for various sports, street artists, and canal ruins, this place serves as a headquarters for surfers, youth culture enthusiasts, artists, rappers, artsy individuals... It's also a famous sunset viewing spot. Image from visitveniceca.com Walking along the beachside path, you can see various small vendors and street performers. There are also dedicated cycling paths and ice skating trails here, allowing you to experience the locals' happy and open lifestyle. The Skate Park is another signature feature of Venice Beach. It has appeared in many films, where skilled skateboarders who excel at tricks always gather. Their performances captivate audiences watching day after day. The Venice Canals Historic District is nestled within residential areas by the beach. This canal was built in 1905 by businessman Abbot Kinney, modeled after Venice canals. Today it has become a historical and cultural landmark of Los Angeles. You can stroll along the waterway's edge, discover charming little bridges one after another, flower-filled courtyards, and enjoy the serene scenery. Abbot Kinney Boulevard, located beside Venice Beach, has a bohemian atmosphere with colorful graffiti walls hidden throughout. Along the street you can find trendy boutiques, design shops, popular restaurants, and ambiance cafes — making it one of Los Angeles' best shopping destinations to visit. This beach feels like a free, year-round carnival festival. Street performers, weightlifters, bohemian-style residents, and quirky boutique shops all contribute to making this place exceptionally vibrant and interesting. Walking along Venice's boardwalk, you might find yourself becoming the "props" of street performers at any moment. The area is steeped in distinctive artistic charm - from tattoos to tie-dye to fortune-telling, various hippie-style street art fills the entire beach. Choose an afternoon to wander through Venice Beach and slowly absorb this unique American culture; wouldn't that be a true reward on your travels? Wild and Untamed: Self-driving along Malibu's coastline With 34 kilometers of Pacific Ocean coastal scenery, Malibu features stunning landscapes - it is synonymous with surfing and mansions. Top stars like director Steven Spielberg and pop singer Lady Gaga have made this their home. With one beautiful beach after another, it is ideal for road trips. Malibu Beach Malibu Beach is a beautiful rocky shore with numerous Hollywood celebrity ocean-view mansions nearby. Malibu Pier forms the heart of the beach area. The surrounding beaches have attracted countless surf enthusiasts since the 1960s and has become a world-class surfing paradise. You can watch professional athletes move gracefully through the waves, or sign up for a club to experience a day of surfing. Every summer, international surfing festivals and beach volleyball open tournaments are held to let competitors from around the world showcase their skills. Compared to other surfing beaches, the waves here are long and powerful with higher swells, making them ideal for freestyle surfing. Hermosa Beach Bordering Manhattan Beach, Hermosa Beach also boasts beautiful sandy shores and coastline, with constant streams of surfers and exciting beach volleyball matches. Many visitors come here to enjoy the sea breeze and spectacular sunsets. You can walk along the beach, blend in with locals who jog, cycle, or walk their dogs, experiencing their leisurely lifestyle. Your first stop on a Los Angeles journey is from airport to beach. Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) ranks as the second busiest passenger airport in America and serves as the gateway for international travelers, especially those from Asia-Pacific entering North America. Beyond being a vital transportation hub for Los Angeles, it stands as a pearl gem along the Pacific coastline. The airport houses the Westfield shopping center, combining dining, retail therapy, and leisure activities. Various duty-free shops and restaurants dot the area. The IN-N-OUT Burger located near the airport offers an excellent vantage point to watch planes take off and land. Due to its coastal location on the Pacific Ocean, accessing nearby beaches is exceptionally convenient. The distances are short: Manhattan Beach lies 7.5 kilometers away, Hermosa Beach sits 10 kilometers distant, and Venice Beach is only 11 kilometers away. With the airport as your base of operations, you can begin your Los Angeles adventure with California sunshine-kissed beaches...
