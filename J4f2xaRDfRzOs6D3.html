Whether you are visiting Los Angeles for the first time or returning multiple times, a stroll through Beverly Hills is always recommended. After all, as one of the world's "most prestigious residential areas," with wealth and fame filling every corner, sprinkled with California sunshine, it shines even more brilliantly and golden. Picture from*** , copyright belongs to the original author If you are tired of driving yourself through America's desolate deserts, why not head to Beverly Hills for a leisurely mix of dining, drinking, and browsing? Here whether you visit restaurants or go shopping, there is a great chance of encountering Hollywood stars. You can enjoy a life environment full of luxury like them, experiencing a romantic daily life that feels like vacationing every day—truly the most relaxing experience possible. Today I will recommend some great places to visit in Beverly Hills! Picture from City Of Beverly Hills, copyright belongs to the original author Transportation Geographic Location Beverly Hills is located on the west side of Los Angeles, along the Pacific coastline and at the foot of the Beverly Hills mountains. Beverly Hills functions as a self-contained enclave within Los Angeles, radiating outward from its world-famous luxury shopping street as the center. North Santa Monica Boulevard serves as a geographical dividing line. [Image courtesy of Google Map. All rights reserved to original author.] **Transportation Methods** **Self-Driving** Recommendation Rating: ★★★★★ Since Los Angeles is naturally suited for exploring by car, self-driving is the most recommended option if you have access to it. Beverly Hills is particularly well-suited to a drive-and-walk approach: drive to a specific area, then walk through the surrounding luxury boutiques. Parking space is reasonably available; with patience you can find appropriate spots, though be mindful of parking fees and local regulations. [Image courtesy of City Of Beverly Hills. All rights reserved to original author.] **Public Transportation** Recommendation Rating: ★★ Bus routes 20 and 4 also provide access to the iconic Rodeo Drive area at the heart of Beverly Hills shopping district. You can use Google Maps to precisely query bus departure times and travel duration. If you want to go to restaurants for dining or tour around the mansions, bus routes do not really meet our needs well. Plus, Los Angeles weather is mostly hot, and walking would be quite tiring. Image from *** , copyright belongs to original author Taxi rides in Beverly Hills are relatively expensive, and you cannot flag down a taxi directly on the roadside. You need to book ahead at the hotel or at designated TaxiZone taxi pickup points on the roadside. But this is also a relatively easy way to get directly to your destination. Image from Flickr , copyright belongs to original author Beverly Hills also has shared bicycles available for everyone to use, but no matter where you ride, there are designated parking points. Unlike back home in China where we just carelessly leave them on the roadside, plus all those actions like binding bank cards and payment deductions, this is too cumbersome for us who just want simple check-ins. This method is more suitable for friends who stay long-term in Los Angeles or America. Image from Love Beverly Hills , copyright belongs to original author Group day tours Rating: ★★★ If you're not confident about self-driving, sign up for a group tour instead. Especially suitable for parents, although it feels more like checking boxes mechanically and you can't explore too much according to your own preferences, but it saves time and worry, allowing you to fully experience the luxurious lifestyle of Beverly Hills as well. Accommodation Although just a small stop on a Los Angeles trip or Western US journey, Beverly Hills also has hotels worth experiencing. Of course, prices won't be very friendly, but the geographical advantage is significant. If time and budget are appropriate, here are some recommendations: The Beverly Hills Hotel Features: Pink palace, the original Instagram-famous hotel Price: Starting at $650 per night Rating: ★★★★ You've surely seen countless photos of this hotel's pink facade on social media already. Tourists visit daily to take pictures, mostly young women here for photoshoots. While it's a trendy spot, this hotel has been around for over 100 years—Marilyn Monroe, Charlie Chaplin, and Princess Grace of Monaco have all stayed here. This hotel is filled with charming and romantic Hollywood style both in its exterior design and interior facilities, with deep cultural heritage. Combined with first-class five-star service and being surrounded by mansions, it feels like an earthly paradise. Many tourists come here to take photos, but there's nothing particularly spectacular - just take pictures and leave~ Picture courtesy of Love Beverly Hills, copyright belongs to the original author Greystone Mansion Fee: Free (no reservation needed in special circumstances) Address: 905 Loma Vista Dr, Beverly Hills, CA 90210 Built in 1920 by oil tycoon Henry Doherty at a cost of $4 million. However, his only son was shot and killed, and he himself became involved in bribery scandals, bringing misfortune to the family. The descendants were unable to pay for the expensive annual maintenance and repairs of this mansion, so the city of Beverly Hills took it over. Picture courtesy of Woman Getting Married, copyright belongs to the original author The Grey Stone Mansion was thus opened as a public park for everyone to visit and enjoy free of charge. Later, it became famous because it served as a filming location for multiple movies. Despite the constant stream of visitors, the castle remains calm and peaceful, allowing guests to comfortably tour and rest here. Many people choose to take wedding photos here or host small-to-large dinner events. Image courtesy of ***, copyright belongs to the original author Virginia Robinson Gardens Admission: Adults $11, Children $4 (advance reservation required) Address: 1008 Elden Way, Beverly Hills, CA 90210 Built in 1911, Virginia Robinson Gardens was formerly the private residence of the founder of Robinson Department Store in Beverly Hills. It later became one of Beverly Hills' most renowned historical buildings. Tours are available from Monday through Friday and must be guided—self-guided visits are not permitted. Visitors are advised to book their tours in advance via the official website.
