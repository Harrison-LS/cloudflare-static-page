Single Rider Pass Thirdly, after you've made your reservations, there are still so many people waiting in line for non-reserved attractions. What should you do? At Disneyland and California Adventure Park, many attractions have a single rider lane called "single rider." What does this mean? For example, if you want to ride a roller coaster that seats three passengers per row, and two of you are traveling together, there will be an empty seat next to you. In this case, staff will arrange for single riders to fill these empty spots. So if you don't mind being separated from your companions while on the ride, you can choose to use the single rider lane. Often, you'll end up in the same car, just possibly not in the same row. But this really speeds up queueing significantly—while others may wait two hours, you might only need 20 minutes, giving you enough time to return to your previously scheduled attractions. Ask a cast member for a "single rider" ticket, and the queuing speed will be several times faster! Every one is a classic~ "The Lion King" (image from internet) However, the speed of the single rider lane also depends on how many people can ride per row at each attraction. Typically, if the number is odd, it requires a large number of single riders to fill empty spots (since most visitors come with one parent and one child, or as couples). If the seating is two per row or four per row, there's less demand for single riders, so the speed isn't as fast. At amusement facility entrances, there will be staff members. You simply need to tell them you're a single passenger, and they'll give you a small card. Some attractions have dedicated single-passenger lanes where you don't even need the card—just go queue directly! Tips: The "Single Passenger Lane" has one major advantage over the "Fast Pass" mentioned above—you can ride infinitely many times.
