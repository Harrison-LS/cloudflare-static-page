Colorado Street Bridge The ornate bridge that the two people walk hand in hand as their feelings grow toward each other is Colorado Street Bridge. The Colorado Street Bridge serves as a transportation hub connecting Los Angeles with Old Pasadena and stands as one of the oldest concrete bridges in the Los Angeles area. When first constructed in 1913, the Colorado Street Bridge was known as the world's tallest concrete bridge, featuring massive arched openings that rise 150 feet high. Angels Flight Railway Under montage-style cinematography, Mia and Sebastian board the iconic Angels Flight on Hill Street in downtown Los Angeles. Built in 1901, this 99-meter railway stands as the world's shortest cable system, exuding a rich vintage charm. Although operations ceased in 2013, producers successfully obtained filming rights for a single day on this historic Angels Flight. Restoration efforts are now underway by the operating committee, promising soon to bring back these memorable cinematic moments from "La La Land." Angels Flight was a filming location for the movie "La La Land," where Mia and Sebastian had a romantic date and shared sweet kisses in the wooden carriages. Angel Railway has been operating since 1901 and is the world's shortest railway. Visitors can take Angel Railway to experience this brief and enchanting journey in a vintage car with orange and black stripes, riding wooden cars. Los Angeles Grand Central Market Located opposite Angel Railway, director used montage techniques in the film to showcase this popular location for viewers. Established in 1917, this market gathers various traditional and modern food stalls. It is a hippie-style food city directly across from Angel Railway, a holy site that must-visit destinations for foodies in Los Angeles. In the movie, Mia and Sebastian visited Sarita's Pupuseria, a small stall in the market, which is the popular restaurant. Watts Towers Mia and Sebastian's date spots also include the uniquely styled Watts Towers. This oddly-shaped tower is hailed as an architectural miracle because it was completely built by Italian architect Simon Rodia over 33 years alone. Japanese architecture master Tadao Andō praised Watts Towers as Rodia's "soul cry" expression. Perhaps La La Land, a film about dreams, serves as a fitting epilogue as well. Watts Tower One of the date scenes between the male and female leads in La La Land was filmed here. Watts Towers was built over more than 30 years by a man named Simon Rodia. The structure features unique architecture that is magnificent and imposing. Standing at more than 30 meters tall, it was constructed primarily from simple materials such as scrap metal, pottery shards, stone, and glass, using steel, cement, and mortar to hold everything together. This tower can be considered a work of art that transcends architectural laws in every aspect. Every year it attracts visitors from all over the world who come specifically to see it. In any way you look at it, this tower stands as an artistic creation that goes beyond absolute architectural principles, making it extremely worth viewing.
