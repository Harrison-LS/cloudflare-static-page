In Los Angeles, driving is certainly the best option. Of course, choosing to take the subway and experience the local culture is also a great choice. So which line should you take in Los Angeles, and which stations can reach attractions? Let me tell you in detail~ This time we will introduce the Los Angeles Metro's Red Line The Red Line is the only line that runs entirely underground in the rail system. From Union Station to downtown, then to Hollywood, and finally to Universal Studios. If you don't get off, the entire trip takes only 29 minutes. This is the most important line for tourists~ First is the ticket price Ticket prices: Single ride $1.75, 1-day pass $7, 7-day pass $25. Purchase tickets: There are automatic ticket machines at the stations. Payment: The rail transit must use a TAP Card (purchased and reloaded at the ticket machines; cost fee is $1 - non-refundable; reload range is $5-$300, remaining balance not returned). Operating hours: Around 4:30 AM to midnight. Others: Within 2 hours after swiping your card, if you continue riding a bus heading in the same direction, the bus fare is free (you still need to swipe the card; the system will automatically not deduct fees). Q. Is there a fare for children riding the Los Angeles Metro? A paying adult may bring up to two children under age 5 for free along with them. Q. Is there a student ticket on LA Metro? Student tickets are available, but only for students attending school in Los Angeles, and must be applied for in advance. Q. Can multiple people share one card? No, each person needs their own card. Q. Is there a Metro app available to download? There are many; please search yourself, such as Go Metro. Most apps provide offline maps and real-time train arrival information.
