Here's my strong recommendation: If you're coming to Los Angeles, Universal Studios Hollywood is absolutely a must-visit — you won't regret it! The attractions are truly world-class, and I have to say the Americans' 5D visual effects are incredibly realistic, letting you experience thrilling rides without much physical discomfort. Alright, let me share a few preparation tips for your trip to Universal Studios Hollywood in Los Angeles. First, tickets. I bought what's basically the most ordinary ticket — 1-Day General Admission Adult, entry before 10 AM, priced at USD $105. Even though it's the cheapest option with no special privileges, if you do a bit of research ahead and ask friends who've been there, you can still experience all the rides in one day, and some of your favorite attractions even two or three times! I recommend buying tickets online from Universal Studios Hollywood's official website beforehand. First, buying online saves you from standing in line at the ticket booth. Second, it's slightly cheaper! Only by a tiny bit — $5 to $10 dollars — but small money is still money, right? Thirdly, Universal Studios offers many types of tickets - single-day entry, multi-day passes, flexible time options, no-wait tickets, and more, all at different prices. If your English isn't perfect and you happen to meet a ticket agent with an accent, you'll definitely get confused right at the counter and waste valuable time. That time could be spent on many more rides! So check ahead on the official website, look up anything unclear, and choose the ticket that best fits your specific situation based on all this research. What should you wear to Universal Studios? Regarding clothing - whether you're female or male, flat shoes are a must! Flat shoes! Flat shoes! Important things are said three times for emphasis. Comfortable sneakers are ideal. Even though Los Angeles' Universal isn't as large as I imagined, spending a full day exploring the park involves extensive walking and queuing with minimal opportunity to sit and rest - your feet will get tired. As for clothes, I recommend wearing pants. Choose your top based on the season and weather. I think skirts are somewhat inconvenient when riding attractions. You can wear brighter colors (though I wore all black that day [facepalm]), after all at Universal Studios you're not only going wild on rides but also taking photos! The park has many people dressed as characters, it would be wonderful to take beautiful pictures with your favorite characters. By the way, regarding clothes, let me share a little trick - I learned this from a friend. If you're a Harry Potter fan flying from China to LA for fun, remember to buy a Harry Potter black robe on Taobao - it's their school uniform at the wizarding school. It's a great tool for posing and taking photos! Universal Studios has a new Harry Potter theme park area that opened last April (2016), truly heaven for HP fans, wearing that black robe makes you feel more immersed. Although they sell those robes inside the park too, the price is unimaginable - probably about as expensive as tickets. Of course, if you want to buy an authentic one inside the park as a souvenir, that's fine too. This suggestion is only for friends who don't want to spend so much money but still want to pose well and take good photos. (I flew from within the US to LA, didn't have the chance to buy on Taobao, so I couldn't dress up as a wizard, but seeing my friends' photos in black robes really looks great!) Universal Studios Hollywood differs significantly from its Orlando counterpart - notably lacking any water-based attractions that would require extra clothing. A compact bag containing essential items like sunglasses, payment methods, mobile device, sun protection, and cosmetics will suffice for visitors. Makeup application should be subtle to complement your day-long experience at the park. Light makeup ensures you maintain a polished appearance throughout your visit, especially given California's warm sunlight even during winter months. Heavy makeup can smudge or become unflattering by evening after extended exposure to the theme park environment. Lip color can be something more lively and vibrant. What to eat at Universal Studios? Let me share some tips about food. A full day of playing is really physically draining. For breakfast, make sure to eat well and have enough before entering the park. Inside the park, you'll definitely need lunch; dinner depends on when you plan to exit. My companions and I bought sandwiches at Walgreens the night before and brought them into the park for lunch. Just bring a small bottle of water. There are many food options inside - including Chinese fast food like Panda Express. At the attractions, don't be too picky about food quality; something filling will suffice. Major chains like Starbucks are available throughout the park, along with various themed restaurants offering typical American fare—pizza, burgers, fries, and more. Snacks are plentiful too: ice cream, cinnamon rolls, donuts, and similar treats that can help pass time in line if you're feeling bored. Personally, I find bringing lunch saves time significantly. Dining within the park involves queuing, ordering, and waiting for your meal, which extends the overall duration. Ultimately, this depends on individual preferences—proper nutrition definitely impacts how well you can enjoy the experience. Oh right, for photography enthusiasts planning a visit to Universal Studios, carrying a bulky DSLR isn't necessary. Because there are simply too many people at Universal Studios Hollywood. When it's this crowded, you can't capture any cinematic shots - everything comes out as tourist photos. Also, carrying a DSLR while riding attractions is inconvenient, and if you accidentally bump or drop your camera, it wouldn't be worth the risk. For photography purposes, a smartphone and mirrorless camera are more than enough. I visited Universal Studios Hollywood on Christmas Eve last year, December 24th, 2016. Christmas Eve is similar to our Chinese New Year's Eve - the number of visitors in the park might be slightly fewer than usual, just a tiny bit less, and it's better in the evening. But it's definitely not that kind of situation where there are very few people. Universal Studios Hollywood has 15 main attractions. My friends and I went through all of them in one day, and we rode Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey three times - that was the most popular ride. The key to having an efficient day at Universal is arranging your time well between rides, shows, and photo opportunities. We arrived at Universal Studios around 9:45 AM on December 24th. Security check and ticketing had no lines whatsoever - everything went smoothly. When getting tickets, remember to pick up a park map; you'll find it very useful throughout the day. There doesn't seem to be a Chinese version available, but the English maps are easy to understand. The moment you enter the park, you're bound to think, "Wow, those little buildings look adorable," "Those people dressed as characters along the path are so interesting," "Oh, this view is perfect for photos!" and "Hmm, that scene would work well for photography too." Ladies who love taking photos, remember: at this point, you really must hold back. You should know that you'll be here all day, so make the most of early morning when things are at their best, and enjoy rides while lines aren't too long yet. How to arrange your visit order? My recommendation is: first take the Studio Tour, then head to Lower Lot (Lower Park), and finally enjoy Upper Lot (Upper Park). After entering the park, there's a main thoroughfare. At this point, visitors must resist all temptations and keep walking straight ahead. When you see Simpsons House, take an escalator to the right; at the bottom is where the Studio Tour boarding occurs. There's a departure schedule next to it - check the times before boarding. By the way, this attraction offers Mandarin service. Visitors with no English knowledge can wait for Mandarin-language vehicles, though options are more limited in terms of timing. I think anyone with basic English skills can comfortably join the regular tours. This tour lasts approximately 60 minutes. On board the sightseeing vehicle, each bus has a guide who serves as both narrator and tour leader. You won't get off during the entire experience - instead, you watch and enjoy various attractions from your seat. The main focus is introducing filming locations for Hollywood movies, moving between different stages with highly authentic recreations of classic scenes from famous Hollywood films. Your guide will provide commentary while small TVs on board play corresponding movie clips. There will also be some 3D experiences along the way, simulating scenarios like floods, earthquakes, explosions, and more. Movie enthusiasts will definitely be very excited. I won't spoil too much here - saying too much would kill the mystery and I might get punched for it, haha. The studio tour vehicle will take you to the pickup location, then return via an elevator on the same route. To the left of the Simpsons house there is a large downward elevator - this is the elevator down to the lower lot area. Go straight down without hesitation. The lower area is smaller and attractions are concentrated. There are mainly three major rides: Jurassic Park, The Mummy, and Transformers. Each ride entrance displays waiting time information. When we visited that day, the average wait was about 30 minutes, which I think is still acceptable. Also, queuing for each attraction won't be boring - you will be immersed into the entire storyline through some movie trailers while waiting in line. As soon as you reach the lower area, check the wait times for these three rides and do whichever one has the shortest queue first. We started with Jurassic Park (Jurassic Park). Remember last year when we watched the Jurassic Park movie and were scared enough to huddle into our seats with friends? Well here there are many dinosaurs but not scary at all after all they are just fake foam boards. In my opinion this ride is basically a non-discomfort version of log flume - it's a water-based project but you won't get wet, and no raincoat needed. The family sitting directly in front of us was all wearing bright yellow raincoats, and they stayed completely dry... I forgive myself and my friends for silently laughing from behind them. Next up was Revenge of the Mummy, another ride. Compared to Jurassic Park, this one is more thrilling, and it's an indoor attraction. It features rapid forward-and-backward motion combined with 4D effects. There's free luggage storage at the entrance where you can bring your phone—just place it in a small mesh pocket on the seat while riding. Once that's done, feel free to scream wildly! Haha, even if you're not scared, screaming feels amazing and is super stress-relieving! The last major ride before leaving the park was Transformers—a 3D experience themed around the Transformers movies. I won't go into too much detail about what happens inside; just go experience it for yourself. At the entrance, you can take photos with costumed Transformers characters who interact with visitors, though they're quite haughty. When I said "I love you" to one, he responded with "Oh, disgusting..." Well, I gave him a disgusted look in return. The photo session requires waiting in line, but it's not long—about five minutes is enough to get your turn. It's suggested that you do this after riding the Transformers attraction so you can take a breather. After experiencing all the attractions at the lower park area with friends, grab some food, take some tourist photos, and rest for a bit. Then continued up using the elevator you came down from earlier. On the way up, you can enjoy the view—it's quite nice. By the time we were ready to explore Upper Park, it was already afternoon, probably when Universal Studios is most crowded. We checked the wait times and chose a ride with the shortest wait: Flight of the Hippogriff in Harry Potter area. Actually it's just a small roller coaster. How small? About 15 seconds of riding time, with manageable curves and height that cause no physical discomfort. From it you can look down at Harry Potter land. During afternoon peak queue times, it's not ideal to go on popular rides. I remember Forbidden Journey being the most popular in Harry Potter area with a 70-minute wait. So we decisively decided to watch various shows instead. First, after going crazy all morning, we really needed a break; second, shows don't require waiting—just check the show times and walk right in. Three main shows caught our attention. The Special Effect Show is 25 minutes long and mainly explains how various effects in movies are made—you'll learn something new. Universal's Animal Actors Performance features dogs, cats, birds, pigs, mice, etc. performing, lasting 15 minutes. I thought it was better than some animal shows I've seen back home in China. There was also Water World - a water stunt show lasting 20 minutes where they perform a story full of tricks right in front of you. I won't tell everyone what the specific story is about. By the way, if you don't want to get wet, better not sit in the front rows because the splashes during the performance are quite significant. Since the first two shows were close together, we watched both in the afternoon. The last one, Water World, was near the Harry Potter area and wasn't on our route, so we planned to watch it at night instead. After watching two shows, the three girls had recovered most of their energy and went to try The Walking Dead attraction, which was added in 2016. Having watched a few episodes of "The Walking Dead", zombies are indeed quite scary. This attraction features real actors dressed as zombies - you walk through a small dark room while they come to scare you, similar to a haunted house experience. I guess describing it this way makes it sound less frightening than it actually was - when I was inside, I screamed the entire time! Of course, those zombies only scared people and never touched anyone. I'd constantly wave my hands saying "No! No! No!" to them, and they would understand and ease off. Visitors shouldn't hit these zombie performers either, as they're dedicated staff members working hard at their craft. After experiencing the Walking Dead attraction, we moved on to explore Despicable Me Minion Mayhem's themed area. This is a 3D ride. If you've seen the Despicable Me movies, it will feel quite familiar. The content is also interesting; not very thrilling, but the visual effects are quite good. As evening fell, we made our way to the eagerly awaited Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey. It was still incredibly popular with a 40-minute queue, though certainly better than daytime crowds. This is an absolutely fantastic ride - fellow travelers who plan to come should definitely experience this regardless of how long they might have to wait! Featuring 360-degree rotating seats, indoor coaster elements, and all-in-one 4D cinema. Actually, time passes quite quickly while waiting; walking through that entrance feels like stepping into Hogwarts from the movie - various magic potions, animated portraits that can talk, the Sorting Hat itself. The level of authenticity is extremely high, so visiting while queuing makes time pass even faster. We rode this attraction twice in a row; the second time the queue moved much faster, probably because it was getting late. On Christmas Eve, many tourists must have gone home by then. The Simpsons Ride at Springfield Residence is also worth experiencing twice. This is more of a visual coaster, but it does deliver genuine weightlessness sensations like real coasters. Although this is an older animated show that's probably older than I am, the ride itself feels quite innovative and fresh. After leaving The Simpsons Ride, I heard Christmas music at Universal Plaza - "I wish you a merry Christmas~" and followed the sound. It was Christmas Eve, so the holiday atmosphere was especially strong. There was a particularly large Christmas tree in the middle of the plaza, with singing and dancing performances underneath it. When the show concluded, tiny snowflakes began falling from above, creating a magical effect. Watching these animatronic figures dance made me feel like I'd stepped into a fairytale realm. By evening, Universal Studios transformed even more beautifully under the night sky. The elaborate lighting decorations created an almost otherworldly atmosphere that seemed to transcend reality itself. I wandered through Fun Land, a dedicated children's entertainment zone with minimal crowds. A friendly cast member invited me onto a Dumbo-style rotating attraction without any waiting line. The experience instantly transported me back to my carefree childhood and awakened something playful within me. After carefully checking the Water World show schedule, I spent the journey there capturing every moment through my camera lens. The performers demonstrated remarkable dedication, braving cold nighttime temperatures while executing their aquatic routines underwater. While the narrative mirrored what audiences had seen locally in China, the technical execution of stunts proved noticeably more sophisticated. Upon discovering Harry Potter's Forbidden Journey had only a ten-minute queue, I decided to revisit this thrilling experience once more. During the third ride attempt, a mechanical glitch briefly halted the attraction for roughly ten seconds, triggering genuine anxiety and uncertainty about potential safety issues. My companion Zhao expressed mounting fear through intense verbal warnings about our precarious height above ground, fearing an unexpected descent. Fortunately, the system quickly restored normal operation without incident. This was also a quite unusual experience, huh. The last one left unwatched was Shrek 4D movie - Monster Shrek. Honestly, compared to other attractions it really fell short; I even fell asleep by the end. A fairly standard 4D film in my opinion with no real highlights; if you're short on time, this attraction can be skipped. Around 8 PM after experiencing all attractions and ready to leave the park. Outside there's a street dedicated to selling souvenirs, so while inside you don't need to waste time choosing memorabilia. On Christmas Eve that street hosted a band live performance, and even the chilly night felt warmer with their music. In short, one day at Universal Studios Hollywood is more than enough. I consulted someone who visited Orlando's Universal Studios - they recommend two full days, especially since Islands of Adventure focuses heavily on water-based rides, making it quite different from this location. I've also explored Disney World in Orlando, but Universal consistently appeals to me more personally. After all my rambling about the experience, I'm confident the visit justified the ticket price. Hopefully readers will share similar enthusiasm after following this guide. Best wishes!
