Myth 1: Driving in Los Angeles is difficult Indeed, LA freeways are certainly complex and confusing. But actually, once you grasp the main principles, driving in LA isn't that hard because all highways and local roads have fixed numbering patterns. After learning these organization systems along with cardinal directions, I actually find LA's grid-style city planning more intuitive and simpler than Taiwan's various streets and alleys! (Downtown Los Angeles DTLA has many one-way streets, which are the few exceptions - this is also why I dislike driving into downtown so much) Even numbers are typically east-west oriented Odd numbers are typically north-south oriented Myth 2: People in Los Angeles are not fashionable Most people always think that only New York is the fashion capital of America. But actually LA is one of the largest clothing manufacturing centers in the entire US. We have a Fashion District right in our downtown DTLA, plus we have the Fashion Institute of Design & Merchandising (FIDM). Furthermore, with all these Hollywood celebrities, socialites, and stylists living locally - how could LA not be fashionable? ! True, people in Los Angeles, due to the climate, geography, and lifestyle, may dress much more casually compared to other fashion capitals around the world, but casual luxury and casual sexy are also forms of fashion! Not to mention that most of the world-renowned, America's most famous premium denim brands originate from Los Angeles! Myth 3: Los Angeles has no culture Everyone always views Los Angeles as the world entertainment capital, so they believe besides pop culture there is nothing else. But in reality, the entire Los Angeles County has more than 230 museums and art galleries, among which the most well-known include Getty Center, Getty Villa, LACMA (Los Angeles County Museum of Art), MOCA (Museum of Contemporary Art), and recently the highly famous modern art museum The Broad. Besides museums and art galleries, LA actually has various galleries and artist studio tours everywhere.
