Wandering around Los Angeles definitely means hitting some flea markets to find exquisite and fun vintage items. Follow along as our little group explores the great flea markets Los Angeles has to offer. 1. Rose Bowl Flea Market Located in artistic Pasadena, this is probably the most famous flea market in the LA area. Surrounding a stadium that can hold 90,000 people, the entire market is divided into different sections offering a dazzling array of products with diverse styles. It takes quite a while to thoroughly explore just one section. Address: 1001 Rose Bowl Dr, Pasadena, CA 91103 Hours: Second Sunday of each month Tickets: Regular $9, Standard $12 (entry at 8am), Premium $15 (entry at 7am), VIP $20 (entry at 5am) *Children under 12 enter free Rose Bowl Flea Market 2. Artists & Fleas This flea market rose from fame in New York to Los Angeles, offering art pieces, clothing, accessories, furniture, and more. Many handmade items are personally crafted by local designers. Next to the flea market are various mobile food carts where you can enjoy delicious food while shopping around. Address: 1010 Abbot Kinney Blvd, Venice, CA 90291 Operating Hours: Every Saturday from Memorial Day (5/28) to Labor Day (9/1) Admission: Free Artists & Fleas 3. Long Beach Antique Flea Market As the flea market most loved by fashion-forward people, it showcases various exquisite vintage items—second-hand clothing or furniture. For example: wooden suitcases from the 1920s, vintage Model T cars, and punk-style jackets. You won't be disappointed if you come here to find treasures. Address: 5000 E LewDavis St, Long Beach, CA 90808 Operating Hours: Third Sunday of each month Admission: Regular ticket: $7, 6:30 AM – 2:00 PM; Early bird ticket: $12, 5:30 AM – 6:30 AM Long Beach Antique Market 4.Melrose Trading Post Located near Melrose's popular Instagrammable walls, this flea market is also beloved by many celebrities and influencers. BCBG's creative director Lubov Azria and fashionista Dana Goldberg are frequent visitors. Here you can find vintage items from around the world, plus there's live music and a food court on-site. It's perfect for a lazy Sunday morning to invite a few friends to browse together. Address: 7850 Melrose Avenue, Los Angeles, CA 90046 Operating Hours: Every Sunday 9AM – 5PM General Admission: $3 Melrose Sunday Flea Market 5.Santa Monica Airport Antiques & Collectibles This Westside market at Santa Monica Airport has earned a reputation as one of the top ten flea markets in America. This market is famous for selling vintage items, including paintings by local artists and old posters, plus mid-century vintage furniture, retro clothing, and more. A good place for West Side shoppers to find bargains. Address: 3050 Airport Ave, Santa Monica, CA 9040 Business Hours: First and fourth Sunday of each month 6. Silver Lake Art/Craft & Vintage As an independent flea market located on the playground of Micheltorena Elementary School, it allows East Side shoppers to conveniently find bargains too. Here you can not only find vintage items, but also many artworks from artists in the Silver Lake area and handcrafted goods by independent designers.
