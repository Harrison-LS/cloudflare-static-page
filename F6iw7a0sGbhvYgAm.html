Diverse Los Angeles has created a diverse food scene, featuring creative restaurants run by celebrity chefs, viral Instagram hotspots, historic farmers' markets, and authentic flavors from around the world. You can eat your way from downtown all along the coastline, from street food carts to Michelin-starred establishments. If you're good at finding deals, you can also enjoy affordable delicious food! **Gathering Place of Delicious Snacks** **01 Century-Old Market: Central Market** The Grand Central Market, opened in 1917, is affectionately called "the kitchen" by locals. This historic traditional market is both a tourist attraction and paradise for foodies. With dozens of shops in this bohemian-style food city, vendors sell fresh produce, seafood, creative items, and street food from various countries. *[Image source: official website]* From Mexican tacos to Southeast Asian laksa, from Italian pasta to Japanese ramen, from sandwiches to donuts, from freshly squeezed juices to craft beers, the endless array of food stalls whets your appetite. The popular famous shops here include the internet-famous breakfast spot Eggslut, Sarita's Pupuseria (filming location for "La La Land"), Tacos Tumbras a Tomas (a popular Mexican taco stand), McConnell's Fine Ice Creams (an established ice cream shop), and more. Value Guide Want to eat at Michelin-starred restaurants at ultra-low prices and experience dining halls that are usually out of reach? Then dont miss the annual two-time Los Angeles Food Festival (DineL.A.) event, which lasts about two weeks each time. During this period, hundreds of Los Angeles restaurants will offer DineL.A. menus with super-value set meals for diners to enjoy. Most participating DineL.A. restaurants charge $15-35 for lunch and $25-65 for dinner, with a small portion exceeding $65 - compared to the restaurant's regular prices, getting in is a win! You can view all participating restaurants via https://www.discoverlosangeles.com/dinela, remember to book in advance as spots are limited.
